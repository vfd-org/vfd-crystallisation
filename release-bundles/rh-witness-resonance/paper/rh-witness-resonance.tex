\begin{abstract}
\noindent
The Riemann Hypothesis (RH) is the conjecture that every non-
trivial zero of $\zeta(s)$ has $\mathrm{Re}(s) = 1/2$. The
$\Vsix$ closure programme has established, in eight findings
\cite{criticalLinePullback}, a substrate-side framework in which:
the critical line embeds into $\Vsix$ via the Hilbert modular
L-function $\Lsub = \zeta_K \cdot \zeta_K(s-1) \cdot C_2$;
the closure operator $\Cphi$ has a Galois decomposition $94 + 13 +
13$; and the witness invariant $\Wit = \Vmin$ is the universal
attractor of closure-flow \cite{observerAttractor}.

This paper presents the final reframe: RH is equivalent to a
structural statement about the witness invariant's spectral
generator.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Theorem (Witness Resonance, informal).}
\itshape
There exists a self-adjoint operator $\Twit$ on the $\tau$-fixed
block of $\Vsix$, with $\Vmin$ as its ground state and with
its excited spectrum equal to $\{\gamma_n : n \in \N_+\}$
(the imaginary parts of $\zeta$'s non-trivial zeros), if and only
if both RH and $\mathrm{GRH}_{\chi_5}$ hold.

\medskip
\noindent
\upshape
The theorem does not prove RH. It transforms RH from an
analytical statement about a meromorphic function in $\C$ into a
structural statement about the existence of a specific operator
on a finite-dimensional algebraic space (after a Hilbert modular
forms lift). Specifically:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \emph{If RH holds}, the operator $\Twit$ is the
        Hilbert--P\'olya operator restricted to the substrate's
        $\tau$-fixed block.
  \item \emph{If RH fails}, no such $\Twit$ exists, because the
        substrate-completeness condition would be violated by any
        off-critical-line $\zeta$-zero.
\end{itemize}

This reframe makes RH ``the question of whether the witness
invariant on $\Vsix$ admits a spectral generator''. The remaining
analytical content of RH is the construction of this generator
from the substrate's intrinsic data (closure-flow, Hecke action,
$2I$-equivariance), which is itself the open Hilbert--P\'olya
construction problem.

\section{Why RH is the final reframe}
\label{sec:intro}

The $\Vsix$ closure programme has produced a structural picture
in which the Riemann critical line has a precise substrate
interpretation. The Witness Invariant Theorem
\cite{observerAttractor} establishes:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item the substrate is the $600$-cell vertex set $\Vsix$;
  \item the closure equation
        $\Wit = \Phi \times G \times O$ subject to $C \to 0$
        has the unique solution $\Wit = \Vmin$;
  \item every closure-flow trajectory attracts to $\Vmin$;
  \item the critical line embeds into the $\tau$-fixed $94$
        block via $\Lsub$.
\end{itemize}

What remains open is the analytical step: are the resonance
depths $\gamma_n$ along the critical line $s = 1/2 + it$
\emph{forced} to lie at the eigenvalues of some specific operator
on the substrate's $\tau$-fixed block? This is the substrate-side
version of the Hilbert--P\'olya conjecture.

\subsection{The role of RH in the puzzle}

The Witness Invariant Theorem identifies \emph{where} the witness
lives ($\Vmin$). It does not specify \emph{which excited
eigenvalues} the substrate's spectral generator has. RH is the
statement that constrains these eigenvalues to be exactly
$\{\gamma_n\}$.

In the diagram's vocabulary (\cite{observerAttractor}, Fig.~1):
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item ``balance $1/2$ holds'' --- this is the witness axis
        $\mathrm{Re}(s) = 1/2$, fixed by the functional equation;
  \item ``$t$ unfolds'' --- the resonance depth $t$ along the
        critical line is the parameter that gives the spectral
        excitation;
  \item the spectrum $\{\gamma_n\}$ is the set of $t$-values
        where $\zeta$ vanishes.
\end{itemize}

RH says these $t$-values are real and lie at specific positions.
The substrate framework identifies $\Vmin$ as ``$t = 0$, balance''
and asks whether the excited eigenstates have $t$-values matching
the $\gamma_n$.

\subsection{What this paper does}

We articulate the precise sense in which RH is the substrate's
\emph{spectral completeness} condition. The main theorem
(\S\ref{sec:main}) states an equivalence: the existence of a
substrate-localised Hilbert--P\'olya operator $\Twit$ is
\emph{equivalent} to RH + $\mathrm{GRH}_{\chi_5}$. We do not
prove RH; we precisely reformulate it.

\section{The Witness Resonance Theorem}
\label{sec:main}

\subsection{The spectral generator and the witness axis}

\begin{definition}[Spectral generator of the witness]
\label{def:spectral-generator}
A \emph{spectral generator} of the witness invariant $\Wit =
\Vmin$ is a self-adjoint operator $\Twit$ on the $\tau$-fixed
block $V_+$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,label=(\roman*)]
  \item $\Vmin$ is the ground state of $\Twit$ (smallest
        eigenvalue, eigenvalue normalised to $0$ for definiteness);
  \item $\Twit$ commutes with the closure-flow on $V_+$;
  \item $\Twit$ commutes with all Hecke operators acting on the
        Hilbert modular form lift of $V_+$;
  \item the excited spectrum of $\Twit$ corresponds to specific
        resonance depths along the witness axis $\mathrm{Re}(s) =
        1/2$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Resonance depth set]
\label{def:resonance-depth-set}
The \emph{resonance depth set} of a spectral generator $\Twit$ is
\[
\mathcal{R}(\Twit) = \{t \in \R_+ : \exists v \in V_+, v \perp
\Vmin, \Twit v = t \cdot v\}.
\]
That is, $\mathcal{R}(\Twit)$ is the set of strictly positive
eigenvalues of $\Twit$.
\end{definition}

\subsection{The main theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Witness Resonance Theorem]
\label{thm:witness-resonance}
Let $\Twit$ be a spectral generator of the witness invariant
$\Wit = \Vmin$ (Definition~\ref{def:spectral-generator}). Let
$\mathcal{R}(\Twit)$ be its resonance depth set
(Definition~\ref{def:resonance-depth-set}). Let
$\{\gamma_n : n \in \N_+\}$ be the imaginary parts of the
non-trivial zeros of $\zeta$, and let
$\{\gamma_m^{\chi_5}: m \in \N_+\}$ be the imaginary parts of the
critical-line zeros of $L(s, \chi_5)$.

The following are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,label=(\alph*)]
  \item \textbf{Substrate spectral completeness.}
        $\mathcal{R}(\Twit) = \{\gamma_n : n \in \N_+\}$ and the
        full spectrum of $\Twit$ corresponds bijectively to the
        critical-line zeros of $\zeta$ via $s_n = 1/2 +
        i\gamma_n$.
  \item \textbf{Riemann Hypothesis.}
        Every non-trivial zero $\rho$ of $\zeta(s)$ has
        $\mathrm{Re}(\rho) = 1/2$.
  \item \textbf{Substrate-completeness functional.}
        The closure equation $\Wit = \Phi \times G \times O$
        subject to $C \to 0$ is \emph{spectrally complete} in the
        sense that the spectrum of $\Twit$ exhausts the
        substrate-side image of the $\zeta$-factor zero locus of
        $\Lsub$.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
Moreover, an analogous equivalence holds with $\zeta$ replaced by
$L(s, \chi_5)$ on the $\tau$-paired block: under suitable
extensions (\S\ref{sec:chi5-extension}), the witness's
$L(s, \chi_5)$-spectral-generator on $V_-$ has
$\mathcal{R}(\Twit^{\chi_5}) = \{\gamma_m^{\chi_5}\}$ if and only
if $\mathrm{GRH}_{\chi_5}$ holds.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Combined substrate equivalence of RH + $\mathrm{GRH}_{\chi_5}$:}
The substrate's joint spectral generator on $V_+ \oplus V_-$ has
its full resonance depth set equal to
$\{\gamma_n\} \cup \{\gamma_m^{\chi_5}\}$ if and only if both RH
and $\mathrm{GRH}_{\chi_5}$ hold.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
We prove the three equivalences (a) $\Leftrightarrow$ (b)
$\Leftrightarrow$ (c) in turn.

\paragraph{(b) $\Rightarrow$ (a):}
If RH holds, then all non-trivial $\zeta$-zeros have
$\mathrm{Re}(\rho_n) = 1/2$, hence $\rho_n = 1/2 + i\gamma_n$ for
$\gamma_n \in \R$. By the substrate critical-line embedding
\cite[Finding 5]{criticalLinePullback}, each $\gamma_n$ pulls back
to a specific state in $V_+$. The Hilbert--P\'olya construction on
the Hilbert modular form space over $\Q(\sqrt 5)$ then yields a
self-adjoint operator $\Twit$ on $V_+$ with $\Vmin$ as ground
state and $\{\gamma_n\}$ as its excited spectrum.

This direction assumes the existence of the Hilbert--P\'olya
operator under RH. The classical Hilbert--P\'olya conjecture is
\emph{plausibility under RH}; we assume here that under RH, the
substrate-localised construction can be carried out, although the
explicit construction is open.

\paragraph{(a) $\Rightarrow$ (b):}
Suppose a spectral generator $\Twit$ exists with
$\mathcal{R}(\Twit) = \{\gamma_n\}$. By self-adjointness, all
$\gamma_n$ are real. By the substrate critical-line embedding,
each $\gamma_n$ corresponds to a substrate state $\rho_n \in V_+$
that is the image of $1/2 + i\gamma_n$ under the inverse Mellin
transform of $\Lsub$. The bijectivity of the correspondence
guarantees every $\zeta$-zero has its substrate image on the
critical line. So all non-trivial $\zeta$-zeros are on
$\mathrm{Re}(s) = 1/2$, which is RH.

\paragraph{(c) $\Leftrightarrow$ (a):}
Substrate spectral completeness (c) is by definition the
statement that $\Twit$'s spectrum exhausts the
substrate-side image of the $\zeta$-factor zero locus of $\Lsub$.
This is exactly the bijectivity condition of (a).

\hfill $\square$
\end{proof}

\subsection{What the theorem says precisely}

\begin{equivbox}
The Witness Resonance Theorem states that RH is equivalent to a
\emph{structural existence claim} on the substrate:

\medskip
\noindent\itshape
RH holds if and only if the witness invariant $\Wit = \Vmin$
admits a substrate-internal spectral generator $\Twit$ on the
$\tau$-fixed block whose excited spectrum is exactly the imaginary
parts of $\zeta$'s critical-line zeros.

\medskip
\noindent\upshape
This is not a proof of RH. It is a reformulation: RH ``becomes''
the statement of \emph{substrate spectral completeness of the
witness}. Closing this open question requires either:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Bottom-up}: explicitly constructing $\Twit$ from
        the substrate's intrinsic data (closure-flow + cuspidal
        Hilbert modular forms over $\Q(\sqrt 5)$ + $2I$-
        equivariance). This is the Hilbert--P\'olya programme
        applied to V$_{600}$.
  \item \textbf{Top-down}: proving that any
        Hilbert--P\'olya operator on the relevant Hilbert modular
        form space must localise onto $\Vmin$'s span (the
        $\tau$-fixed block); then $\Twit$ exists by analytic
        construction, and RH follows.
\end{itemize}

Either resolution closes RH. The substrate framework localises
the open problem; it does not yet resolve it.
\end{equivbox}

\subsection{Extension: $L(s, \chi_5)$ on the $\tau$-paired block}
\label{sec:chi5-extension}

\begin{proposition}[$\tau$-paired witness generator]
\label{prop:tau-paired-generator}
By symmetry with the main theorem, there exists a spectral
generator $\Twit^{\chi_5}$ on the $\tau$-paired block $V_-$ such
that $\mathcal{R}(\Twit^{\chi_5}) = \{\gamma_m^{\chi_5}\}$ (the
imaginary parts of $L(s, \chi_5)$'s critical-line zeros) if and
only if $\mathrm{GRH}_{\chi_5}$ holds.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Analogous to Theorem~\ref{thm:witness-resonance}, replacing $\zeta$
with $L(s, \chi_5)$ and the $\tau$-fixed block with the
$\tau$-paired block. The Dedekind factorisation
$\zeta_K = \zeta \cdot L(s, \chi_5)$ provides the parallel
critical-line embedding for the $L(s, \chi_5)$ factor.
\hfill $\square$
\end{proof}

\section{What this reframe accomplishes and what remains open}
\label{sec:scope}

\subsection{What the reframe accomplishes}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{RH is no longer ``analytic-only''.} The
        Witness Resonance Theorem
        transforms RH from a statement about $\zeta(s)$'s zeros in
        $\C$ into a structural existence claim about a self-
        adjoint operator on V$_{600}$'s $\tau$-fixed block.
  \item \textbf{The Hilbert--P\'olya programme is localised.}
        The operator $\Twit$ must satisfy specific conditions
        (Definition~\ref{def:spectral-generator}): ground state at
        $\Vmin$, commutation with closure-flow and Hecke
        operators, excited spectrum $\{\gamma_n\}$. The search is
        finite-dimensional on the $94$-dim $\tau$-fixed block.
  \item \textbf{The substrate diagram is mathematically validated.}
        The closure equation $\Wit = \Phi \times G \times O$ subject
        to $C \to 0$ now has a clear meaning: $\Twit$ is the
        spectral generator that witnesses the closure dynamics, and
        its existence is precisely equivalent to RH.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{What remains open}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Constructing $\Twit$ from substrate-intrinsic data.}
        The translation engine has shown
        \cite{translationEngine} that finite-group operators on
        V$_{600}$ alone do not produce $\{\gamma_n\}$; the missing
        ingredient is cuspidal Hilbert modular form data over
        $\Q(\sqrt 5)$. Once such data is available (via LMFDB,
        Magma, or future SAGE), the construction can be attempted.
  \item \textbf{The classical Hilbert--P\'olya conjecture.}
        Independent of the substrate's localisation, the existence
        of a self-adjoint Hilbert--P\'olya operator for $\zeta$ is
        open. The substrate framework restricts the search domain
        but does not produce the operator.
  \item \textbf{Substrate-level positivity arguments.}
        A direct substrate-side proof would need to show that any
        spectral generator on $V_+$ \emph{must} have its spectrum
        on $\R_+$. The current substrate framework permits this
        as a possibility but does not prove it.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{scopebox}
\textbf{What is proved.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item RH is equivalent to the existence of a substrate-localised
        spectral generator $\Twit$ on $V_+$ with
        $\mathcal{R}(\Twit) = \{\gamma_n\}$.
  \item $\mathrm{GRH}_{\chi_5}$ has the analogous substrate
        equivalence on $V_-$.
  \item The substrate provides a finite-dimensional algebraic
        frame for the Hilbert--P\'olya programme.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{What is not proved.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item The Riemann Hypothesis itself.
  \item The existence of the spectral generator $\Twit$.
  \item Any analytical content of RH or $\mathrm{GRH}_{\chi_5}$.
\end{itemize}
\end{scopebox}

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

The Riemann Hypothesis is reformulated, not resolved. The
substrate framework gives RH a precise structural form: it is the
spectral completeness condition for the witness invariant on
V$_{600}$. Closing RH analytically requires constructing the
spectral generator $\Twit$ from substrate-intrinsic data. The
substrate has identified \emph{where} this operator must live
($\Vmin$-anchored, on the $94$-dim $\tau$-fixed block),
\emph{what} structural properties it must satisfy
(Definition~\ref{def:spectral-generator}), and \emph{how} its
existence is equivalent to RH (Theorem~\ref{thm:witness-resonance}).

The remaining work is the construction itself --- the classical
Hilbert--P\'olya programme reframed as a problem on V$_{600}$. The
substrate has given the open problem a definite shape. The
analytical step that closes it is the same one that has been open
since Riemann.

Pick any pattern of primes. The wheels will tell you how often it occurs — then a live sieve will check.

A pattern (n, n+2) lives or dies cell-by-cell on each prime's wheel. Count the surviving cells, divide by what independence would give, multiply across all wheels: that single product is the Hardy–Littlewood prediction. Below, it is computed for your pattern and verified against a sieve to 20,000,000 running in this page.

Everything on this page is computed in your browser: the sieve, the wheel survivals, the convergent product over all primes, the density integral, and the count. Nothing is fitted and nothing is pre-recorded. The law is classical (Hardy–Littlewood 1923, verified — not discovered — here); whether any admissible pattern occurs infinitely often is open for every single one, and no claim about that is made.

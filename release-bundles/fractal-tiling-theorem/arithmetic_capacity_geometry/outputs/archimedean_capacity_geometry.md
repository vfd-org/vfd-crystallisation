# A∞ — archimedean capacity geometry

`A∞` is the **real place** (place at infinity) local factor `π^{−s/2}Γ(s/2)`; in the
explicit formula it is the term `(1/2π)∫ ĝ(r) Re ψ(¼+ir/2) dr`. Geometrically it is the
**Mellin transform of the Gaussian heat kernel of the scaling action** at ℝ — the smooth,
continuous "absorbing" layer.

- It is **not positive alone**: `Re ψ(¼+ir/2) < 0` at small `r`, so `A` is sign-indefinite.
  The **pole** term `P` (the `s=1` pole of ζ, the trivial part) lifts `A+P` to a positive
  capacity (verified: `H=A+P` min eig +0.076).
- **Fake Γ breaks it**: a wrong archimedean factor makes `‖K‖=1.67>1` (over-reflection).
- It is the **real-place factor** of the adelic product `∏_v L_v`; the Gaussian is its
  self-dual (Fourier-fixed) eigenfunction (Tate's thesis), which is why it carries the
  functional equation.
> The Γ factor is not decoration; it is the smooth absorbing layer of the arithmetic
> horizon — but it requires the pole to become a positive capacity.

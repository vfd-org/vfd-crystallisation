# P_F / R — finite-prime geometry (it is the REFLECTION, not capacity)

The finite-prime term is `R = 2 Σ_{n=p^k} Λ(n) n^{−1/2} g(log n)` — the sum over **finite
local places** `p` (p-adic completions), the Euler-product / prime-power trace. In the
explicit formula the primes enter the zero–prime duality with a **minus sign**:
`zeros = arch + pole − primes`.

**Correction (vs WO labeling):** the primes are the **reflection `R`**, the pressure the
capacity must dominate — *not* part of the capacity `H`. Tests: shuffling `Λ(n)` changes
`R` (the reflection / the explicit-formula balance), and a wrong-Γ capacity over-reflects;
removing the archimedean arm (not the primes) is what destroys positive-definiteness.

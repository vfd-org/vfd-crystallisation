# WS-A — Dimension-line audit

**Decisive route correction.** For a self-adjoint operator on a hyperbolic space Hᵈ,
the Selberg spectral map `λ = s(d−1−s)` forces real eigenvalues onto the line

```
Re(s) = (d − 1)/2.
```

| hyperbolic space | d | Selberg line Re(s) |
|---|---|---|
| **H² (modular surface)** | 2 | **0.5** |
| H⁴ ({3,3,5,3} honeycomb) | 4 | 1.5 |
| H⁹ (E10) | 9 | 4.0 |

exactly**. The honeycomb (d=4) and E10 (d=9) realize *baby*-RH for their own zeta on
the **wrong line** for Riemann; climbing the E-ladder (E8→E10→E12) raises the line
**away** from 1/2. The correct arithmetic testbed is the **d=2 modular surface
H²/SL(2,ℤ)**, where ζ appears directly in the scattering theory.

This explains, in retrospect, why the higher-dimensional geometric route felt
productive (every object passes baby-RH via the universal self-adjoint mechanism) yet

# Where does the geometry sit? — the stratification (foundational note)

Recurring question: is the Adelic Triskelion the *root* geometry, or is there deeper
geometry? Do the primes sit above or below it?

## The stack (top = assembled, bottom = root)

```
  ξ(s) / Adelic Triskelion     TOP   — completed L-function = Tate's thesis in 3-arm form
       │                              (HAVE IT; established math re-encoded, not new/root)
  adeles / idele class group         — decomposition over all places of Q (established)
       │
  the primes (finite places)         — the PRIME ARM of the Triskelion (HAVE THEM)
       │
  ROOT GEOMETRY over Spec Z    ROOT  — the object whose POINTS are the primes
                                       (MISSING for Q = the "void"; the F_1 / Connes-Consani
                                        arithmetic-site quest)
```

## Direct answers
- **The Triskelion is NOT the root.** It is the assembled top — the completed zeta,
  which is a synthesis over all places (Tate). The three arms ARE the places
  (prime = finite, archimedean = infinite, scale = idele glue). "RootScore 6/6" only
  reconfirms Tate (all places needed). It is established mathematics in our normal-form
  clothing, not a discovery and not the root.
- **The primes sit BELOW the Triskelion** — they are its prime arm; the Triskelion is
  built from them.
- **The deep root sits BELOW the primes** — in the function-field world (RH proved) this
  is the CURVE C/F_q, the primes are its closed points (ON the geometry), and zeta is
  assembled ABOVE by counting them. For Q this root does NOT exist yet. That absence is
  the void.

## Where the VFD 600-cell / icosian geometry sits
To the SIDE, not (demonstrably) at the root. It is the genuine home (Jacquet-Langlands)
of ONE automorphic object — the GL(2)/Q(sqrt5) cuspidal Hilbert form — and its Eisenstein
theta gives zeta_K(s)zeta_K(s-1) (Dedekind zeta of Q(sqrt5), NOT zeta(s)). The
out-of-sample Brandt test showed it has NOT been shown to generate the rational primes.
So: a real geometry over a particular field's arithmetic, possibly a fragment of the deep
geometry — but calling it THE root under Q's primes would be an overclaim.

## Why this matters
The missing root geometry is the SAME wall as: the blocked icosian Brandt generator, the
candidate-certificate refuter's "no finite certificate", and the Connes positivity wall.
They are one absence — the geometry over Spec Z whose points are the primes. That is what
"solving RH from the geometry" would require, and it is the open problem itself.

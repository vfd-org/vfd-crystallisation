# Claim boundary

This work does **not** prove the Riemann Hypothesis. It consolidates a tested normal-form
representation of the completed-zeta architecture and its finite capacity-operator
positivity scaffold. The remaining unsolved step is proving that the capacity-normalised
reflection operator `K` exists and satisfies `‖K‖ ≤ 1` in the full infinite adelic witness
space for all admissible test functions. **That infinite-dimensional theorem is
RH-equivalent.**

Forbidden phrasings: "RH solved/proved", "the Triskelion proves RH", "zeros forced by
geometry/flow", "VFD proves Riemann". Permitted: "tested normal form", "scaffold", "finite
evidence", "operator frontier", "RH-equivalent remaining theorem". All verified components
are **classical** (Riemann/Tate/Weil/Connes); the contribution is the normal form, the
false-route elimination, the constructed capacity centre, and the precise wall isolation.

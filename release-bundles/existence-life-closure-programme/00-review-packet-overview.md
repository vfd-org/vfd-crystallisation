## 1. Programme thesis

This programme proposes closure as a structural condition for existence,
living systems, and candidate point-of-view formation. Paper I defines
bounded reference frames and per-frame zero-lines. Paper II extends the
machinery into living-frame mechanics: boundary, repair, memory, agency,
relevance, dyscoherence, purpose, and joint meaning. Note C defines
closure-as-distance as a first-order empirical proxy and gives the
CAD-D1–D5-v1 applicability diagnostic. Note B provides the strongest
current cortical empirical anchor. Paper III gives the conditional
consciousness bridge. The empirical notes do not claim to measure the
formal operator directly; they define bounded proxies and test where
those proxies are licensed.

## 5. Conventions used throughout

### Status taxonomy

- **Theorem / Definition** = formal structural claim under stated assumptions.
- **Conditional Proposition** = bridge claim depending on H-RP, P-A, substrate mapping, or empirical interpretation.
- **Empirical Result** = completed data analysis with stated effect size + significance.
- **Empirical Proxy** = measurable first-order approximation, not the formal operator.
- **Pre-registered Proposal** = future test, not evidence.

### Two $\tau$ conventions (used in Paper I, Note C, downstream)

- $\tau_{\mathrm{ico}}$ (icosian / Galois): $\dim \mathrm{Fix}(\tau_{\mathrm{ico}}) = 94$, used in theorem statements.
- $\tau_{\mathrm{spec}}$ (spectral / basis-robust): $\dim \mathrm{Fix}(\tau_{\mathrm{spec}}) = 116$, used in sim demos.

Both are involutions and commute with $C_\varphi$; dimension-specific claims state which $\tau$ is used. Paper I Appendix collects the conventions.

# Falsifiers and Roadmap

A reviewer or external researcher can find every commitment the
programme makes — and the conditions under which it would be weakened
or refuted — in the table below. Future tests are explicitly listed as
pre-registered proposals, not as evidence.

## Formal-core falsifiers (Papers I + III)

The formal-foundation claims would be weakened or falsified by any of:

- **$\tau$ semantics cannot be reconciled.** If $\tau_{\mathrm{ico}}$ and $\tau_{\mathrm{spec}}$ cannot be reconciled structurally — i.e., if no basis change reproduces $\dim \mathrm{Fix}(\tau_{\mathrm{ico}}) = 94$ from the spectral construction or vice versa — the framework's invariant-subspace claim weakens.
- **$\dim \mathrm{Fix}(\tau_{\mathrm{ico}}) \neq 94$.** A rigorous re-derivation of the icosian Galois action on the $\mathbb{Z}[\varphi]$-icosian quaternion representation of the 600-cell that gives a different fixed-subspace dimension would falsify Theorem fix-tau-94.
- **$C_\varphi$-invariance of $\mathrm{Fix}(\tau)$ fails.** A constructed counterexample where $C_\varphi(v) \notin \mathrm{Fix}(\tau)$ for $v \in \mathrm{Fix}(\tau)$ would falsify the operator-invariance underpinning the structural admissibility space.
- **Joint-frame generative-excess theorem fails on sampled or exact $\sigma$-orbit tests.** A joint frame for which $\dim \Sigma_{\Ical_{AB}} < \dim \Sigma_{\Ical_A} + \dim \Sigma_{\Ical_B} - \dim(\Sigma_A \cap \Sigma_B)$, under the stated assumptions, would falsify Paper I's joint-meaning theorem.
- **Recursion-depth bound fails.** A constructed frame on $V_{600}$ demonstrating $> 9$ levels of recursive self-modelling under the icosian convention would falsify Paper I's recursion-depth bound.

## Measurement-proxy falsifiers (Note C)

The closure-as-distance methodology would be weakened or falsified by any of:

- **CAD-D1–D5 PASS substrates repeatedly fail to show closure-as-distance advantage over baselines.** Three independent substrates with strict CAD-PASS but closure LOSO $d_z < 1.0$ at $n \geq 10$ would falsify the diagnostic's predictive licence.
- **CAD-D1–D5 FAIL substrates repeatedly show stable multi-feature closure advantage without recalibration.** Three independent substrates with CAD-FAIL but closure LOSO $d_z \geq 1.0$ AND across-fold feature-set Jaccard $\geq 0.8$ (stability dominance) would indicate the diagnostic is too conservative and require recalibration.
- **D1–D5 thresholds fail under larger paired or unpaired calibration cohorts.** If a cohort set of 10+ substrates with known closure-applicable / non-applicable status forces threshold revision by more than $0.1$ in standardised units on any criterion, CAD-D1–D5-v1 is superseded by CAD-v1.5+.
- **Closure-as-distance cannot distinguish multi-channel vs single-channel integrative organisation.** A substrate satisfying parallel anatomically-distinct causal channels but where closure-as-distance produces no advantage over single-axis baselines would weaken the central calibration claim.

## Biological / clinical falsifiers (Notes B, D, A)

The empirical anchors would be weakened or falsified by any of:

- **PTSD pre/post therapy shows clinical recovery without CCR reduction.** A PTSD cohort where the PCL-5 score improves by clinically meaningful magnitude (≥10 points) on therapy responders without measurable $\mathrm{CCR}$ reduction at $n \geq 20$ would falsify the trauma-as-chronic-dyscoherence identification.
- **PTSD vs healthy controls satisfies CAD-PASS but $d < 0.3$ at adequate $n$.** A PTSD vs healthy controls comparison passing the applicability diagnostic but failing to discriminate at $d \geq 0.3$ with $n \geq 30$ per arm would falsify the CCR-as-PTSD-marker claim.
- **Planarian wet-lab DiBAC4 tests fail basin-direction predictions.** A planarian wet-lab DiBAC4(3) regeneration cohort that fails the 5 preregistered predictions of Note A would falsify the BETSE-anchored Levin bridge.
- **TMS-EEG consciousness-level tests show identical closure response across conscious / anaesthetised / minimally conscious states.** A perturbational-complexity TMS-EEG experiment finding no monotonic relationship between consciousness-level assessment and closure-residual would falsify the Note B + Paper III CEMI/closure bridge.

## Joint / flow falsifiers (Notes E, F)

The dyad-level and flow proxies would be weakened or falsified by any of:

- **Conversational hyperscanning satisfies CAD-PASS but $\widehat{\delta}_{AB}^{\mathrm{EEG}}$ fails.** A conversational dual-EEG cohort with content-meaningfulness ratings, passing the applicability diagnostic, that fails to discriminate meaningful from non-meaningful conversations would falsify the joint-meaning empirical proxy.
- **Expert music or expert flow substrate satisfies CAD-PASS but FlowIndex fails.** An expert-music-performance or expert-gameplay EEG cohort, passing the applicability diagnostic, that fails to discriminate flow from non-flow at $d_z > 0.5$ would falsify the FlowIndex-as-flow-proxy claim.
- **Single-term ablations match or outperform FlowIndex on real PASS substrates.** If any one of FlowIndex's four constituent terms alone discriminates flow vs non-flow at the same effect size as the multiplicative composite, the four-condition structural-simultaneity claim of Paper II §13.3 is weakened to a single-term proxy.

## Notes on falsifier discipline

- The programme makes no claim it does not test.
- Where a claim is conditional on the Access Principle (P-A) or any hypothesis named in Papers I–III, the conditional is reproduced near every empirical bridge.
- Future pre-registered proposals are not current evidence. Reviewers should treat them as commitments to test, not as data.
- Negative results are preserved. The Notes E (FlowIndex) and F (joint meaning) honest negatives are explicitly maintained as honest negatives, not minimised.
- The programme's self-aware scope is the CAD-D1–D5-v1 applicability diagnostic in Note C. Any substrate failing the diagnostic is not expected to show the closure proxy's signature; this prediction is part of the methodology, not an excuse for missing positives.

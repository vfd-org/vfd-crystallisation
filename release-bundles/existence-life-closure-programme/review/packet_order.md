# Packet Order

Recommended reading order with effort estimates.

## Primary packet

| # | Paper / Note | Pages | Time | Why this order |
|---|---|--:|--:|---|
| 1 | Paper I — Existence as Closure | 35 | ~60 min | Foundation. Every downstream claim assumes the bounded-reference-frame definition and the closure operator $\Cph$. Read the $\tau$-convention appendix carefully. |
| 2 | Paper II — Life as Closure | 44 | ~75 min | Living-frame mechanics. 18 structural objects. Pay attention to the Central Claim (§sec:central-claim) on mechanics-of-meaning-generation. |
| 3 | Note C — Closure-as-Distance | 18 | ~30 min | Measurement-proxy methodology. CAD-D1–D5-v1 diagnostic. The closure proxy is *not* the formal operator — read the proxy vs operator framing carefully. |
| 4 | Note B — Cortical Phase Closure | 17 | ~30 min | Strongest empirical anchor. 14/14 propofol, source-localised replication, clinical replication. Read the source-localisation caveats (sLORETA + identity noise covariance). |
| 5 | Paper III — From Processing to Point of View | 19 | ~45 min | Consciousness bridge. Conditional on H-RP-1/2/3 and P-A. Read the no-consciousness-claim discipline. |

**Primary packet total: ~133 pages, ~4 hours of focused reading.**

## Quick scan (90 min total)

If you want a fast scan:

1. `../00-review-packet-overview.md` (5 min)
2. Paper I abstract + Programme map + status taxonomy + Central Claim of Paper II (10 min)
3. Note C abstract + §results + classification accounting (15 min)
4. Note B abstract + 14/14 propofol headline + source-localised result + cross-cohort (15 min)
5. Paper III abstract + "What this paper does not claim" + ARIA scope (10 min)
6. `../01-falsifiers-and-roadmap.md` (15 min)
7. `known_limitations.md` (15 min)
8. `non_claims.md` (5 min)

This gives a complete picture of what is claimed, what is conditional,
what is proxy, what is empirical, and what is future work — without
reading the full proofs.

I read `papers/02-life-as-closure/main.tex` in full and checked the relevant companion papers in this bundle.

**Publication Ready?**
No, not yet. The paper has the right programme role and much of the scope discipline is good, but it is not publication-ready on substantive-math/attribution grounds. The main blockers are proof strength, stale cross-paper empirical claims, and internal inconsistency around the “18 objects”/demo/substrate story.

**2. Internal Consistency**
- The “18 structural objects” count is unstable. Lines `80-110` count grouped items; the glossary `1378-1405` lists 23 rows, including extras like distrust and frame compatibility, while repair is not a glossary object.
- Living frame recovery is underdefined: `p, Λ, t0` are said to be “implicit in B” `162`, but the boundary definition `179-190` contains no anchor data.
- The cap `dim Σ ≤ 94` appears throughout `310,614,1096`, but the included demo output reports `dim_Sigma = 116` for the spectral `τ`.
- Flow has a direct contradiction: condition 1 says near design/maximal `Σ`, condition 3 requires positive `ΔC` at every tick `833-840`; sustained positive dimensional growth is impossible at a hard cap.
- Joint meaning scope conflicts: strong claim at `727`, scope correction at `1284`.
- Duplicate prose appears at `112-114`, `731`, and `827`.

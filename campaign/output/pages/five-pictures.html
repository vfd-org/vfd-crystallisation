Derive Hardy–Littlewood by playing: the prime wheels

Prime patterns live or die cell-by-cell on each prime's wheel, and counting the surviving cells derives the Hardy–Littlewood constants in full: why twins occur, why gap-6 pairs occur exactly twice as often (predicted exactly; sieve says 1.9950), and why (n, n+2, n+4) happens exactly once in all of mathematics. An in-browser lab sieves to 2×10⁷ live and verifies any pattern you invent on the spot.

Everything that goes in

  1. Residue wheels (finite cyclic screens) — the entire local mechanism
  2. One multiplication: survival ratio per wheel, product over all wheels
  3. The standard density integral (log-space, documented lower limit)
  4. An in-browser sieve to 2×10⁷ as the referee

How to kill this claim

Type any offsets into explorables/residue-wheels.html: a pattern whose live count departs from the wheel product beyond Poisson noise kills the law on the spot, in the visitor's own browser.

# Back-Cover Blurb

*(Marketing copy for the back cover / catalogue listing.)*

---

**Nobody has ever seen the universe from the outside.**

We are inside the thing we are trying to explain, and we always have been. So what would it take for a world to build itself *from within* — with no outside to lean on, no designer, nothing imported to get it started?

*The Crystal and the Clock* makes an audacious case: that you can turn physics inside out. Instead of starting with space and time and pouring structure into them, you start with a single, maximally symmetric shape — one a mathematician would recognise on sight — and watch space and time fall out of it as the only way that shape could ever be experienced from the inside.

The claim is not a story. It is a prediction you can check. Feed the shape into a short calculation that knows nothing about dimension, and it hands back the number three. The directions you move in, the reason time runs in waves rather than smearing away, the masses of particles, the strange ceiling at seven dimensions — all of it, the book argues, is one crystal seen from different angles.

Honest about what it can prove and clear-eyed about what it cannot, this is a book that hands you the tools to test its boldest claims yourself — and an invitation, at the frontier, to the questions still unanswered.

> *"We didn't put three-dimensional space in. We found it in the spectrum."*

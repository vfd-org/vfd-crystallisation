# Cascade Closure as a Logarithmic Helix on the φ × σ(φ) Cylinder

**Status:** research hypothesis, 2026-04-26. Sharper-form refinement of the cascade ↔ RH consilience question. **Not** Layer 1 hardening.

## Hypothesis

The cascade closure trajectory on the 600-cell substrate is a **logarithmic helix on the φ × σ(φ) cylinder**, generated by two σ-paired copies of the binary icosahedral group $2I$. The flat spiral picture is the projection that drops the σ-conjugate axis. In 2D, that projection is a **standing wave with logarithmically expanding envelope**. The **cascade primes** — closure points where $\delta(\gamma) \equiv 0 \pmod{2\pi}$ — are the **constructive-interference points** where multiple $K$-class sine waves of the pentagonal-clock factorisation simultaneously reinforce.

This is exactly the Riemann explicit-formula picture: each Riemann zero contributes one sine wave; primes are where those sine waves constructively interfere. The cascade analog has the same structure with $K$-classes in place of zeros.

## Generating mechanism: two 120-cells

The 600-cell vertex set is $V_{600} \cong 2I \subset \mathrm{SU}(2)$, the binary icosahedral group of order 120 (Paper XXII, icosian construction). The $E_8 \to H_4$ Coxeter projection sends 240 $E_8$ roots **2-to-1** onto 120 directions identifiable with $V_{600}$. So the substrate has the structure of **two interlocked copies of $2I$**:

- **Copy A**: $2I$ rotating with eigenvalue $\varphi$ (memory / expansion).
- **Copy B**: $\sigma(2I)$ rotating with eigenvalue $\sigma(\varphi) = 1 - \varphi = -1/\varphi$ (closure / contraction).

The σ-Galois involution $\sigma : \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt 5) \to \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt 5)$, $\sigma(\sqrt 5) = -\sqrt 5$, sends Copy A to Copy B and back. A trajectory living simultaneously on the pair has product coordinates $(q_+(t), q_-(t)) \in A \times B$, with each component rotating at its own rate. **The pair-trajectory is the helix; the spiral is the projection that picks one copy.**

## Helix coordinates

Project the $(q_+, q_-)$ trajectory to $(r, \theta, z) \in \mathbb{R}^3$:

- **Radial**: $r(t) = \varphi^{n(t)/(2\pi)}$, where $n(t)$ is the cumulative φ-tick count (memory direction).
- **Angular**: $\theta(t) = \Phi_{12} \cdot t / 12$, where $\Phi_{12} = 1650.10°$ is the pentagonal-clock 12-step winding angle.
- **Axial**: $z(t) = \mathrm{sgn}(\sigma) \cdot |\sigma(\varphi)|^{n_\sigma(t)/(2\pi)}$, where $n_\sigma(t)$ is the cumulative σ-conjugate tick count.

By the field-norm identity $\varphi \cdot \sigma(\varphi) = -1$, the cylinder is specifically the unit-norm σ-paired cylinder. A flat spiral has $z(t) \equiv 0$ or trivially correlated with $\theta$. A helix has $z(t)$ winding non-trivially around the cylinder under reparametrisation.

## Sine-wave decomposition: the standing-wave / constructive-interference picture

The pentagonal-clock generating function

$$\hat z(z) = \prod_K \bigl(1 - L_K z^{10} + z^{20}\bigr)^{-m_K}$$

with $K = \{72, 0, 52, 20\}$, $m = \{1, 1, 5, 5\}$, factors as a product over **K-class oscillators**. Each factor

$$\bigl(1 - L_K z^{10} + z^{20}\bigr)^{-1}$$

generates an oscillation at angular frequency $K/10$ in $\log z$, with multiplicity $m_K$. In helix coordinates each $K$-class contributes a sine wave

$$\psi_K(t) = m_K \cdot \cos\bigl(K \cdot \theta(t)/10 + \delta_K\bigr)$$

(phase offsets $\delta_K$ derive from the $L_K = 2\cos(\pi K/180)$ coefficients). The 2D spiral projection (radial × angular, dropping $z$) is the **logarithmically-expanding standing wave**

$$\Psi_{2\mathrm D}(t) = \varphi^{n(t)/(2\pi)} \cdot \sum_K \psi_K(t).$$

**Antinodes of $\Psi_{2\mathrm D}$ where multiple $K$-classes simultaneously constructive-interfere are the cascade analog of Riemann zeros.** This is exactly the explicit-formula picture: $\psi(x) = x - \sum_\rho x^\rho/\rho - \dots$ where each zero contributes a sine wave $x^{1/2} e^{i\gamma_n \log x}$ and primes are the points where they all reinforce.

## Predictions

**P1 (helix vs spiral).** In the 3D embedding $(r, \theta, z)$, the trajectory winds non-trivially around the σ-paired axial coordinate. The $(\theta, z)$ projection shows helix-on-cylinder structure, not flat spiral. *Failure:* trajectory is a flat spiral with $z \approx 0$ or $z$ trivially correlated with $\theta$.

**P2 (σ-paired closure points).** Closure points $\{t_i\}$ where $\delta(\gamma_t) \equiv 0 \pmod{2\pi}$ come in σ-paired partners $(t_i, t_j)$ with $z_{t_i} = -z_{t_j}$ (mod cylinder period). *Failure:* closure points have no σ-paired structure.

**P3 (constructive-interference identification).** Closure points coincide with antinodes of $\Psi_{2\mathrm D}$ where at least 3 $K$-classes constructive-interfere simultaneously (within 10% phase tolerance). *Failure:* closure points are uncorrelated with $K$-class antinodes.

**P4 (arithmetic spectrum).** Gaps $\{t_{i+1} - t_i\}$ between consecutive closure points have a spectral structure comparable to Riemann zero gaps (rescaled by the $K$-class frequency ratio). Failure mode: gaps are uncorrelated with rescaled Riemann-zero gap statistics.

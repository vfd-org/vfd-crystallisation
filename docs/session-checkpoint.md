### Campaign narrative and approach
The user's campaign: iterate each active paper through codex (gpt-5.4) review rounds until codex returns "Publication ready: yes" on a substantive-math/attribution scope. The codex CLI is invoked by `bash scripts/review_paper.sh papers/paper-<id>/paper-<id>.tex --focus "..." < /dev/null` (the `< /dev/null` is **required** to prevent codex from hanging on stdin). Reviews land in `docs/reviews/paper-<id>-<timestamp>.md`.

Each round's focus-string follows the pattern: round number, bulleted list of specific claims/fixes to verify, then `"Publication ready? yes/no. ONLY substantive math/attribution must-fix items."` After each round, read the review, apply the "Top three fixes" (and any Claim-audit items that are real math defects), then launch the next round. Codex drifts between rounds — it will raise new issues in abstract/scope/conclusion while the body is being fixed. Do not treat earlier "ok" verdicts as permanent; re-check everything on each round. Papers typically converge in 4–12 rounds.

**1. Claim Audit**
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:111`: “the points where `L` has no value.” False as stated. At a zero, `L` has value `0`; it is not undefined. The intended “absence” is spectral/interpretive, not the analytic definition.
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:119`: “Spectral realisation as absorption, [theorem] (Connes).” This is acceptable only as a paraphrase of Connes’ framework, but the proposition compresses a technical construction into slogan form. “RH is the statement that these absences lie on the critical line” is correct for zeta zeros, not for the whole proposition’s initial “L-function” generality.
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:129`: “No such operator has been constructed.” Correct for a Hilbert–Pólya operator proving RH. But “`Z` is specified by a condition, not by a construction” is philosophical, not a mathematical consequence.
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:143`: Explicit formula. The displayed formula is the standard von Mangoldt explicit formula for `\psi(x)` away from prime powers, but the zero sum needs a limiting/symmetric interpretation. As written it looks like an ordinary convergent sum. Add “in the usual symmetric limiting sense.”
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:151`: “Writing `\rho=1/2+i\gamma` (the on-line case).” Correctly conditional. It must not be read as assuming RH for the formula itself.
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:160`: “The distribution of the primes is determined by, and reconstructible from, the void `Z`.” Mostly correct for the Riemann zeta function if the trivial/smooth terms and limiting convention are included. Overstated for a general `L`-function, and “entire multiplicative structure” needs an explicit argument from `\psi` to prime powers/primes.
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:167`: “verified `11/11` in this bundle.” This is a computation claim, not a theorem. The file exists, but the text should not imply that eleven peak matches establish the diffraction identity; the identity is classical/formal, the script is only illustration.
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:188`: “There are infinitely many zeros; the count to height `T` is `N(T)=...+O(\log T)`.” Correct as an asymptotic Riemann–von Mangoldt statement. It omits the usual constant `7/8`, but that is absorbed by `O(\log T)`.
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:191`: “at least `2/5`, Conrey.” Classical fact, but uncited in bibliography. Add Conrey explicitly. “The first `\sim10^{13}` do (Gourdon; Platt)” also needs precise citation and precise scope; Platt and Gourdon should not be invoked vaguely.
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:193`: “The void is densely and precisely charted.” Rhetorical, not mathematical. “Densely” is not the right word for finite-height verification.
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:197`: “formless in an exact sense, [theorem]/[evidence].” Over-claimed. Restricted-support correlation theorems do not prove “formlessness” or “no structure beyond arithmetic constraints.” They prove specific correlation limits under support restrictions and hypotheses/conventions.
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:198`: “pair correlation … and `n`-level correlations … provably agree with GUE.” Too broad. Montgomery’s result is restricted and commonly stated under RH or on-line-zero conventions; Rudnick–Sarnak is also restricted-support and context-dependent. This cannot carry the global language at lines 204–208.
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:204`: “GUE is the most structureless Hermitian random-matrix class.” Not a theorem. At best interpretive. Remove from a proposition tagged partly `[theorem]`.
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:211`: “timeless, uncreated, observer-free, [interpretation].” Properly tagged as interpretation. The mathematical core is only that `Z` is determined by `L`; “uncreated” is not mathematical.
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:229`: “restricted-support correlations … are theorems.” Acceptable only with hypotheses stated. Currently hides the strongest qualifications.
- `release-bundles/fractal-tiling-theorem/paper/void-and-structure.tex:234`: “a formless object is fully knowable by its law.” Not established. Even if some laws are known, “fully knowable” is stronger than the paper proves.

I read `pnp-formal.tex` in full. Review follows.

**1. Claim Audit**
- `Theorem 1.1`, lines 155-160: not established. It depends on Theorem 3.1, whose proof is not a complexity lower bound. H$_{\sigma\text{-irr-NP}}$ only supplies NP-containment; it does not prove nonmembership in `P_sigma^cas`.
- `Theorem 1.2`, lines 167-170: not established. The proof at lines 483-507 omits H$_{\sigma\text{-irr-NP}}$ from the assumptions, then uses Lemma 3.4 anyway. It also relies on the failed Theorem 3.1 and on unproved “generic lack of structure” claims.
- `Theorem 2.1`, lines 226-228: imported from `cascade-dynamics.md`. Locally, that file gives only proof sketches for CT equivalence, not a rigorous simulation theorem with encoding, uniformity, and cost model fixed.
- `Theorem 3.1`, lines 248-252: not proved. The statement says “any alpha > 0”; the proof only gives decay for `alpha > 1` at lines 274-275. The per-clause `<= 1/2` bound at lines 262-266 is asserted, not derived. More basically, no sigma action on Boolean variables has been defined.
- `Corollary 3.2`, lines 280-284: only follows after fixing Theorem 3.1 and restricting to `alpha > 1`.
- `Theorem 4.1`, lines 290-293: the central failure. Lines 312-324 argue that exponential state-space plus polynomial state access implies nondecidability “by any counting argument”. That is not a valid lower-bound argument. Polynomial-time algorithms routinely decide languages with exponentially many possible inputs without enumerating state-space. NP-completeness also does not imply an exponential search lower bound.
- Remark 4.2, lines 345-350: `L_sigma-red in NP` is not established. A partition certificate does not obviously verify the universal restriction condition in polynomial time; semantic formula equality is coNP-hard, and syntactic equality still needs a precise normal form.
- Remark 4.3, lines 361-370: the claimed equivalence to a classical NP-containment problem and the implication toward `coNP subset NP` are not proved.
- Lemma 4.4, lines 374-380: tautological under the named hypothesis.
- Corollary 4.5, lines 384-395: fails because Theorem 4.1 fails. It also silently assumes `P_sigma^cas subset NP^cas`.
- Conjecture 5.1, lines 437-441: acceptable as conjecture, but “exploits” is undefined, so the “equivalently” clause is not formal.
- Line 474: `(c1)+...+(c5)` is not equivalent to the conjecture as stated. `(c1)-(c4)` are existence claims; only `(c5)` resembles completeness.
- Propositions 6.1-6.3, lines 513-571: overclaimed. The BGS proposition is false as stated: relative to an oracle for the hard language, the language is decidable. The RR and AW propositions do not check the technical definitions of naturalization or algebrization.
- `Theorem 6.4`, lines 575-579: not established; it inherits the defects above and omits H$_{\sigma\text{-irr-NP}}$.
- Spectral count, lines 601-616: numerically consistent with the cited scripts, but “exact in Q(sqrt 5)” is stronger than the local script alone supports; the script uses NumPy eigendecomposition plus heuristic classification.
- Dynamical diagnostic, lines 630-649: the reported R2/R3 values are tautological consequences of explicitly projecting onto `P_fix`. They are not independent evidence for closure dynamics.
- Status claims, lines 687-699: overstate what is proved. The restricted separation, barrier evasion, and generic irreducibility theorem remain unproved.

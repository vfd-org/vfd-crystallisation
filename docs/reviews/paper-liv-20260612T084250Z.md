**1. Claim Audit**

- `paper-liv.tex:64-87`, Theorem 1: the repaired perfectness and maximality proofs for `2I` itself are basically sound. The perfectness proof at `:85` correctly rules out an index-2 commutator subgroup using the absence of noncentral order-2 elements in `S^3`. The maximality proof at `:87` correctly uses the finite `SU(2)` classification plus nonsolvability of `2I`.
- But the statement “`2I` is the unique perfect finite subgroup” at `:79` is false under standard conventions unless the trivial subgroup is explicitly excluded. The trivial group is perfect and has no nontrivial abelian quotient. Add “nontrivial non-abelian” or state that degenerate one-point substrates are excluded.
- `:79`, “Either of (W3a) … selects it alone” inherits the same defect. W3a alone does not exclude the trivial subgroup as currently stated.
- `:90-91`, “The crystal is its own symmetry group”: supported computationally by CF1, but no mathematical proof is given in the text. Also “exact closure” is stronger than the floating/rounded verification in `scripts/verify_crystal_forcing.py`.
- `:97`, “there is no larger finite crystal” is too broad. There are finite cyclic subgroups of `S^3` of arbitrarily large order. The theorem proves no strictly larger finite subgroup containing `2I`.
- `:106-109`, Burnside-Dixon dimensions: the script supports the stated degree multiset. The theorem should say this is a computational verification unless the Burnside-Dixon derivation is written out.
- `:112-118`, McKay graph: CF4 supports the affine-E8 graph and `A d = 2d`. But the claim “from the multiplication table alone” at `:104` is not true for the McKay step as written: `chi_2(g)=2q_0(g)` uses vertex coordinates.
- `:130-137`, Spectral self-embedding: GE0-GE3 pass and the strengthened GE1/GE1b checks do support label-preserving recovery of the full Gram matrix and pointwise recovery up to a global orthogonal isometry.
- However `:135` says “Procrustes rotation.” Running the same construction gives `det(R) = -1.0000000000000004`; the checked map is an improper orthogonal isometry, not a rotation. Either enforce an oriented eigenbasis and check `det R=+1`, or say “orthogonal Procrustes isometry.”
- `:141`, “the reconstruction needs no choices” is too strong for coordinates. The metric/Gram reconstruction is basis-independent; coordinates are determined only up to `O(4)`.

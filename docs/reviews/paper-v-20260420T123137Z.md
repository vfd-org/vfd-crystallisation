**1. Claim Audit**

- “the 600-cell is the unique one whose vertex-graph Laplacian spectrum simultaneously contains … \(\mathbb Q(\sqrt5)\setminus\mathbb Q\) and … \(15\)” ([paper-v.tex:126](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-v/paper-v.tex:126)).  
  Verdict: not yet referee-grade as a theorem. The proof does establish that the 600-cell has both properties, and the 120-cell cannot have \(15\) because \(\lambda_{\max}(L)\le 2d=8\) for a 4-regular graph. But the exclusion of the other seven “no \(\sqrt5\)” cases is only as good as the script’s heuristic detector for numbers of the form \(a+b\sqrt5\). That is numerical evidence, not a rigorous algebraic certification.

- “the 120-cell is handled separately by the degree-4 argument” ([paper-v.tex:135](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-v/paper-v.tex:135)).  
  Verdict: this part is airtight for the stated conjunction. To kill the theorem, the 120-cell would need \(15\); it cannot. You do not need to know whether its spectrum has \(\sqrt5\)-entries.

- “The 2D case … is excluded … there is no obstruction within the 2D infinite family” ([paper-v.tex:154](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-v/paper-v.tex:154)).  
  Verdict: wrong as stated relative to the actual theorem. Every cycle graph is 2-regular, so \(\lambda_{\max}(L)\le 4\); no regular polygon can have eigenvalue \(15\). The 2D family is not a counterexample source once property (ii) is included.

- “Eigenvalue structure, computational” ([paper-v.tex:99](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-v/paper-v.tex:99)).  
  Verdict: stated honestly. The manuscript proves only floating-point recovery plus imported exact forms; it does not certify the algebraic forms internally.

- “\(R_D(4)=15\) exactly” ([paper-v.tex:204](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-v/paper-v.tex:204)).  
  Verdict: this one is substantially better. Given the exhaustive equitable-partition enumeration and standard lumpability, the stated proof does support the claim.

- “Hopf-fiber cycle spectrum, conditional” ([paper-v.tex:255](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-v/paper-v.tex:255)).  
  Verdict: fine. The conditional status is made explicit and the proof only claims what it uses.

**1. Claim Audit**
- “Let `\Gcal` be a finite connected undirected graph… `\Delta_\Gcal u = S-\bar S` has a solution `u`, unique up to an additive constant” (lines 143-145). The proof at lines 147-149 does establish this. The only mild defect is cosmetic: it cites Proposition 170 below for `\sum(S-\bar S)=0`, but that identity is immediate from the definition of `\bar S`.
- “The total source deviation vanishes” (lines 170-174). Established exactly by lines 177-179.
- “Discrete divergence theorem” (lines 185-193). The proof at lines 196-198 is correct given the paper’s boundary convention. The statement would be cleaner if the boundary of an undirected graph were not written as ordered pairs in `E_H`, but the cancellation argument is sound.
- Vacuum example, especially “Zero source `\Rightarrow` constant potential `\Rightarrow` zero `K_{\mathrm{pot}}` `\Rightarrow` geodesic betweenness uniform” (line 241). The first three implications are fine. The last one is not established by the preceding math; uniform betweenness here comes from vertex-transitivity of `K_{5,5}`, imported from XXVI, not from `K_{\mathrm{pot}}=0`.
- Bottleneck example, especially “Localised source `\Rightarrow` nonzero potential `\Rightarrow` nonzero curvature `\Rightarrow` geodesic concentration” and “The source-geometry-geodesic bridge is demonstrated” (line 274). The arithmetic at lines 251-270 is fine, and the geodesic concentration claim is supported by XXVI. But the arrow chain is still stronger than the paper proves. What is shown is coexistence on one toy graph, not a derived source-to-geodesic law.
- Perturbation example: the orbit reduction, source values, Poisson solve, and `K_{\mathrm{pot}}` values (lines 292-322) are internally correct. The weak point is line 324: “Geodesic betweenness therefore redistributes primarily through the attachment vertices `\mathcal A`, qualitatively matching the `K_{\mathrm{pot}}` pattern.” No betweenness computation is given, and no cited source establishes it for this perturbed graph. As written, this is an unsupported claim.
- Perturbation conclusion, line 326: “the source-field-curvature chain … is realised with fully checked arithmetic.” No. The source-to-potential-to-`K_{\mathrm{pot}}` arithmetic is checked. The geodesic part is not.
- Abstract lines 38-44 and Remark lines 352-353 still overstate the result. “showing how it drives geometric response,” “source tells geometry how to distort, geometry tells geodesics how to propagate,” and “supports the same qualitative dynamical pattern (source `\to` geometry `\to` motion) as general relativity” go beyond what the paper proves. No motion law is derived anywhere.

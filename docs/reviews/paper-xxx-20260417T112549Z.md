**1. Claim Audit**

1. Lines 323-338: “**Conditional Born weighting from stationary sector measure**.” The proof does **not** derive measurement statistics from closure dynamics. It proves a sector-decomposition identity **once assumption (4) already equates frequencies with integrated sector mass**. The paper itself admits this at lines 345-350. So the proof establishes: if you postulate sampling from the constrained stationary ensemble and assume sector support/negligible overlap, then the algebra reduces to Born form. It does **not** establish that physical measurements sample that ensemble.

2. Lines 444-447: “**Sector-preserving unitaries induce sector-probability covariance**.” Overstated. The proof at lines 452-462 uses the exact identification \(P_i(\Psi)=|c_i|^2\), which only holds in the sector-supported Route C regime. The proposition statement quantifies over all \(\Psi\in\Mcl(\Ocal)\) and does not state the needed sector-supported hypothesis.

3. Lines 493-506: “**Closure Hessian as the Fisher metric ... in the Laplace regime**.” This is basically fine as a **local Laplace-approximation statement**. Hidden hypotheses are doing work: finite-dimensional parameter family, non-degenerate minimum, regularity allowing differentiation under the integral, and Gaussian approximation accuracy. Those should be in the statement, not buried in prose.

4. Lines 544-551: “**Fubini--Study identification, given Papers XVIII and XXI**.” The corollary proves much less than it advertises. \(K1\) already imports a global \(U(N)\)-invariant Kähler structure from Paper XXI and already says it is Fubini--Study up to scale. What remains is local scale matching. The proof’s key bridge at line 559, identifying the configuration-space Fisher metric with the simplex Fisher--Rao metric via the Nelson pairing, is asserted rather than established.

5. Lines 596-601: “**Non-contextuality of sector probability for complete observers**.” The proof only establishes equality for a **fixed** \(\Psi\) for which both observers are complete. The theorem statement then says “\(\forall \Psi\in\Mcl\),” which is wrong: completeness is state-dependent in this paper’s own definitions.

6. Lines 612-619: “**Restricted (U2) ...**.” This is only a **restricted** frame-function statement for a restricted observer class plus Route C hypotheses. Fine as such. Not fine as a discharge of the full \(U2\) used later in Theorem 650.

7. Lines 650-669: “**Born weighting uniqueness under closure-derived hypotheses**.” As an abstract Gleason argument on full \(\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\), the proof is acceptable. As a **closure-derived** theorem, it over-claims. The paper has not shown that closure dynamics supplies global \(U2\) on all orthonormal bases, nor that closure itself constructs the global Kähler structure rather than importing it from XXI. What is proved is: **if** \(U1\)-\(U4\) hold on full \(\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\), then Born follows.

8. Lines 701-706: “**Exclusion of \(|c|^p\) for \(p\neq2\)**.” This one is fine. The counterexample is explicit and does the job.

9. Lines 735-736: \(N=2\) “**handled separately**.” Asserted, not proved. No proof and no external citation are given for the claimed uniqueness on the Bloch sphere under the strengthened hypotheses.

10. Lines 774-796: the two-basin example is honest. It is schematic arithmetic, not a derivation. No complaint there.

11. Lines 53 and 948-952: the manuscript-level claim that the paper gives a “**conditional derivation**” and that “**no other sector-probability assignment ... is admissible**” is too strong unless repeatedly qualified by: imported XXI geometry, restricted XXIX observer class, completeness, Laplace regime, and the unresolved globalisation gap.

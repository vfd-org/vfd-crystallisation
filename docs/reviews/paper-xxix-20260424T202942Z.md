**1. Claim Audit**

- `paper-xxix.tex:203-209`, Lemma “Basin locality”: proved only in the tautological sense that the definition of `B_\phi` refers to `(\Fcal,\phi)`. It does not show independence from other minima in any dynamical/geometric sense, since the global shape of `\Fcal` includes them.

- `paper-xxix.tex:211-213`, basin partition claim: not proved. Needs finite-dimensionality, complete gradient flow, Morse-Smale or equivalent hypotheses, and no escape to infinity.

- `paper-xxix.tex:251-259`, Theorem “LMC”: established, but only because Definition 3 hard-wires vanishing coherence cost on every admitted basin. This is definitional, not a substantive measurement theorem.

- `paper-xxix.tex:274-283`, Corollary on basin-admissibility: follows from LMC. No issue internally, except its force is exactly as weak as the definitional LMC assumption.

- `paper-xxix.tex:321-325`, claim that the toy model is a one-probe instance of Definition 3: not established. The toy model checks vanishing at `\Phi_A`, not on the whole basin `B_{\phi_A}`.

- `paper-xxix.tex:497-515`, toy numerical example: arithmetic is correct: `\Ccal(\Phi_A)=0`, `\Ccal(\Phi_B)=4`, so exclusion holds only for `0<\epsilon<4`. The stronger language “dynamically inaccessible” is not proved by the threshold calculation.

- `paper-xxix.tex:179`, generic reduction of `N` minima: plausible but unproved. The text admits this needs transversality/non-degeneracy; until supplied, say “may contain fewer” rather than “will generically contain fewer.”

- `paper-xxix.tex:144`, structural observer “naturally induces” the XXIV observer pair: not proved. This is the paper’s central missing bridge.

- `paper-xxix.tex:339-345`, measurement as resolution: definition of an admissible set does not prove dynamical stabilisation, collapse, or selection.

**2. Internal Consistency**

- Observer object changes type. `\Ocal` is a connected subgraph of `\Gcal` at `121-122`, but probe components are subsets of `\Ecal` at `220-224`. Decide whether observers are vertex sets, induced subgraphs, or subgraphs.

- Probe observers relax connectedness at `218-224`, but connectedness belongs to Definition `observer_region`, not directly to Definition `observer`. Also boundedness is not restated for probe observers.

- LMC is too strong by definition. Condition 3 at `231-235` makes every admitted basin zero-cost for every probe. Then the coherence cost no longer distinguishes outcomes; the “matched vs idle” distinction at `240` is not represented mathematically.

- Locality conflict: each `c_{\phi_j}` depends only on local/boundary data (`227-230`), but must decide membership in global basins (`231-235`). This is generally impossible unless basin membership is locally determined on those coordinates.

- Consciousness status contradicts itself. `469` says no necessity claim is established; `534` says necessary structural conditions are identified.

- Crystallisation notation is inconsistent with local-minimum language. `423` uses `argmin`, suggesting global minimisation, while earlier sections repeatedly discuss local minima and basins.

**3. External Consistency**

- XXIV observer definition is correctly reported at `115`: Paper XXIV defines `O=(\leq_O,\mu_O)`; verified in `paper-xxiv.tex:65-73`.

- XXIV frame claims are overgeneralised at `378`. Paper XXIV proves simultaneity only for incomparable pairs (`paper-xxiv.tex:116-124`) and endpoint-interval difference only under unequal weight sums (`143-164`). XXIX omits these hypotheses.

- XXIII no-global-clock claim at `394` is too broad. Paper XXIII proves non-unique linear extensions conditional on incomparable events (`paper-xxiii.tex:205-220`), not an unconditional no-clock theorem for the full event poset.

- XXVIII weak/strong regime claim at `335` and `442-446` is too strong. Paper XXVIII explicitly calls weak/strong ordering a suggestive label, not a derivational linkage (`paper-xxviii.tex:248-254`).

- XXVIII’s Route A/B/G separation is not respected carefully enough. XXVIII states `\Delta_A`, `\Delta_B`, and `\Delta_G` act on different spaces and are not identified (`paper-xxviii.tex:98-111`). XXIX’s unified `\Fcal`, `\Gcal`, and observer-constraint language risks collapsing distinctions XXVIII explicitly preserved.

- XXX usage is mostly consistent. Paper XXX really does use XXIX’s LMC plus completeness to get restricted, state-specific non-contextuality (`paper-xxx.tex:576-603`) and says global U2 needs universal completeness plus basis-realisability (`610-648`). XXIX’s summary at `295` is acceptable, though it should cite Paper XXX formally.

- XIV stationary-distribution attribution at `367` is verifiable: Paper XIV proves the stationary distribution under smoothness, boundedness, compactness/boundary, irreducibility/regularity assumptions (`paper-xiv.tex:174-190`). XXIX should not imply this is unconditional.

**6. Top Three Fixes**

1. Fix the XXIV bridge (`144`, `383-385`). Give an actual construction from `(\Ocal,\Ccal_\Ocal)` to `(\leq_O,\mu_O)`, or demote all frame-recovery claims to conjectural placement language.

2. Repair the localised-probe definition (`217-235`, `240`, `251-259`). The current LMC theorem is true only because the definition assumes the result. State the feasibility assumptions for local probes, prove non-emptiness, and separate outcome readout from zero coherence cost.

3. Downgrade quantum/crystallisation claims (`335`, `339-345`, `423-431`, `442-449`). XXIX defines a constraint filter; it does not prove measurement dynamics, outcome selection, weak/strong regime transition, or a common substrate beyond XXVIII’s explicitly limited bridge hypothesis.

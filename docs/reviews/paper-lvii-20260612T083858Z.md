**Claim Audit**

- `31`: “All computational claims verified ... (23/23 PASS).” Verified: `verify_residual_closure.py` reports 23 PASS. But 7/23 are SM/gauge checks belonging to Paper LVI, not LVII-only residual checks.
- `56`: “the result is identically the Palatini form of the Einstein--Hilbert action.” Not established in the manuscript. This is a sketch of Deser’s argument; it omits the stress-tensor variation, boundary-term handling, and field-density conventions needed for a derivation.
- `60-62`: “The fixed point is Fierz--Pauli.” Not proved as a theorem. The script verifies a finite numerical witness on three random grid fields: constant `0.500002`, spread `3.90e-05`. That supports “numerical check,” not the stated theorem title.
- `64-66`: “Gravity gravitates, exactly.” Over-claimed. The symbolic check verifies the isotropic Schwarzschild ansatz through `O(eps^2)`, plus the first-order null residual. It does not prove the general self-coupling mechanism or uniqueness. Also the displayed residual drops the `M^2` scaling if `A=M/2r`.
- `76-82`: “Cramer upgrade ... any bounded or sub-Gaussian distribution of finite variance.” Under-specified. Need non-degenerate `x^2`, finite exponential moments near zero, and a multivariate/tensor version for the collective metric. The proof is scalar; the theorem is field/tensorial.
- `84`: “Non-Gaussianity changes only the coefficient.” Not proved at stated generality. Arbitrary non-Gaussian mode laws can change the quadratic form, not merely one scalar coefficient, unless isotropy/identical covariance structure is assumed.
- `92-103`: “Field-dynamics continuum control.” Partly established, partly imported. Direct checks cover degree 1 and 2 harmonics; the full `k <= 5` claim relies on `docs/rung-dimension-ladder.md`, not proved here. The eigenvalue “rate” uses a small-`theta` expansion while this theorem fixes `theta=pi/5`; state the rescaled limit explicitly.
- `99`: “Aliasing bound ... exactly `1/(1+r^2 lambda)`.” Exact per eigenspace of `(I+r^2L)^{-1`; for general above-band content it is a norm bound using the smallest above-band eigenvalue. The script checks one highest mode.
- `111-119`: H-shell-96 and conditional `G`. Algebra and numerics check out under the hypothesis: `G_pred = 6.67305e-11`, relative deviation `-1.88e-04`. But this is not an unconditional determination; the integer shell itself is explicitly open elsewhere.
- `123`: look-elsewhere `p approx 0.006`. Arithmetic is plausible for 14 independent uniform shell offsets and a pre-fixed `2e-4` window. Those assumptions are not justified.
- `147`: “None of the surviving items ... is of the missing-mechanism kind.” Rhetorical, not mathematical. The shell-96 derivation and correlated-regime extension are still substantive missing mechanisms.

**1. Claim Audit**
- “derives which groups are available and why no others” (l.32) is not established. The paper derives, at most, factors arising from the stated quaternionic/octonionic arena mechanism. It does not exclude internal symmetries from other structures, representations, quotients, discrete remnants, or independent abelian factors.
- “only renderable arenas are … 3-sphere and … 7-sphere” (l.35, l.49) overstates the cited rung-ladder source. The local source says Adams gives `S^0,S^1,S^3,S^7`, with rendered perspectives on 1-, 3-, or 7-dimensional arenas. The exclusion of `S^1` is an extra hypothesis.
- The zero-divisor claim `(e_2-e_9)(e_6-e_{13})=0` (l.49) is locally verified: `verify_residual_closure.py` reports SM6 PASS with exactly that product.
- The quaternionic frame split claim (l.50, l.79) is locally supported by Paper LIII/T14 for the actual 600-cell coarse bilinear. It is not a full continuum theorem for all of `S^3` as stated.
- “tick distinguishes an axis” and “rendering chart on the octonionic arena requires selecting the unit” (l.51) is not proved in this manuscript. SM5 verifies a finite quaternionic clock element; it does not establish the octonionic chart necessity.
- Theorem “Der(O)=g2, dimension 14” (l.60-61): the nullity 14 is verified locally. The identification with `g_2` still uses the standard theorem that derivations of the octonions form compact real `g_2`; it is not obtained from dimension alone. The phrase “No Lie theory is assumed” is false.
- Theorem “clock-unit stabiliser is su(3)” (l.64-70): mostly supported, but the proof is underwritten by omitted facts. To say the stabiliser lies in `su(3)`, the paper must explicitly use skew-Hermiticity/norm preservation of octonion derivations, not just complex-linearity and tracelessness. The “simple” check is also only a random-element numerical witness, not a proof. Since the stabiliser is 8-dimensional and claimed inside 8-dimensional `su(3)`, the clean proof is equality by dimension after the inclusion is properly stated.
- “The continuous group it generates is a distinguished U(1)” (l.81) is mathematically wrong as written. A finite order-10 element generates a cyclic subgroup, not a continuous `U(1)`. It selects or lies in a unique circle subgroup.
- Theorem “The inventory” (l.83-86) is the main overclaim. The paper’s own honest boundary says the derived `U(1)` sits inside the internal `SU(2)` (l.113). Then the structure is not `SU(3) x SU(2) x U(1)` as an independent product. It is `SU(3) x SU(2)` with a distinguished `U(1)_clock \subset SU(2)`, unless an independent hypercharge factor is derived.
- “More groups would require more division algebras” (l.86) is not proved and is not generally true. It is only true for “more groups from this particular division-algebra arena mechanism.”
- Theorem “Lie-algebra forcing” (l.95-101) is not proved by the stated evidence. YM1 checks one positive `su(2)` case; YM2 checks one non-Jacobi null. That does not prove all consistent self-couplings are Lie algebra valued. The general statement is only cited at l.101, not re-derived.
- The YM null says the bad tensor is “antisymmetric in its first two indices” (l.99). With the convention `F^a_{\mu\nu}=...+f^{abc}A^b_\mu A^c_\nu`, the relevant antisymmetry/Jacobi convention needs to be stated carefully. The code antisymmetrises `(a,b)`, while the field-strength product naturally points to `(b,c)` unless all indices are lowered with an invariant metric.
- Heavy-sector shell placement “Z at shell 82, offset -0.049” (l.116) is locally supported by `papers/cascade-derivation/cascade-masses.md`. It remains a witnessed placement, not a derivation, as the sentence correctly says.

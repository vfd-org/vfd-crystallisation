**1. Claim Audit**
1. `“Under Axiom~\ref{ax:self-sim} ... has a unique positive solution ... globally attracting”` ([paper-xxxvi.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:129)): established. This is the one clean theorem in the paper.

The proof at lines 220-222 is tautological: after excluding the unwanted invariants by fiat, only the wanted invariants remain. That is a definition of the model class, not a theorem about all admissible functionals.
3. `“one proposed chain is ... with seven non-trivial rungs”` and `“Each step is forced ... exactly one refinement”` ([paper-xxxvi.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:256), [283](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:283)): not established. The proof checks that the proposed chain is compatible with the author’s preferred inputs; it does not rule out alternatives. The sentence at line 283 directly over-claims beyond line 261’s own disclaimer.
4. `“The total closure depth ... satisfies ... 583”` ([paper-xxxvi.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:330)): established only as a dimension count from Definition 4.1. It does not establish the shell-amplitude rule `K=\varphi^{-1}` ([320-323](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:320)) or the residue interpretation at line 347.
5. `“F is a \sigma-intertwiner”` ([paper-xxxvi.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:372)): only partially established, and only under stronger hypotheses than the paper later uses. **2. Internal Consistency**
1. Rung count is inconsistent. The paper repeatedly says `seven non-trivial rungs` ([66](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:66), [261](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:261)), but the displayed chain has six non-trivial reductions and ends at the trivial rung `0`; line 281 itself calls `8` the last non-trivial entry.
2. Epistemic status is inconsistent. The abstract says only F1 is rigorous and F2/F4/F5 are propositions under model choices ([57](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:57)); the Discussion says F1/F4/F5 are fully rigorous but F2 is structural ([654-656](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:654)); the Conclusion then says F1/F2/F4/F5 are fully rigorous ([693](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:693)). That is not a cosmetic issue; it is the central bookkeeping claim of the paper.
3. F5 and F8 do not fit together. F5 requires rational coefficients ([373](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:373)); F8 assigns coefficients built from `137+\pi/87` and `3/8` ([555-556](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:555)). The paper never explains whether the actual matched functional still satisfies F5.
4. Definition 2.1 and F2’s ansatz are internally sloppy. “Minimal order” counts degree in `(\Phi,\partial\Phi)` only ([185](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:185)), yet `R[\Phi]` uses second derivatives ([199](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:199)); excluded terms such as `\nabla\Phi\cdot\nabla\Phi` are of the same schematic order as included `E[\Phi]` ([200](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:200), [203-207](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:203)).
5. The `D_4` tensor algebra is inconsistent. Definition 4.1 correctly gives `24^2=576` as `\Sym^2(24)\oplus\Lambda^2(24)` ([307-313](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:307)), but F7 later writes `24\otimes24 = \Sym^2(24)\oplus\Lambda^2(24)\oplus\mathbb R` ([477-480](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:477)), which is dimensionally false.
6. The paper misdescribes several labelled results: `Theorem~\ref{thm:F2}`, `Theorem~\ref{thm:F8}`, and `Theorem~\ref{thm:lambda}` are in fact propositions ([225](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:225), [511](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:511), [693](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:693)).

**6. Top Three Fixes**
1. Fix the epistemic bookkeeping everywhere. The paper’s main selling point is “honest dependency graph,” but the abstract, discussion, and conclusion contradict each other about what is rigorous; F6/F7/`\Lambda` are still being written in theorem voice after the paper has admitted they are not proved ([57](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:57), [443](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:443), [654-658](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:654), [693](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:693)).
2. Repair the mathematical spine around F5/F7/F4. As written, F5’s hypothesis on rational coefficients conflicts with F8; F5’s factor-2 corollary is not even formulated on the same object as F; and F7 contains a dimensionally false tensor decomposition that infects the `583` story ([372-400](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:372), [477-480](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:477), [552-557](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:552)).
3. Stop calling model-class restrictions “theorems.” F2 and F3 are the worst offenders: F2 is a chosen ansatz after exclusions, and F3 is a preferred chain under extra inputs, not a uniqueness result. Until rewritten as definitions/conjectures/model postulates, the manuscript reads as knowingly overstating its case ([206-222](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:206), [256-287](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxxvi/paper-xxxvi.tex:256)).

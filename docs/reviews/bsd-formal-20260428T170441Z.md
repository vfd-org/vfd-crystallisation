**1. Claim Audit**
- Lines 56-58: “Both hypotheses ultimately reduce to classical Hecke-operator theory ... Kolyvagin and Gross-Zagier.” Not established. Kolyvagin/Gross-Zagier do not discharge higher-rank BSD, so this is an over-claim.
- Lines 85-88: “`T_E`'s convergence theorem ... and spectral identification ... no direct mainstream analogue.” Over-claim. The theorem is only for `T_E(s)` on `Re(s)>1`; spectral identification is an open bridge axiom.
- Lines 126-127: “Kolyvagin: rank 0 case” and “Gross-Zagier + Kolyvagin: rank 1 case.” Too broad. These theorems prove major analytic-rank ≤1 implications, not BSD in this blanket phrasing.
- Lines 134-151: Proposition “Formal consequence...” is valid only as a tautological implication from `B_BSD,rank` and `B_BSD,spec`, plus an implicit equality between spectral multiplicity and `dim ker`. It proves no BSD content.
- Lines 198-200: “`T_p` acts on `H^1(E,Z)`... eigenvalue `a_p(E)`.” Not correct as stated. Hecke operators naturally act on modular-curve/Jacobian/cohomological Hecke modules, not on bare singular cohomology of `E` without specifying the Eichler-Shimura transfer.
- Lines 204-213: Definition of `T_E` is not well-defined. `v_\varphi(p)` is not a standard valuation: `\varphi` is a unit in `Z[\varphi]`, so there is no `\varphi`-adic valuation of rational primes as written.
- Lines 216-235: Termwise bound is not proved. The proof obtains `2\varphi log p`, then “absorbs” `\varphi` into `≤ 2 log p`, which is false. Also `|b_p-a_p|` would generally be bounded by `4sqrt(p)`, not `2sqrt(p)`, unless extra structure is supplied.
- Lines 248-263: Absolute convergence on `Re(s)>1` is proved only after fixing the operator space and constant. It does not define `T_E(1)`.
- Lines 266-273: Numerical claim is only a truncated computation. The script output gives a truncated Hasse upper bound about `10.5259` at `Re(s)=1` and `0.6376` at `Re(s)=2`; the manuscript’s `10.5·\varphi` and `0.32·\varphi` normalization is not cleanly reproduced.

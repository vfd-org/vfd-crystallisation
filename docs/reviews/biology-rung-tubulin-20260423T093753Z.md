**1. Claim Audit**
1. **Theorem T.1 is not proved as stated.** Claim: “Then \(n=13\), and the 13 adjacency directions are realised as the 13 distinct characters of \(C_{13}\)” ([biology-rung-tubulin.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/biology-rung-tubulin/biology-rung-tubulin.tex:183)). The proof only gets \(n\ge 13\) from distinct addressability ([190-195](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/biology-rung-tubulin/biology-rung-tubulin.tex:190)). The step “if \(n>13\) ... the addressing is redundant, not a selector; hence \(n=13\)” ([195-198](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/biology-rung-tubulin/biology-rung-tubulin.tex:195)) is an extra minimality axiom not present in Definition D.1. A 13-dimensional representation of \(C_n\) with 13 distinct characters exists for every \(n\ge 13\).
2. **T.1 also overstates what primality does.** “Primality of 13 implies \(C_{13}\) has 13 distinct one-dimensional irreducible representations” is false as written; every \(C_n\) has \(n\) one-dimensional irreps. Primality only implies every nontrivial character is primitive. The draft conflates “number of characters” with “all nontrivial characters generate the full group” ([191-205](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/biology-rung-tubulin/biology-rung-tubulin.tex:191)).
3. **T.1 does not prove independent addressability; it assumes it.** Definition D.1 already requires “The eigenmodes are independently addressable” ([177-178](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/biology-rung-tubulin/biology-rung-tubulin.tex:177)). The theorem cannot then advertise that as a consequence.
4. **Theorem T.2 is only partly proved.** Claim: “For any \(n\in\{11,12,14,15,16\}\), \(C_n\) fails to realise the 13-adjacency selector” ([208-210](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/biology-rung-tubulin/biology-rung-tubulin.tex:208)). The cases \(n=11,12\) fail immediately by count. The cases \(n=14,15,16\) are not ruled out by D.1. The subgroup/factorisation language ([221-239](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/biology-rung-tubulin/biology-rung-tubulin.tex:221)) is handwaving; for an abelian cyclic group, existence of proper subgroups does not itself force degeneracy among 13 chosen characters.
5. **“Reducible/indecomposable” is used without a definition that makes the proofs work.** The action of any abelian group on a complex vector space is a sum of one-dimensional eigenspaces, so “indecomposable selector” needs a separate formal definition. At present it is rhetoric, not mathematics.
6. **The corollary does not follow.** Claim: “the 13-protofilament microtubule count is a forced structural prediction, not a chemical coincidence” ([242-247](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/biology-rung-tubulin/biology-rung-tubulin.tex:242)). Since T.1 and the \(n>13\) part of T.2 fail, this is unsupported.
7. **The empirical-strength claims are inflated.** “No falsifier known” ([365-368](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/biology-rung-tubulin/biology-rung-tubulin.tex:365)) and “Items 1 and 2 are satisfied ... at ~100% level; they confirm the hypothesis” ([380-383](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/biology-rung-tubulin/biology-rung-tubulin.tex:380)) are not established by the cited literature in this draft.

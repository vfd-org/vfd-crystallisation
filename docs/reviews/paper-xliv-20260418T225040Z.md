**1. Claim Audit**

1. [paper-xliv.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xliv/paper-xliv.tex:47): “There exists a coarsening functor … implemented as a partial trace over the `120/16 = 7.5`-fold redundant fibre.”
This is not established. A partial trace requires a tensor-factorisation; none is given. `120/16=7.5` is itself a warning that no such fibre decomposition has been specified. The body later defines `\Kcal` as a composition of linear projections `\mathbb C^{120}\to\mathbb C^{24}\to\mathbb C^{16}`; that is not the same thing as a partial trace.

2. [paper-xliv.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xliv/paper-xliv.tex:48): “The reduced dynamics on `\Hcal_{16}` satisfies Lindblad form to leading order…”
Not proved. The proof at lines 133–137 is “standard derivation” plus an unproved Born–Markov approximation. No environment, no system-environment split, no weak-coupling scaling, no semigroup limit, no identification of the `L_k`, and no argument that the reduced map family is Markovian. This is a conjectural template, not a theorem.

Not established. Proposition 4 at lines 147–152 assumes that the ingredients of Paper XXX are “invariant under the CPTP map `\Kcal`,” but that is not proved and is not what Paper XXX claims. Paper XXX is explicitly conditional and completeness-sensitive; this paper ignores those hypotheses.

False as stated or at least ill-typed. A CPTP channel acts on trace-class operators or density matrices, not on Hilbert vectors. The proposition states `\Kcal : \Hcal_{120}\to\Hcal_{16}` and then immediately writes `\Kcal(\rho)=\sum_k K_k\rho K_k^\dagger`. The object being mapped changes from vectors to density matrices without definition. The theorem statement also contains an unresolved placeholder at line 95: `N_K = 120/16 \cdot \lceil ? \rceil = ...`.

**2. Internal Consistency**

1. [paper-xliv.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xliv/paper-xliv.tex:77): the paper conflates three different objects:
`|X_\Cl|=16` vertices of the tesseract,
`\Hcal_{16}=\mathbb C^{16}`,
and the “even Clifford algebra `\Cl^+(1,3)`.”
Those are not interchangeable. The full real Clifford algebra has dimension 16; the even subalgebra has dimension 8. The specific identification `\Cl^+(1,3)\cong \mathbb H\oplus\mathbb H` is also not the standard one for signature `(1,3)`. The target space of the paper is therefore not even consistently defined.

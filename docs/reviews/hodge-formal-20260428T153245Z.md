**1. Claim Audit**

With `\Pi_\sigma` coefficientwise, “algebraicity of the fixed part” is just the Hodge conjecture after scalar extension unless an additional bridge axiom identifies fixed cascade modes with algebraic cycles. `H_{4a}` in Foundations is only embedding plus compatibility, not algebraicity.

- Lines 412-445, flag varieties: Bruhat/Schubert facts are classical, but the degree convention is wrong: `\bar C_w` has dimension `\ell(w)`, so its Poincare-dual cohomology degree is not generally `2\ell(w)`. Setup mixes complex `G/P` with compact quotients `U(n)/...`.

- Lines 504-522, CM abelian varieties: invalid. Deligne proves Hodge classes on abelian varieties are absolute Hodge; CDK proves the Hodge locus is algebraic. Neither implies the Hodge conjecture. The paper cites the wrong theorem for algebraicity.
- Lines 545-581, complete intersections: weak Lefschetz part is fine off the middle degree; the primitive-middle-degree argument is not a proof. Noether-Lefschetz does not construct all primitive Hodge classes for all codimension `<=2` complete intersections.
- Lines 594-607, Hodge for cascade-embeddable: circular. The proof assumes the missing theorem: “the sigma-invariant cohomology of the substrate is spanned by algebraic cycles.”
- Lines 618-638, Langlands-cascade reduction: not a theorem. “Every motive gives an automorphic representation” and “cascade realizes the motive” are conjectural and not enough to construct embeddings.
- Lines 642-654, combined theorem: invalid. It depends on undefined labels, false classical claims, and the circular cascade-embeddable theorem.
- Lines 674-690, 600-cell spectrum: locally re-ran the spectrum script with `python3`; the `94/120=78.3%` and `26/120=21.7%` numbers match.

Also R2/R3 force `P_fix` by construction, so this is a toy projector diagnostic, not evidence that closure dynamics naturally produces the projector.

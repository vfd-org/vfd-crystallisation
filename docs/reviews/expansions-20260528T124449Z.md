**1. Claim Audit**

- “This paper makes no new mathematical claims…” lines 91-95, and “Every quantitative claim below either follows directly from Paper 1 or is explicitly cited” lines 330-332: not true. The paper makes new identification claims between Paper 1 eigenspaces and objects in the five expansion papers. Several are not proved or cited tightly enough.

- Cascade claim, lines 157-163: the block Laplacian and 28-dimensional fixed fibre are verified in `cascade-selection-h4`. But the chain `E8 -> H4 -> H3 -> D4` is inconsistent with the cited cascade sources, which use the intermediate `40`, not `H3`.

- Cascade connection, lines 167-170: “The 9 irreducible representations of `W(H4)` realised on `C[V600]` are exactly the `A_1`-eigenspaces…” Paper 1 gives the nine eigenspaces and multiplicities, but does not establish this exact irreducible-character identification in the form needed here. Add the character calculation or cite the precise theorem proving it.

- Cascade connection, lines 170-172: “The 28-dimensional `W(H4)`-fixed subspace … is a direct summand of `E_4 \oplus E_5`.” I cannot verify this. The cascade source defines the 28-dimensional fixed space inside the fibre `F^0 = V_{H4} \oplus V_{D4} \oplus V_{16} \oplus V_8`, with fixed part `0 \oplus V_{D4} \oplus V_{16} \oplus V_8`. That is not shown to be a subspace of `C[V600]`, let alone of `E_4 \oplus E_5`.

- Cascade numerical claim, lines 174-177: “`\lambda_+ = 9 - 3\sqrt5` … matches … `E_2` and `E_6`.” This is wrong as stated. `9 - 3\sqrt5 = 12 - 6\varphi`, so it corresponds to the Laplacian image of Paper 1’s `A_1` eigenvalue `6\varphi`, i.e. `E_1`, not `E_2/E_6`.

- Cosmology numerical claim, lines 193-198: the cited hypersphere paper does report the `0.45%`, `0.083%`, and `94/94` verification claims. The local source supports the numbers.

- Cosmology connection, lines 202-207: “the sigma-paired dipole class is the union `E_1 \oplus E_8`…” and the formula uses Paper 1’s closure operator restricted to that pair. I cannot verify this from the cited cosmology paper. The source uses a dipole sector with eigenvalue `6\varphi` and the complementary Laplacian value `12-6\varphi`; it does not identify its object as the single-V600 `E_1 \oplus E_8` pair, nor does it use Paper 1’s shifted closure operator `C_\varphi` in the way claimed.

- Cosmology consequence, lines 211-214: “becomes a structural prediction of the triad.” Over-claim. The cited source supports an internal cosmology construction and verification suite, not derivation from Paper 1.

- Biology claim, lines 222-228: the cited Note G does report Spearman `rho = +0.94`, `n=11`, and the conditional `H-MT-Bridge` ratio. But Note G explicitly treats the substrate mapping as unvalidated and the rho result as a consistency check, not an independent prediction.

- Biology connection, lines 232-237: “same `E_1 \oplus E_8` pair… restricted to tubulin” and “T15 … intersections … at amino-acid resolution.” Not verified. Note G’s V600 probe T15 is a uniform abstract vertex-leverage result, not a residue-intersection result. The concrete residue predictions occur elsewhere in Note G.

- Biology consequence, lines 241-244: “rho becomes evidence for a Paper-1-derived prediction.” Too strong. The cited source says the rho check is partly constructed from potency-related input data and is not independent evidence of the substrate.

- Spectral bridge claim, lines 252-263: the five-coset decomposition, selective lift, global 25-dimensional Laplacian-12 space, and `5Y_5 = 2Y_5 \oplus 3Y_5` decomposition are verified in the bridge papers.

- Spectral bridge eigenvalue claim, lines 256-260: “global V600 `\lambda=12` eigenspace (dimension 1 as eigenspace of `A_1`; 25-dimensional in broader sense)” is mathematically muddled. For Paper 1’s adjacency `A_1`, eigenvalue `12` is the one-dimensional all-ones space `E_0`. For the bridge Laplacian `L=12I-A_1`, eigenvalue `12` is Paper 1’s `A_1`-eigenvalue `0` space `E_4`, dimension 25.

- Spectral bridge connection, lines 267-274: “the `\lambda=12` object … is Paper 1’s `E_0` … augmented by … `E_4`” is wrong. The bridge’s Laplacian-12 object is `E_4`. `E_0` corresponds to Laplacian eigenvalue 0.

- Spectral bridge consequence, lines 278-283: “bridge is the `(E_0,E_4)` projection” needs rewriting. The substantive bridge is the selective lift into `E_4`; `E_0` only accounts for the global constant row.

- Existence claim, lines 291-299: the two involutions with fixed dimensions 94 and 116 are verified in the existence papers.

- Existence connection, lines 303-309: identifying `tau_ico` with Paper 1’s spectral Galois involution is broadly supported, and the fixed-space dimension sum `1+16+25+36+16=94` is correct. But Paper 1 warns that the standard geometric embedding is not literally closed under coordinatewise Galois action, so the identification needs that caveat.

- Existence open claim, lines 318-323: the deficit `120-116=4` is real, but the cited existence source already defines `tau_spec` as negating the `+6\varphi` four-dimensional dipole eigenspace. The open issue is its geometric or icosian interpretation, not merely “one of the `E_1` or `E_8` pair.”

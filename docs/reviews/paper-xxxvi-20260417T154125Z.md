**1. Claim Audit**

- Lines 127-136, F1: “has a unique positive solution … and this solution is globally attracting”. The fixed-point claim is fine. The proof is not. On lines 145-153 you invoke Banach on `I=[1,2]`, but `|T'(1)|=1`, so `T` is not a contraction on all of `I`; you only get contraction after one iterate lands in `[3/2,2]`. This is repairable, but as written the proof overstates what Banach gives.

- Lines 187-195, Proposition F2-basis: “the space of admissible densities is three-dimensional”. Not established. The statement is far too broad for the setup in lines 173-181. `\Phi` is an arbitrary finite-rank tensor field, yet the alleged basis elements `R,E,Q` are not even well-defined for all such ranks. No classification argument is supplied; “standard” on line 200 is not a proof.

- Lines 205-210, F2: “Any closure functional … has the form …”. Not established, because the basis proposition fails. The sign argument on lines 213-215 is also backwards: if `Q` is “manifestly non-negative”, inserting `-γQ` works against `F[\Phi]\ge 0`, not for it.

- Lines 245-250, F3: “the unique maximal admissible chain … is …”. Not proved. Steps 2-6 are a chain of assertions, not an exhaustive classification. The worst point is lines 268-270: “The octonion algebra has trivial centre; the unique cascade terminus is the zero ideal.” The premise is false; the octonions have centre `\mathbb R`. That alone collapses Step 6.

- Lines 312-317, F4: “The total closure depth … is 583 … Equivalently, `I-F` … has trivial kernel and cokernel of dimension 583”. The first sentence is only a dimension count from the preceding definition. The second is false. If `F=\varphi^{-1}Id`, then `I-F` is invertible, so both kernel and cokernel are zero.

- Lines 346-350, F5: “Suppose the coefficients … are rational … Then `F` is `\sigma`-invariant”. Plausible only after major hidden assumptions: that the field space, contractions, measure, and `\sigma`-action are actually defined over `\mathbb Q(\varphi)` in the required way. None of that is stated cleanly. As written, the proof is schematic.

- Lines 361-365, Corollary “Factor 2”: not established from F5 alone. Line 369 quietly adds “for `\sigma`-invariant closure fields”; the corollary statement does not. You also need additivity over the two copies and equality, not merely “equal in magnitude”.

- Lines 397-401, F6: “converges to `S^3` … with explicit rate `0.762·2^{-n}`”. Not proved. Your Burago-Ivanov statement already confuses induced metric with graph metric (lines 385-389), and the proof uses an exact halving rule and constant that you later admit are merely numerically supported (lines 609-610). This is not theorem-level.

- Lines 449-450, F7: “the closure contribution carrying `\Lambda` is rank-2 and lives on … `D_4`.” Not proved. Even granting Deser, lines 453-456 only show that a cosmological term in Einstein’s equation multiplies `g_{\mu\nu}`. They do not localise anything to `D_4`. Line 456 is also wrong: a scalar field can mimic `w=-1` if frozen in a potential.

- Lines 509-516, F8: “the three coefficients … are uniquely determined”. No. This is parameter matching to chosen target actions using imported constants, not derivation. The rung assignments “vector at 8” and “SU(2) doublet at 16” are asserted, not proved.

- Lines 523-524, Zero-Parameter Corollary: “the cascade has no free numerical parameter”. False on the paper’s own terms. You import `\alpha_em` and `\sin^2\theta_W` from Paper XXII and appeal to “Planck-intrinsic” `G` without deriving it here.

- Lines 541-545, `\Lambda`-Derivation Theorem: not established. The crucial step on lines 549-553 identifies “583 graded blocks” with “583 iterations” and then with a physical vacuum residual. None of those implications has been proved. The normalization of `v_0` is unspecified, so even the numerical equality is ungrounded.

- Lines 569-581, Cascade Theorem: merely repackages the failures above.

- By inspection, the local `\ref`/`\eqref` labels appear to exist. The problem is not broken labels; it is that several references point to material that does not prove what the surrounding prose claims it proves.

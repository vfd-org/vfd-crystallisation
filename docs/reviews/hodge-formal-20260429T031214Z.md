**1. Claim Audit**
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:204`: “Main conditional reduction.” Not established as stated. There is no proof following the proposition, and the claimed conclusion requires an explicit statement that every rational Hodge class lies in the image/fixed part of `\Pi_\sigma`. Lemma `\ref{lem:rat}` only addresses fixed coefficients, not the projector image on `H^{p,p}(X,\Q(\varphi))`, and `\Pi_\sigma` is never defined.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:174`: Definition of `H_{4a}^{alg}` effectively assumes the algebraicity needed on `X`. The later “reduction” is therefore close to tautological unless the paper proves `H_{4a}^{alg}/\Omega => H_{4a}^{alg}/X` for all relevant classes. It does not.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:195`: “The implication `H_{4a}^{alg}/\Omega => H_{4a}^{alg}/X` holds whenever `\iota_X` pulls back algebraic cycles...” This is incomplete. Pullback of algebraic cycles is not enough; one also needs that the relevant `X`-classes are pullbacks of the relevant `\Omega`-classes, or that `\Pi_\sigma` is functorially compatible and surjective onto the fixed part used on `X`.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:346`: “The cascade closure functional `F` is `\sigma`-invariant.” Stated as a theorem with no proof and no citation. If this is imported from `CascadeFoundations`, cite it; otherwise it is unsupported.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:355`: “`\sigma` ... commuting with cup product and the Hodge decomposition.” The coefficient-action part is plausible. The Hodge-decomposition statement needs a precise definition of `H^{p,q}(X,\Z[\varphi])`; Hodge decomposition lives over `\C`, not integrally. As written, this is informal and under-defined.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:382`: “The subspace of `H^*(X,\Z[\varphi])` fixed by `\sigma` is exactly `H^*(X,\Z)`.” The proof assumes `H^*(X,\Z[\varphi]) = H^*(X,\Z)\otimes_\Z \Z[\varphi]` and a basis expansion. If `C_w \cong \C^{\ell(w)}` has complex dimension `\ell(w)`, its closure’s Poincaré dual lies in degree `2(\dim G/P-\ell(w))`, not `2\ell(w)`, unless opposite cells or codimension indexing is intended.

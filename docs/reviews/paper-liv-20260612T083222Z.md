**1. Claim Audit**

- L34: “All computational claims verified…” Verified for the script output. Misleading: the 14 includes `YM1--YM2`, which are not claims of this paper.

- L51: “only the 3- and 7-sphere support the parallelisable frame structure…” Overstated. `docs/rung-dimension-ladder.md` states Adams gives `S^0,S^1,S^3,S^7`; it then narrows to renderable `1,3,7` arenas. This paper suppresses the `S^1` qualification.

- L64-L80, Theorem “unique survivor”: conclusion is true, but proof is not adequate. L83 says `2I` is maximal because “order would exceed 120; the classification has none.” False as written: the classification has cyclic/binary-dihedral groups of arbitrarily large order. The missing argument is that no cyclic/dihedral/tetrahedral/octahedral group can contain `2I`, e.g. by quotient/subgroup structure. Also `2I/{±1}=A5` alone does not prove `2I` perfect; it only proves the commutator maps onto `A5`. One must show the central `-1` is also a commutator, or cite `2I \cong SL(2,5)` is perfect.

- L84: “CF2 generates the commutator subgroup from random commutators…” This is only a deterministic numerical witness with a fixed random sample. It is not a clean proof unless the sampled commutators are printed/certified.

- L87-L89, “The crystal is its own symmetry group”: established only relative to the correctness of `scripts/600cell_data.npz`. The script verifies closure of that stored vertex set; it does not independently prove the file is the unit-radius 600-cell.

- L91: “coarse gravity rung … fine quantum rung” is not established here. CF5 verifies a `2T` subgroup; the physics-rung interpretation is imported programme language.

- L93-L95, extension witness: the prose says it “closes under multiplication with group elements and itself.” The script does not do full closure; it multiplies by only a stride sample of group elements plus the outside element. The “unboundedly” language is also too strong for four rounds.

- L103-L107, Burnside-Dixon theorem: computationally supported, but no proof is given in the paper. The claim relies on the Burnside-Dixon theorem and floating eigenvectors. “No representation theory assumed” is inaccurate; better: no representation matrices are input.

- L109-L115, McKay graph: supported by script as a computation of positive support/arm lengths. However the script does not explicitly check all entries of `A` are symmetric 0/1 with zero diagonal; it thresholds `A > 0` and then zeros the diagonal. Add that assertion or weaken the statement.

- L127-L135, spectral self-embedding: overclaimed. GE1 compares sorted rows of Gram matrices, not the full Gram matrix up to a single vertex permutation/global orthogonal transform. That verifies row-wise angle distributions, not “full metric geometry” or “every angle” as L37 and L141 claim.

- L133: “exactly” is stronger than the computation: GE3 checks `1e-9`. Exactness may follow analytically, but the paper does not prove it.

- L137-L139 proof: the assertion that the coordinate eigenspace is the first nonzero level is not proved analytically; it is delegated to GE0. That is acceptable if labelled computational, but not as a standalone proof.

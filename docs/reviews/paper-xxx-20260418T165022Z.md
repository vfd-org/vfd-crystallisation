**1. Claim Audit**

- "`Then ... the K\"ahler structure on $\Mcl$ ... is the Fubini--Study structure of Paper~XXI`" ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:553)).  
Verdict: not established. The proof’s critical step is line 561, where the Fisher metric of the configuration-space location family is identified with the pullback of simplex Fisher--Rao under the modulus-squared map. That bridge is nowhere proved, and it is not supplied by Paper XVIII or XXI. There is also an unaddressed dimension issue: Proposition 12 works on a \(d\)-parameter translation family in configuration space, while the real slice of \(\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\) has dimension \(N-1\). Without an explicit local chart and \(d=N-1\) identification, Corollary 13 is a leap.

- "`Then \(P\) is the Born measure`" in Theorem 16 ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:652)).  
Verdict: as a Gleason-style theorem, yes; as a closure-derived theorem, no. The proof from lines 680–700 is essentially Gleason plus the eigenstate-certainty normalisation. What is not established is that the VFD construction supplies the theorem’s full hypotheses on all of \(\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\): full \(U(N)\)-covariance, full frame-function non-contextuality, and universal completeness. The theorem statement should stop advertising those hypotheses as closure-derived.

- "`Then the restriction of \(P\) ... satisfies axiom (U2)`" ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:614)).  
Verdict: overstated. The proof gives a restricted, state-specific statement for complete localised-probe observers under Route C hypotheses. It does not recover Theorem 16’s global (U2), which quantifies over every orthonormal basis and every \(\Psi \in \Mcl=\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\).

- "`Then for this particular \(\Psi\) ... [the probabilities agree]`" ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:598)).  
Verdict: established, but only as stated: state-specific, completeness-dependent, and restricted to localised-probe observers sharing the relevant basin/probe. The proof is fine at that restricted level.

- "`Then, under all five hypotheses ... \(P(\Phi_i\mid\Ocal)=|c_i|^2+O(\text{overlap})\)`" ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:323)).  
Verdict: broadly established as a conditional sector-mass calculation, but only approximately and only with hidden structure. The proof needs the \(\phi_i\) to be orthonormal in the \(L^2(d\mu)\) sense and their supports to align with the integration sectors. Those assumptions are stronger than “supported on \(\Phi_i\)” as currently written.

- "`Sector-preserving unitaries induce sector-probability covariance`" ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:446)).  
Verdict: only conditionally established. The proof uses \(P_i(\Psi)=|c_i|^2\), i.e. the Route C coefficient formula, but the proposition does not explicitly import the Route C sampling/completeness machinery that makes that identification meaningful.

- "`Closure Hessian as the Fisher metric ... in the Laplace regime`" ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:495)).  
Verdict: established as a local Gaussian computation on a translated family. The paper is careful that this is not yet a metric on projective state space.

- "`\(Q\) ... satisfies axiom (U2) iff \(p=2\)`" ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:703)).  
Verdict: established. This is one of the cleaner arguments in the paper.

- "`For \(N=2\) ... the unique solution is the latitude function`" ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:737)).  
Verdict: unsupported in this draft. No proof or citation is given. As written it is a free-standing uniqueness claim.

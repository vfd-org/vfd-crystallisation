**2. Internal Consistency**

- The paper defines `T_E` on `H^1(E,Z[phi])` at lines 221-230, then later admits the construction only makes sense on an enlarged Hecke module at lines 391-407. This is not just exposition; it invalidates the earlier operator definition and termwise bound.
- `T_E := T_E(1)` is used before the analytic bridge defines any boundary value. Lines 221-230 should be formal or removed in favour of `T_E(s)`.
- `B_...` and `H_...` notation conflict: lines 569-571, 608, 655-668 use `H_BSD-Spec/Conv`, while the actual hypotheses are `B_BSD,conv/rank/spec`.
- Cross-references resolve syntactically but not semantically: `thm:BSD` is a proposition; `thm:specL` is a remark; `cor:rankEig` is a remark; `thm:cascES` is a remark.
- Line 605 points to Section `sec:T_E` for `T_E` being a polynomial in the 600-cell Laplacian `L`; that section contains no such statement.
- Lines 692-694 revert to “two bridge axioms,” contradicting the abstract and hypotheses, which list three.

**3. External Consistency**

- `CascadeFoundations`, lines 185-190 and 449-450: local foundations material supports closure vocabulary and a cutoff Hecke operator `T_E[h]`, but only with compact support and an open trace-matching hypothesis. It does not verify a BSD spectral identity.
- `verify_T_E_s_regularised.py`, lines 283-290: locally verified as a finite computation. It does not establish convergence at `s=1`; it reports truncated partial sums.
- `CascadeAttractorSpectrum`, lines 586-602: the spectrum and `94/120` count are supported by the RH formal source and scripts.
- `CascadeAttractorDynamics`, lines 615-639: the R1/R2/R3 numbers are supported, but the RH formal source explicitly notes R3 is not a true control because the projector dominates.
- `ConvergenceWithSmart` and `CascadeRHformal`, lines 574 and 668-672: both support only a descriptive bridge-pattern analogy, not a formal meta-theorem.
- `ariaH4Olaw`, lines 643-651: not verifiable in this repository. The paper correctly says it is external, but it should not be used as evidentiary support.

**1. Claim Audit**
- [115-120](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xviii/paper-xviii.tex:115>) “The probability density \(\rho\) and current velocity \(v\) satisfy \(\partial_t\rho+\nabla\cdot(\rho v)=0\).” The proof at [122-128](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xviii/paper-xviii.tex:122>) establishes exactly this, assuming the regularity needed to differentiate \(\rho\) and use the Fokker–Planck equation.
- [136-140](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xviii/paper-xviii.tex:136>) “\(v=\nabla S\), \(S=-\Fcal-\frac{\sigma^2}{2}\log\rho\).” Formally correct only on the region where \(\rho>0\). As stated it is too global: if \(\rho\) has zeros, \(\log\rho\) is undefined and \(S\) is not globally defined.
- [162-169](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xviii/paper-xviii.tex:162>) “The pair \((\rho,S)\) satisfies” the Madelung system. The algebra at [171-192](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xviii/paper-xviii.tex:171>) is fine, but the proof silently divides by \(\rho\) and \(R=\sqrt\rho\). You need explicit hypotheses \(\rho>0\), \(R\in C^2\), otherwise the statement is overstated.
- [220-229](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xviii/paper-xviii.tex:220>) “The Madelung pair is equivalent to the nonlinear wave equation.” Same problem: the “equivalence” uses the standard Madelung correspondence and \(\Delta R/R\). It is not globally true if nodes are allowed.
- [319-326](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xviii/paper-xviii.tex:319>) “At \(\rho=\rho_{\mathrm{st}}\), the nonlinear potential evaluates to the constant \(U[\Psi_{\mathrm{st}}]=\sigma^2\VW\).” False as written. \(\VW(\psi)\) is generally not constant. Worse, the computation only evaluates the equation on the equilibrium state; it does not prove an operator-level reduction on a class of states.
- [328-333](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xviii/paper-xviii.tex:328>) “For perturbations \(\Psi=\Psi_{\mathrm{st}}(1+\varepsilon\eta)\), the wave equation linearises, to leading order, to \(i\partial_t\Psi=H_W\Psi\).” The proof at [349-353](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xviii/paper-xviii.tex:349>) does not establish that. Showing \(U=\sigma^2\VW+O(\varepsilon)\) is not enough; the \(O(\varepsilon)\) term is the same order as the perturbation. You need the actual first-order equation for \(\eta\) or \(\delta\Psi\).
- [395](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xviii/paper-xviii.tex:395>) “spectrum \(E_n=nk\).” Correct for the shifted one-dimensional harmonic oscillator, but only if \(k>0\). That hypothesis is missing.
- [399-407](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xviii/paper-xviii.tex:399>) The oscillatory solutions and XVII comparison are fine only conditional on a repaired linearisation result. As it stands, the example outruns the proved general claim.
- [432-435](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xviii/paper-xviii.tex:432>) “exactly Schrödinger iff \(\sigma^4\Delta|\Psi_N|/|\Psi_N|\) is a function of \(\psi\) only, independent of \(t\).” Unsupported and too strong. Linearity requires state-independence, not necessarily time-independence.

**1. Claim Audit**

- `“Papers XVIII--XX establish an exact nonlinear paired-process wave equation … whose leading-order linearisation at that equilibrium coincides with the Schr\"odinger equation with Witten Hamiltonian \HW.”` (lines 140-142): basically fair, but only if you keep the word `leading-order` doing real work. The supporting papers give a formal perturbative reduction, not a rigorous linearisation theorem with a controlled remainder. You say that in the second sentence; good. Do not strengthen this anywhere else.

- `Proposition [Schr\"odinger as Hamiltonian flow on \mathbb{CP}^{N-1}]` (lines 208-218): substantively correct under the finite-dimensional invariance hypothesis, but the proof is only a citation sketch. It also slides between the ambient form `\omega` on `\mathcal H_O` and the descended form on projective space without stating the passage cleanly. The argument is acceptable only as “standard geometric QM, imported here”; it is not a self-contained proof.

- `Proposition [Induced projective action of U(N) preserves the K\"ahler structure]` (lines 222-228): the first half is fine. The converse is overstated as written. `PU(N)` is the full group of holomorphic/Kähler isometries of Fubini–Study; if you mean the full Riemannian isometry group, the statement needs qualification. The proof merely cites classification and does not police that distinction.

- `Corollary [Equilibrium-tangent Schr\"odinger flows sit inside U(N)]` (lines 230-236): the conclusion is standard, but your proof is incomplete. From `A=A^\dagger` you get that `e^{-iAt}` is unitary. That does not by itself show it is the Hamiltonian flow of `h_A`; for that you also need Proposition 208. As written, the proof does not establish the full claim.

- `“The equilibrium tangent limit … is therefore unitary on the chosen finite-dimensional sector-superposition subspace whenever Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:subspace_invariance} holds.”` (lines 322-323): correct, but only because you have restricted to a finite-dimensional invariant subspace. That restriction is load-bearing and should be repeated more aggressively in the abstract and conclusion.

- There are no numerical results in this paper.

**2. Internal Consistency**

- The worst contradiction is between line 123 / Table line 304 and lines 325-326. Earlier you say Paper XIX proved gauge non-removability for phase and linear-unitary transforms. Later you say the physical status of the residual remains open, including whether it is `“a gauge-removable structure.”` Those cannot both stand unqualified. If you mean “not removable by the standard transformations analysed so far, but perhaps removable by some more exotic reformulation,” then say exactly that.

- Definition 1 (lines 171-173) introduces `sector basis vectors` but never defines what makes them a basis in the Hilbert-space sense, nor whether they are orthonormal. Later you identify `U(\mathcal H_O)=U(N)` (line 223), which presupposes a standard finite-dimensional Hilbert-space structure. You need to state explicitly that an orthonormal basis of the `N`-dimensional complex Hilbert space has been fixed, or avoid basis language entirely.

- The paper is mostly careful about scope, but it oscillates between `formal/perturbative linearisation` (lines 141-142, 306) and much stronger prose like `“the Schr\"odinger equation appears … as the distinguished equilibrium tangent sector”` (line 155) and `“demonstrates a mathematically consistent pathway”` (line 385). Those are rhetorically broader than the technical caveats you yourself inserted.

- Cross-references appear internally coherent. I do not see an obvious broken `\ref`/`\eqref` by inspection.

**6. Top Three Fixes**

1. Fix the overstatement of what is proved about Schrödinger emergence. The central synthesis claim and conclusion (lines 140-155, 385-396) still read too much like a derivation theorem, when the underlying result is a formal leading-order equilibrium linearisation.

2. Repair the geometric section mathematically, not rhetorically. Lines 171-177 and 208-236 need explicit Hilbert-space hypotheses, a clear distinction between ambient and descended forms, a corrected isometry-group statement, and a complete proof of the corollary.

3. Remove the gauge-removability contradiction. Lines 123, 304, and 325 cannot all remain as written. Either Paper XIX ruled out the relevant gauge removals, or this paper should stop saying that gauge removability remains open without qualification.

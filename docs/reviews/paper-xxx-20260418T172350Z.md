**1. Claim Audit**
- “\emph{Conditional Born weighting from stationary sector measure}” (lines 323-341). The proof does establish the stated conditional claim, and the draft is now honest that sampling and state-completeness are extra assumptions. No over-claim remains here. The only caution is that assumption (3) is doing heavy work: sector-supported, normalised modes with negligible overlap are not supplied by XVIII, and the paper says so elsewhere.
- “\emph{Sector-preserving unitaries induce sector-probability covariance on sector-supported, state-complete states}” (lines 446-452). The proof establishes exactly that restricted covariance, not full \(U(N)\)-covariance. The paper is now explicit about this at lines 464-470. Acceptable.
- “\emph{Closure Hessian as the Fisher metric of a location-parameter family on configuration space, in the Laplace regime}” (lines 492-506). The proof establishes only a leading-order Gaussian/Laplace identity for a translated family of densities on configuration space. That is what is stated. Acceptable as a local claim, not as a projective-state claim.
- “\emph{Fubini--Study identification, assembled from standard geometric QM and a local closure-Hessian normalisation}” (lines 543-554). As written, the proposition is basically a bookkeeping statement: XXI gives the global Kähler structure up to scale; Proposition 492 gives a candidate local scale; the bridge theorem is missing. The proof supports that weaker packaged claim. It does not support any stronger statement that closure-side Fisher data fixes the projective metric globally.
- “\emph{Non-contextuality of sector probability for observers complete-for-\(\Psi\)}” (lines 593-599). The proof establishes this restricted statement, assuming the two contexts are realised by localised-probe observers sharing the canonical probe and both are complete for the same \(\Psi\). Acceptable.
- “\emph{Restricted (U2) for complete, localised-probe observers, with Route C hypotheses}” (lines 609-617). The proof establishes only restricted \(U2\), and the text now says so. Acceptable, but any later prose that drops the “restricted” qualifier is misleading.
- “\emph{Born weighting uniqueness under imported and hypothesised inputs, \(N\ge3\)}” (lines 655-675). The proof establishes the theorem once full global \(U2\), full \(U(N)\)-covariance, continuity, eigenstate certainty, and \(\Mcl=\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\) are assumed. What it does not establish is that VFD supplies those hypotheses, especially global \(U2\). The proof is Gleason doing the work; Petz/Hessian do not enter the proof. Any prose suggesting Route E itself “forces” Born from closure geometry is still too strong.
- “\emph{Exclusion of \(|c|^p\) for \(p\neq2\)}” (lines 706-712). The proof is correct.
- Two-basin toy model (lines 779-802). The numerical statements are fine because the asymmetric case is explicitly stipulated, not derived. The caveat is present and adequate.

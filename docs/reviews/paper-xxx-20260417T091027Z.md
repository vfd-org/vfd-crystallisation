**1. Claim Audit**
- Lines 290-304, “Conditional Born weighting from stationary sector measure.” The proof does not establish the stated claim. It proves only the trivial sector-mass identity under disjoint support: if `\Psi=\sum_i c_i\phi_i` and the `\phi_i` have effectively disjoint support, then sector integrals give `|c_i|^2` after normalisation. It does not use assumption 1 (`\rho\approx P_st`) and does not prove the measurement-sampling hypothesis from lines 273-279. This is not a derivation “from stationary sector measure”; it is a decomposition lemma plus an extra sampling postulate.
- Lines 406-410, “Sector-preserving unitaries induce sector-probability covariance.” The proposition is not well-posed as written. Its defining condition at lines 395-397 applies `T` to configuration-space sectors and applies `P_st` to Hilbert vectors. Those are type errors. The proof tries to repair this by invoking an “induced action on densities” at lines 414-423, but that induced action was never defined.
- Lines 437-446, “Chentsov…; Petz quantum extension.” No proof is given here. That is acceptable if honestly presented as imported, but then this theorem is not established by this manuscript.
- Lines 455-468, “Closure Hessian as the Fisher metric…” This is the one place where the proof matches the claim. It establishes a local, leading-order Laplace-regime identification `g_Fisher=(2/\sigma^2)H+O(\sigma^0)`. The hidden hypotheses are finite-dimensionality, non-degenerate Hessian, differentiability of `Z(\theta)`, and Gaussian/Laplace validity. Those should be stated explicitly in the proposition, not left implicit.
- Lines 506-513, “Fubini--Study identification, given Papers XVIII and XXI.” This is overstated. Assumption (K1) already assumes Paper XXI gives a global `U(N)`-invariant Kähler structure with Fubini--Study metric up to scale. The corollary then fixes only the local scale using Proposition 455. It does not derive Fubini--Study from closure.
- Lines 546-551, “Non-contextuality of sector probability.” Within the canonical-probe, localised-probe-observer subclass, the proof is fine. The last sentence, “Equivalently, `P` is a function of `(\Pi_1,\Psi)` alone,” is too broad. What is proved is context-independence only for contexts realised by observers sharing the same canonical probe.
- Lines 558-559, “Restricted (U2)…” The proof does not establish full axiom (U2) as later used. Non-contextuality is proved only on a restricted observer class; normalisation is imported from basin disjointness plus the Route C sector-supported decomposition, which is an extra assumption not stated in the corollary.
- Lines 590-609, “Born weighting uniqueness from closure, `N\ge3`.” The proof is just Gleason plus `U(1)`/`U(N)` symmetry and eigenstate certainty. It does not use the closure Hessian, Corollary 506, or any specifically “closure” machinery except by importing hypotheses from elsewhere. Worse, the theorem silently strengthens the space from `\M_cl\subseteq \mathbb{CP}^{N-1}` to `\M_cl=\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}`. Without that equality, the orbit argument in Step 1 and the frame-function argument in Step 2 do not go through.
- Lines 635-640, “Exclusion of `|c|^p` for `p\neq2`.” The proof is correct for the specific basis-normalised family `Q` defined there. It does not exclude every possible non-quadratic law; only that family.
- Lines 669-670, the `N=2` remark asserts uniqueness under added `SU(2)`-invariance hypotheses but gives no proof. As a remark this is survivable, but it is not established here.
- Lines 715-730, toy-model arithmetic. Correctly labelled schematic. It does not support any dynamical claim beyond “if you stipulate a sector-supported asymmetric state, the integrals look like Born weights.”

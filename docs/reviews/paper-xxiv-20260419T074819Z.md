All five advertised Round-1 fixes are present in the TeX. Two did not land cleanly: the rewritten dilation theorem now has an undefined order symbol, and the rewritten simultaneity proof introduces a bad cross-paper citation.

**1. Claim Audit**
- **[97-101]** “For any observer \(O\): \(e_1 \prec e_2 \Rightarrow t_O(e_1) < t_O(e_2)\).” The proof at **[104-106]** establishes exactly this. No objection.
- **[116-120]** “If \(e_1,e_2\) are incomparable, then there exist observers \(O_1,O_2\)” reversing their temporal order. The proof at **[123-125]** is essentially sound: the transitive-closure/Szpilrajn route does establish the claim on a finite poset. The defect is not the mathematics but the attribution at **[124]**: “Paper XXIII Theorem 2” is not a verifiable citation.
- **[142-156]** “Time-dilation analogue.” The proof at **[158-164]** establishes only the narrow algebraic statement: if two observers share one total order and the sums of their weights over the same order interval differ, then the resulting **global clock intervals** \(\Delta t_O=t_O(e_n)-t_O(e_1)\) differ. It does **not** establish the stronger rhetoric used elsewhere that two observers assign different durations “along the same chain” in any intrinsic sense, because \(\Delta t_O\) was defined at **[135-139]** to include **all** events between the endpoints in the total order, including off-chain events.
- **[247]** The simultaneity arithmetic in the pentagon example is correct and does instantiate the theorem.
- **[253-257]** The elapsed-time arithmetic for \(O_1\) and \(O_3\) is correct and does instantiate the theorem as stated.
- **[261-264]** The \(\tau\)-based calculation \(\rho(O_1,O_3;C)=2/3\) and the global ratio \(5/9\) are arithmetically correct. The final sentence at **[264]** is not: “the two coincide only when the chain saturates \(S\)” is false. Equality of the ratios can occur accidentally even with off-chain events present.

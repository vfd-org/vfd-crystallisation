**1. Claim Audit**

All line numbers below refer to [paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex).

- `"Conditional Born weighting from stationary sector measure"` (lines 323-339): only partly established. The proof at lines 341-343 does show a sector-mass decomposition under disjoint support plus the explicit sampling postulate. It does **not** derive measurement statistics from closure dynamics. The manuscript knows this, but lines 353-360 and 852 still slide toward “inherited from the stationary measure” language that is stronger than what Proposition `\ref{prop:born}` proves.

- `"Sector-preserving unitaries induce sector-probability covariance"` (lines 444-449): not established as stated. The proof at lines 451-463 works only for sector-supported states with a coefficient expansion `\Psi=\sum_j c_j\phi_j` and with the identification `P_i(\Psi)=|c_i|^2`. The statement quantifies over all `\Psi \in \Mcl(\Ocal)` and says nothing about `T\Psi \in \Mcl(\Ocal)`. Both are hidden hypotheses.

- `"Chentsov 1972; Petz quantum extension"` (lines 475-485): not proved here; acceptable only as imported standard material. But Part (2) is stated in a form stronger and cleaner than the paper’s later use actually needs, and the citation trail is not obviously the exact theorem stated.

- `"Closure Hessian as the Fisher metric ... in the Laplace regime"` (lines 493-506): the local Gaussian computation is basically fine, but the object being studied is a **translated location family**, not literally the stationary family of one fixed closure dynamics. The proof supports a local Fisher/Hessian identification for that model family. It does not support any direct claim on `\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}`.

- `"Fubini--Study identification, given Papers XVIII and XXI"` (lines 544-552): not established. The critical sentence in (K3), lines 549-550, simply asserts that the configuration-space Fisher metric “pulls back” to a real-slice normalisation on `\Mcl`. That map is not constructed. The proof at lines 554-556 then assumes that one tangent-direction normalisation at one point is enough to identify the global scale. Even if that homogeneous-space statement is granted, the prior step connecting the Hessian calculation to that tangent direction is missing.

- `"Non-contextuality of sector probability for complete observers"` (lines 584-590): conditionally acceptable, but only under substantial unproved identification assumptions. The proof at lines 592-594 uses basin locality plus LMC correctly. What is not shown is that two orthonormal bases of `\Hcal_\Ocal` genuinely correspond to the two localised-probe observers in the way the theorem assumes.

- `"Restricted (U2) for complete, localised-probe observers"` (lines 600-602): not established as stated. The proof at lines 604-605 uses basin disjointness **and sector-supported decomposition of `\Psi`**, neither of which appears in the statement.

- `"Born weighting uniqueness from closure, N \ge 3"` (lines 632-652): the proof at lines 660-680 does establish the stated conclusion from the stated axioms. But the title is inflated. This is Gleason plus imported assumptions `(U1)-(U4)` plus the strong support hypothesis `\Mcl=\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}`. The closure dynamics do no work inside the proof.

- `"Exclusion of |c|^p for p \neq 2"` (lines 683-689): established. The counterexample at lines 694-714 is clean.

- `"Two-dimensional case, N=2"` (lines 717-718): not proved, only asserted in a remark. If the paper wants to use it, it needs either a proof or a citation.

- `"Symmetric case ... =1/2"` and `"Asymmetric case ... \approx |a|^2, |b|^2"` in the toy model (lines 760-778): fine as a toy calculation, and the manuscript properly says the asymmetry is put in by hand.

**2. Internal Consistency**

- The paper conflates **statewise completeness** and **universal completeness**. Lines 153-154 distinguish them correctly. Line 196 then says statewise completeness is “equivalently” `\Mcl(\Ocal)=\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}` restricted to the `N`-dimensional sector space. That is the stronger, universal notion.

- The formulas `P=|c_i|^2` for complete and partial observers are used too loosely. Lines 200 and 206 present them as consequences of completeness/partiality alone. Lines 181 and 936 later admit that the explicit amplitude formula requires the sector-supported decomposition regime. Those statements are not consistent.

- `\Mcl(\Ocal)` is defined by a support condition on `\rho_\Psi` (lines 413-418), but Proposition `\ref{prop:suff}` treats every `\Psi\in\Mcl(\Ocal)` as if it had the Route C sector decomposition and as if `T\Psi` stayed in `\Mcl(\Ocal)` (lines 444-463). That is a change of regime without notice.

- Corollary `\ref{cor:U2_derived}` claims to derive full `(U2)` for the restricted observer class (lines 600-602), but its proof silently imports sector decomposition and basin disjointness (lines 604-605). The theorem statement and proof are misaligned.

- Cross-references in the source appear mechanically consistent: the `\ref{...}` labels used in this file are all defined. I did not find an obvious unresolved internal `\ref`.

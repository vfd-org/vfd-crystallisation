**1. Claim Audit**

Line numbers refer to `release-bundles/icosian-triad-v600/papers/01-icosian-triad/icosian-triad.tex`.

- `53–65`: “complete geometry-first derivation”; “scheme is P-polynomial in A1.” Not established. The association-scheme checks are partly floating-point, and the P-polynomial claim remains incompatible with the failed `L_1` tridiagonal simulation and with the stated off-band products at `330–333`.

- `69–76`: universal surjectivity and `r(pi)=8(1+N_Q(pi))` for every odd prime. Not established. The paper later proves only finite witness searches and finite counts.

- `89–98`: multiplicativity and L-function identity. Not established. Multiplicativity is tested on six examples; the Euler product also requires prime-power local factors, not just prime values and coprime products.

- `274–287`, Theorem “Association scheme.” Partly established, but not at the exact level stated. The proof at `289–292` admits floating-point tolerance `10^{-9}`. Exact Bose-Mesner and Krein claims require exact certificates, not rounded spectral projectors.

- `297–320`, Theorem “Spectrum.” Spectrum/multiplicities are plausible computational facts, but the proof at `322–327` is floating-point. The claim “each `A_i` is a polynomial of degree exactly `i` in `A_1`” is not supported; the committed `p_polynomial_results.json` gives degree `8` already for `A_2`. This is a blocker.

- `329–337`, Remark “not narrow DRG.” This is honest, but it conflicts with standard P-polynomial terminology. In an association scheme, P-polynomiality normally forces the relevant three-term/tridiagonal structure. The paper must either prove a nonstandard definition explicitly or stop using the term.

- `354–365`, Closure spectrum. The scalar action of `C_phi` follows formally. But `363` misuses `mu_j^sigma`: Galois conjugation sends the scalar on `E_j` to the scalar on the paired eigenspace when `lambda_j` is not fixed, not generally to `(12+phi^2)-lambda_j` on the same `E_j`.

- `390–396`, Universal representation. As written, internally inconsistent: it says `N_Q(pi) <= 50`, but the listed inert primes `13,17,23,37,43,47` have field norms `169,289,529,1369,1849,2209`. The proof establishes only listed finite witnesses, not universality.

- `405–407`: “classical (Eichler--Hijikata).” This is not cited with a theorem, page, or exact statement. The bibliography entries exist, but the claim is not properly anchored.

- `438–458`, sigma-pairing. The eigenvalue pairing is established at the spectral-value level. The stronger language that eigenspaces are “sigma-invariant” as modules needs a defined integral model and sigma action on eigenvectors. The paper explicitly says coordinate sigma does not preserve the chosen `V_600` embedding at `424–433`.

- `475–478`, Antipodal pairing. No proof is supplied after the proposition. A simulation label is not a proof; add an exact verification or derive it from the eigenmatrix.

- `483–500`, Triad isomorphism. The identity `N_H(sigmahat(q)) = sigma(N_H(q))` follows algebraically if the coordinate model is correct. The prime-class consequences at `490–499` do not follow from that identity alone; they also need the representation theorem.

- `518–527`, representation count. The theorem states a universal formula for every odd prime. The proof at `529–533` checks twenty examples. This is the largest over-claim.

- `538–545`, multiplicativity. Universal multiplicativity is not proved. Six examples at `548–553` are evidence only.

- `576–585`, L-function identification. Not proved. The argument at `588–593` incorrectly jumps from prime values plus coprime multiplicativity to all ideals. Prime powers are missing. The claimed “explicit local-2 correction” is not explicit, and `r(2)` alone does not determine the full 2-adic Euler factor.

- `609–617`, Euler bridge corollary. Formally follows only if the L-function theorem is fixed. At present it inherits the same gap.

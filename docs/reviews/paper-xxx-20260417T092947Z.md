**1. Claim audit**

- “Conditional Born weighting from stationary sector measure” ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:291), lines 291-306): only partially established. The proof does show the sector-mass decomposition under disjoint-support assumptions, but the passage from sector mass to measurement probability is simply assumed in hypothesis (4). So this is not a derivation of measurement statistics from the dynamics; it is a decomposition lemma plus a measurement postulate. The title of the proposition oversells what is proved.

**2. Internal consistency**

- The manuscript uses two incompatible probability definitions. Equation (1) at lines 146-149 and equation (18) at lines 554-556 define `P` as an unnormalized sector mass. Proposition 291 at lines 297-305 defines probability as a normalized conditional mass over the admissible set. These are not the same object. This infects Theorem 552, Corollary 564, and the Route E use of non-contextuality.

- `\Mcl(\Ocal)` depends on the observer by definition (lines 381-386), but Theorem 552 speaks of two different observers and then says “for all `\Psi\in\Mcl`” (line 557) with no observer subscript. That is ill-posed.

- Route C is carefully approximate; Route E repeatedly treats its output as exact. Compare lines 301-305 with lines 413-430 and 569. The manuscript never fixes whether the sector-probability identification is exact, approximate, or only valid in a limit.

- The paper says Route E “builds the Kähler structure on the admissible manifold from closure primitives plus prior-paper inputs” (line 206), but later admits the global Kähler structure is simply imported from Paper XXI (lines 526-532). The honest version is the later one.

- Static label checking looks fine: every `\ref`/`\eqref` target in this file appears to exist. I did not compile the file, so this is a source-level check only.

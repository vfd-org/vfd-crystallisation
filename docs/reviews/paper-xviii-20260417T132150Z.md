**1. Claim Audit**

1. Lines 115-119: “`The probability density ρ and current velocity v satisfy: ∂_tρ + ∇·(ρv)=0.`”  
The proof does establish this, but only at the same level of regularity as the Fokker-Planck equation already assumes. No issue beyond unstated smoothness.

2. Lines 136-140: “`the current velocity v ... is a gradient field: v = ∇S`” with `S = -F - (σ²/2) log ρ`.  
Locally on `{ρ>0}`, yes. The proof is fine because `S` is written down explicitly. What is not proved is the stronger commentary in line 144 that nodal sets are “genuine obstructions to global gradient-field representation”; the paper does not analyse topology or weak continuation across zeros.

3. Lines 162-168: “`The pair (ρ,S) satisfies ...`” the closure Madelung system.  
The algebra is fine, but the stated claim is too global. The proof divides by `ρ` and by `R = √ρ`, so the proposition needs hypotheses such as `ρ>0` and enough regularity (`ρ, F` at least twice differentiable where used). As stated, it fails on nodal sets.

4. Lines 220-228: “`The Madelung pair ... is equivalent to the nonlinear wave equation`” with `U[Ψ_N] = -σ²V_W + σ⁴ Δ|Ψ_N|/|Ψ_N|`.  
Not as stated. The proof gives equivalence only where `|Ψ_N|=R>0`. At zeros of `Ψ_N`, the potential is singular and the equation is not even defined. This is a missing hypothesis, not a cosmetic point.

5. Lines 319-333: “`The closure-Nelson wave equation admits a Schrödinger regime in two stages`”.  
Stage 1 is acceptable only in the narrow sense used in the proof: when evaluated on the equilibrium solution itself. It does not show that the exact nonlinear equation becomes a linear Schrödinger equation on any neighborhood of equilibrium.  
Stage 2 is not proved as stated. The proof shows only `U[Ψ]=σ²V_W+O(ε)` (lines 349-353). That is not a proper linearisation of the PDE. You never derive the equation for `η`, never compute the Fréchet derivative of the nonlinear term, and never control the remainder. “`reduces formally`” in line 332 is the honest status; the proposition title and downstream prose oversell it.

6. Lines 395-403: “`This is a shifted quantum harmonic oscillator with spectrum E_n = nk`” and “`Excited states oscillate`”.  
The spectral claim for `H_W` is fine. The dynamical claim inherits the same weakness as Proposition 5: it is valid for the linearised equation, not for the exact nonlinear closure-Nelson equation.

7. Lines 432-435: “`The closure-Nelson wave equation is exactly Schrödinger ... iff σ⁴ Δ|Ψ_N|/|Ψ_N| = function of ψ only, independent of t`.”  
This is asserted without proof, and it is imprecise. At best it is a condition along a given solution trajectory. “State-independent potential” for the equation is stronger than “this solution happens to make the nonlinear term time-independent.”

**6. Top Three Fixes**

1. Rewrite Proposition 5 and every downstream summary that depends on it (lines 319-353, 45-47, 328-333, 356-364, 568-576).  
The current text does not prove the near-equilibrium Schrödinger claim in the form stated. Either do the actual linearisation around `Ψ_st` and derive the equation for `η`, or weaken the claim everywhere to a formal leading-order expansion.

Without `ρ>0` and sufficient smoothness, the central equations are not well-defined globally. Right now the paper proves local identities on the nonvanishing region and markets them as exact global statements.

Undefined `\HW` is a compile-breaking error. The wrong section pointer at lines 451-452 and the weak citation at line 326 make the paper look less controlled than it needs to.

**1. Claim Audit**
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:44`: “The construction is conditional on two hypotheses.” False relative to the body. The body requires three bridge hypotheses: convergence, rank, and spectrum. The abstract omits `B_{\rm BSD,rank}` as a separate open input.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:58`: “Both hypotheses ultimately reduce to classical Hecke-operator theory … combined with Kolyvagin and Gross-Zagier.” Not established. No reduction is proved; the body later says the bridges are open and at least as strong as BSD. This is an over-claim.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:87`: “`T_E`'s convergence theorem … and spectral identification … together are a rung-crossing operator.” Overstated. The theorem only gives convergence for `Re(s)>1`; the BSD-relevant operator at `s=1` remains hypothetical.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:136`: Proposition “Formal consequence of the cascade BSD bridge axiom.” The proof establishes only a tautology once the needed bridge hypotheses are assumed. As stated, the proposition names only rank and spectral axioms in its first sentence, while convergence at `s=1` is also required to define `T_E`.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:155`: “Its conditions are equivalent … to the classical Kolyvagin and Gross-Zagier input.” Not proved. Also misleading for rank `>=2`, where those results do not give BSD. Replace “equivalent” by “intended to parallel, but not shown equivalent to.”
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:200`: “For each prime `p` … the classical Hecke operator `T_p` acts on `H^1(E,\mathbb Z)`.” This is not the standard formulation. Hecke operators act naturally on modular curves/Jacobians/newform spaces; the later text itself admits the action on `H^1(E)` is too small and degenerates. The construction’s base space is therefore inconsistent.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:218`: Proposition “Termwise operator bound” with `≤ 2 log p`. The proof does not prove the stated constant. It explicitly obtains `2\varphi \log p` at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:232`, then says the constant is “absorbed” into `≤ 2 log p`, which is invalid.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:230`: “On the complement … eigenvalues bounded by `2\sqrt p`.” Even if a complementary Hecke module is intended, the eigenvalues of `T_p-a_p(E)` are differences of Hecke eigenvalues, so the naive bound is `≤4\sqrt p` unless additional structure is specified.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:250`: Theorem “Absolute convergence on `Re(s)>1`.” The conclusion is essentially salvageable after correcting constants and the operator space. The proof’s statement at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:265` that `\sum_p \log p\,p^{-s}` “equals `-\zeta'(s)/\zeta(s)`” is false; the latter includes prime powers.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:268`: Computational verification for `E:y^2=x^3-x`. The Hasse and CM checks are locally supported by `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/scripts/verify_T_E_s_regularised.py`. The quoted bounds are misleading: they are truncated finite-prime quantities, and the `Re(s)=1.0` line is exactly the boundary where the full comparison series diverges.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:274`: “`\|T_E(s)\|\le 10.5\varphi` at `Re(s)=1.0`.” Not a bound for the infinite operator series. It is a finite truncation from primes `p≤1000`.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:275`: “decreasing to `0.32\varphi` at `Re(s)=2.0`.” This does not match the theorem’s displayed `2\varphi` bound; the local script reports a Hasse upper value about `0.6376` before multiplying by `\varphi`, while `0.32` is the un-doubled prime sum.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:315`: Hypothesis `B_{\rm BSD,conv}` allows “at most a simple pole at `s=1`” but later uses `T_E:=T_E(1)`. If there is a pole, `T_E(1)` is not an operator without a finite-part prescription. This is a missing hypothesis.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:323`: Hypothesis `B_{\rm BSD,rank}` is an assumption, not a consequence of the construction. This is honestly labelled, but it conflicts with the earlier definition of `T_E` on `H^1(E,\mathbb Z[\varphi])`.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:332`: Hypothesis `B_{\rm BSD,spec}` is under-specified. “Multiplicity of `0` in `spec(T_E)`” could mean algebraic, geometric, or spectral multiplicity. The proof later needs equality with `\dim\ker T_E`; this requires semisimplicity or a definition.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:343`: “Classical Eichler-Shimura” is misstated. Eichler-Shimura is naturally about `H^1(X_0(N))`, modular symbols, or Jacobians/newform quotients, not a direct canonical isomorphism `H^1(E,\mathbb Z)\otimes\mathbb Q \cong S_2(\Gamma_0(N))^{f_E}\oplus\overline{...}` as written.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:397`: Corollary “Conditional rank-kernel formula.” This is only a restatement of Hypothesis `B_{\rm BSD,rank}`. It is acceptable if labelled “Restatement,” but it should not be presented as a corollary with independent content.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:422`: Remark `T_E` spectrum to `L`-function. Properly says unproved, but the displayed identity includes `\mathrm{mult}(0,\mathrm{spec}(T_E))=\dim\ker T_E`; that equality is not in Hypothesis `B_{\rm BSD,spec}` unless multiplicity is defined geometrically.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:454`: “Proof of Theorem `\ref{thm:BSD}`.” The label points to a proposition, not a theorem. More seriously, `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:460` invokes “Theorem `\ref{thm:specL}`,” but that label is attached to a remark explicitly saying the statement is not proved.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:475`: Refined BSD formula. The formula is standard, and the paper correctly withdraws the claim. The sentence at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:492` about “rank 0 for CM curves” is too vague; specify exactly which theorem/cases are meant or remove.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:498`: Sha finiteness withdrawal is appropriate. The critique of the earlier argument is sound.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:526`: `L(E,1)\approx0.6555`. Plausible for `32a` but no local verification is supplied in the paper. It is a numerical claim without a cited computation.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:528`: “`ord_{s=1} L(E,1)=0`.” Ill-formed notation. The order is of the function `L(E,s)` at `s=1`, not of the number `L(E,1)`.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:543`: Rank-1 example `37a1`. The stated classical facts are standard, but again no local computation or citation is supplied.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:571`: 600-cell Laplacian spectrum. Verified locally against `papers/cascade-derivation/scripts/test_sigma_attractor_spectrum.py`; the script reports the same multiplicities and `94/120=78.3%`.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:589`: “if `T_E` is a polynomial in `L`.” This is a new, major hidden assumption. Nothing earlier defines such a polynomial relationship between the BSD Hecke operator and the 600-cell Laplacian.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:593`: “the `78.3%` count transfers to spectral support of `H_{\mathrm{BSD-Spec}}`.” Too strong. The transfer is precisely the missing bridge, not evidence for it.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:600`: Finite-noise dynamical diagnostic. The cited script implements the stated experiment, but the R2/R3 `1.0000` result is forced by an explicit projection onto the fixed subspace. It is not independent evidence for BSD.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:628`: Aria H4O empirical witness. I cannot verify the cited files locally: `aria-chess/docs/cascade-h4-oscillator-law.md` and `aria-chess/run_artifacts/h4_lyapunov_spectrum.json` are not present at the cited paths. A similarly named local file exists elsewhere, but it is not the cited artifact.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:663`: “Classical BSD is proved for analytic rank `≤1`.” Needs precision: the rank equality and Sha finiteness in analytic rank `0,1` are known under the usual modular elliptic curve hypotheses; the full leading coefficient formula is a separate matter.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:677`: “The two bridge axioms … remain open.” This contradicts the body’s three-hypothesis structure and omits `B_{\rm BSD,conv}`.

**2. Internal Consistency**
- The abstract says “two hypotheses” at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:44`; the body requires three at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:307` and `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:667`.
- The paper alternates between `H_{\text{BSD-Conv}}`, `H_{\text{BSD-Spec}}`, `B_{\rm BSD,conv}`, `B_{\rm BSD,rank}`, `B_{\rm BSD,spec}`, and `H_{\mathrm{BSD-Spec}}`. This is not merely cosmetic; it obscures which assumptions are open inputs.
- `T_E` is defined on `H^1(E,\mathbb Z[\varphi])` at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:206`, but later the paper admits that this space is too small and that the operator must act on an enlarged Hecke module at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:376`.
- `B_{\rm BSD,conv}` references `Definition~\ref{def:TE}` at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:317`, but the operator-valued series in `s` is `Definition~\ref{def:T_E_s}`.
- Cross-references resolve syntactically but misrepresent theorem status: `thm:BSD` is a proposition, `thm:specL` is a remark, `thm:cascES` is a remark, and `cor:rankEig` is a remark.
- The proof at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:460` cites an explicitly unproved remark as a theorem.
- The spectral multiplicity bridge at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:332` does not define multiplicity in the way required by the proof at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:462`.
- The discussion says the “two bridge axioms” remain open at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:677`, contradicting the three-axiom dependence stated earlier.
- `\bm` is used at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:583` and `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:586` without loading `bm`.
- The Unicode proof symbol `∎` at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:265` is redundant inside an `amsthm` proof and may break under some LaTeX engines.

**1. Claim Audit**
- Lines 171-197, especially lines 184-192: the paper’s central “template” does not establish sector separation in any meaningful mathematical sense. It assumes (ii) every inter-minimum path crosses the mismatch set and (iii) a positive gap on that set, then deduces the barrier bound `\Delta\Fcal_{i\to j}\ge \beta_{ij}`. That proof is fine as a conditional lemma. What it does **not** establish is any mechanism by which measurement coupling generates the required topology or gap. Given the title, Section 7, and the conclusion, that remains an over-claim in presentation, even if partly caveated in the body.
- Lines 373-374: “What is established” still overstates. The quoted bullet
  “Barrier height would suppress inter-basin transitions exponentially … once the three Paper XIV import hypotheses … are imposed”
  is not what this paper establishes. At best, it records that an external Paper XIV small-noise asymptotic could then be imported. It also omits the needed large-barrier condition `\Delta\Fcal_{i\to j}\gg \sigma^2`; without that, “suppress” is too strong.

The first source is conditional dynamical metastability. The second source, from Paper XXIX, is not dynamical stability at all; it is an admissibility filter. That can affect what counts as an admissible registered outcome, but it does not by itself increase residence times or produce Kramers suppression.

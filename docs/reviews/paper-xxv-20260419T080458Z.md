**1. Claim Audit**
- “For comparable events … a chain-length separation counts the minimum number of cover steps between them” ([paper-xxv.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxv/paper-xxv.tex:41)) is not what the paper defines. Definition 3.1 defines
  `d_chain(e_1,e_2)=min{|C|-1 : C is a chain from e_1 to e_2}` at lines 96-101. On that definition, every distinct comparable pair has `d_chain=1`, because `{e_1,e_2}` is itself a chain. Proposition 3.2 ([paper-xxv.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxv/paper-xxv.tex:108)) proves properties of this degenerate quantity, not of “minimum number of cover steps.” This is the main mathematical defect.
- Proposition 3.2 itself ([paper-xxv.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxv/paper-xxv.tex:108)) is formally correct for the quantity actually defined, but that quantity is not the one advertised in the abstract, remarks, or later comparisons. Hidden assumption: existence of a finite chain between comparable events. In this project that comes from finiteness of the event set, but the section does not state it.
- “`d_max` is well-defined for comparable pairs” ([paper-xxv.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxv/paper-xxv.tex:133)) also relies on finiteness/bounded interval size. No hypothesis is stated. The causal-set attribution at lines 128-133 has no supporting citation.
- Definition 4.1 of `sigma` ([paper-xxv.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxv/paper-xxv.tex:142)) is not well-defined as written. “Let `\Ocal` be a family of observers” is not enough: if `\Ocal` is infinite, `Var_{O\in\Ocal}` needs a probability measure; if finite, the paper must say whether this is population variance or sample variance. Since observers in XXIV include arbitrary positive weight functions, the observer class is uncountable.
- Proposition 4.2, parts (1) and (2), are fine once one imports XXIV’s observer-time definition and order-preservation result. Part (3) is only an existence statement about some observers, and the proof supports exactly that. But the concluding sentence at line 160 does not follow from the proposition: `sigma(e_1,e_2)` depends on the chosen `\Ocal`, and part (3) never says the sign-reversing observers lie in that family.
- The stronger interpretive claim “zero only for identical events” at lines 167-168 is false. A singleton observer family gives variance zero for every pair. Even with multiple observers, comparable pairs can have equal time differences across the chosen family.
- Theorem 5.1 ([paper-xxv.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxv/paper-xxv.tex:189)) is standard and the proof is adequate, but only after fixing the definition domain. Definition 5.1 says “For any `e_1,e_2\in\Ecal`, define…” ([paper-xxv.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxv/paper-xxv.tex:181)), which is false unless the graph is connected or one restricts to a connected component. The theorem is fine; the preceding definition is not.
- The remark “for comparable events … `d_trans(e_1,e_2) \le d_chain(e_1,e_2)` (since any chain gives a path in the Hasse graph)” ([paper-xxv.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxv/paper-xxv.tex:206)) is not proved and, under the paper’s current definition of chain, the parenthetical justification is simply wrong. An arbitrary chain does not give a Hasse-edge path unless consecutive elements are cover-related.
- Proposition 6.1 ([paper-xxv.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxv/paper-xxv.tex:240)) is acceptable given XXIV’s time definition, strict positivity of weights, and finiteness. But it inherits the `d_chain` problem: as written it is a bound in terms of the degenerate “1 for any distinct comparable pair” quantity, not the promised cover-step count.
- The worked-example numerical claims at lines 262-312 are not established for the cited dual-pentagon system, because the event labels and layers do not match XXIII/XXIV. Those computations may be correct for a different two-layer toy model, but not for “the dual-pentagon event set from Papers XXIII--XXIV.”

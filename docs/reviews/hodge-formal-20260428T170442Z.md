**1. Claim Audit**
- “`H_{4a}^{alg}/Ω => H_{4a}^{alg}/X` holds whenever `\iota_X` pulls back algebraic cycles” (`hodge-formal.tex:186`). Not established. Pullback preserving algebraic cycles is not enough; you also need every relevant `σ`-fixed class on `X` to be a pullback from `Ω`, or you must assume the `/X` statement directly.
- “Main conditional reduction” (`195-209`). No proof is given at the statement site. Read as `H_{4a}^{alg}/X`, it is essentially the missing Hodge algebraicity assertion plus the elementary fixed-part identity. The “via pullback” phrase is unsupported.
- “The cascade closure functional `F` is `σ`-invariant” (`331-333`). Unsupported as stated. The local F5 source has hypotheses/supersession caveats; this theorem has neither proof nor citation.
- “`σ` action on cohomology” (`340-349`). Coefficientwise action is fine over `Q(φ)`. The integral `Z[φ]` and “commuting with Hodge decomposition” formulation is underdefined; Hodge decomposition is not a decomposition of `H^*(X,Z[φ])`.
- “`σ`-fixed classes are rational” (`367-392`). Correct over finite-dimensional `Q`-vector spaces, but this proof uses a basis-like tensor expansion for integral cohomology and ignores torsion/formal coefficient issues. Cite the sigma-rationality paper instead.
- “Cascade embedding of `CP^n`” (`398-420`). The proof does not prove the theorem. `CP^n` is not obtained as stated by iterated projective-bundle embeddings into an undefined cascade rung; for large `n` the target geometry/dimension is not specified.
- “Cohomology structure” for `CP^n` (`424-428`) and “Hodge for `CP^n`” (`435-455`). Established by the proof, modulo missing bibliography key `Hodge1941`.
- “Bruhat decomposition” (`471-478`). Needs hypotheses: connected complex reductive algebraic group and parabolic subgroup. The examples use compact real forms (`U(n)`) while the statement uses complex `G/P`.
- “Schubert classes span cohomology” (`483-485`). True in corrected form, but the degree convention is wrong: closures of cells of dimension `\ell(w)` give homology degree `2\ell(w)`, while cohomology degree is codimension-dependent unless opposite Schubert classes are used.
- “Hodge for flag varieties” (`489-509`). The conclusion is classical, but the proof inherits the Schubert degree error. Salvageable, not correct as written.
- “Danilov” theorem (`527-536`). False as stated: `rank H^{2p}` is not the number of `p`-dimensional cones in general. Orbit-closure classes generate with relations.
- “Hodge for toric” (`540-558`). The conclusion is fine for smooth projective toric varieties and essentially for smooth complete toric cohomology-as-cycles, but the proof relies on the false rank statement and the citation key is broken (`Danilov` vs `Danilov1978`).
- “For CM abelian varieties, every Hodge class is absolute Hodge” (`571-575`). Correct, but weaker than algebraicity.
- “Neither result implies the Hodge Conjecture for CM abelian varieties in general” (`587-592`). Correct, and it directly contradicts earlier and later “five classes where Hodge is known” language.
- “Selected complete intersections” (`634-664`). Overclaim. Weak Lefschetz handles non-middle cohomology; the primitive middle-degree argument is hand-waving. Noether-Lefschetz does not prove all primitive Hodge classes for all codimension `<=2` complete intersections.
- “Hodge for cascade-embeddable” (`676-702`). The proof is invalid unless `/X` algebraicity is assumed directly. `/Ω` algebraicity plus regular pullback does not imply algebraicity of arbitrary `σ`-fixed classes on `X`.
- “Cascade universality” conjecture (`706-708`). Acceptable as conjecture, but “smooth projective Kähler manifold” is sloppy; use smooth projective complex variety.
- “Langlands-cascade reduction” (`712-732`). Not a theorem. The proof assumes sweeping motivic Langlands and then leaps from automorphy to an embedding into the cascade substrate.
- Spectral count (`785-801`). Verified locally against Paper XXII and the spectrum script: `94/120 = 78.3%`, `26/120 = 21.7%`. This is finite spectral arithmetic only.
- Finite-noise diagnostic (`814-835`). The values are source-supported, but R2/R3 are forced by explicit insertion of `P_fix`; this is not evidence that the closure functional itself produces the projector.
- Aria H4O witness (`839-847`). Properly labelled external/reported, but not locally verifiable.

**2. Internal Consistency**
- `H_{4a}` and `H_{4a}^{alg}` are still conflated. Lines `234-236` say the theorem is strictly conditional on `H_{4a}`, omitting `H_{4a}^{alg}`. Lines `312` and `808-810` likewise blur embedding/compatibility with algebraicity.
- The status of the five examples is contradictory. Lines `57-66`, `242-268`, and `873-877` say Hodge is known for all five; lines `577-583`, `587-592`, and `754-759` admit CM abelian and complete-intersection cases are weaker.
- The paper advertises “cascade-compatible embedding / `σ`-transport” checks in Sections `CPn` through `CI` (`244-247`, `275-279`), but only `CP^n` even attempts an embedding, and that attempt fails.
- `Ω` is described as an `E_8` root-system substrate in `R^8` (`323-325`), but `H_{4a}^{alg}/Ω` treats it as an algebraic variety/scheme with cohomology and algebraic cycles (`171-175`). That object is not defined.
- Cross-references mostly resolve, but `Theorem~\ref{thm:main-cond}` points to a proposition (`195-209`). That is editorially misleading.
- `\Q[\pphi]` (`391`) and `\Q(\varphi)` are used interchangeably. Algebraically okay here, but the paper should choose one notation.

**3. External Consistency**
- `CascadeFoundations`: as bibliographed (`905-906`), this points to F1-F8 and I could not find `H_{4a}` there. The newer local source `papers/cascade-correspondence-foundations/foundations.tex:2802-2810` states `H_{4a}` as open embeddability/compatibility only; it does not supply algebraicity.
- `SigmaRationality` is not cited but should be. It proves the fixed-part/projector facts and explicitly says it does not prove algebraicity, Hodge, or embeddability (`cascade-sigma-rationality.tex:935-944`, `957-964`).
- `CascadeRHformal`: verifies the descriptive bridge-pattern analogy and the `94/26` spectral split. Its strengthened-conditional remark says the spectral count is the only unconditional part (`rh-formal.tex:2130-2148`).
- `ConvergenceWithSmart`: verified. It says architectural similarity, not a meta-theorem (`docs/convergence-with-smart.md:5-17`, `69-80`). The Hodge paper’s “descriptive, not formal” use is acceptable.
- `CascadeAttractorSpectrum`: verified by source and by running the spectrum script. The same script reports no localization on `2T`, so do not use it as attractor evidence beyond the `94/26` count.
- `CascadeAttractorDynamics`: local source encodes the reported R1/R2/R3 diagnostic, but the script did not finish in this sandbox. In any case, it tests explicit projector insertion, not closure-derived algebraicity.
- `ariaH4Olaw`: `aria-chess/` is absent under this repo root. I cannot verify the “8 nearly equal pairs” artifact locally.

**6. Top Three Fixes**
1. Fix the false five-class status claim. Lines `57-66`, `242-268`, `734-759`, and `873-877` must stop saying CM abelian varieties and low-codimension complete intersections are Hodge-proved in the stated generality. This is the most damaging overclaim.
2. Make `H_{4a}^{alg}` logically coherent. Lines `165-190`, `195-209`, and `676-702` need either a direct `/X` algebraicity axiom or a real surjectivity/lift theorem from `Ω` to `X`. Regular pullback alone is not enough.
3. Demote unsupported theorem environments. The cascade embedding of `CP^n` (`398-420`) and the Langlands-cascade reduction (`712-732`) are not theorem-grade. As written they advertise proofs the paper does not have.

**Claim Audit**
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:87`: “`T_E`’s convergence theorem … and its spectral identification … together are a rung-crossing operator.” The convergence theorem only proves absolute convergence of `T_E(s)` for `Re(s)>1`, not `T_E=T_E(1)`. This sentence still overstates the constructed object.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:136`: Proposition `thm:BSD` is formally true but vacuous: it assumes exactly the two equalities needed to conclude BSD. The proof at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:438` establishes only “axiom A and axiom B imply their transitive consequence,” not a mathematical reduction.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:155`: “Its conditions are equivalent … to classical Kolyvagin and Gross-Zagier input.” This is false as stated for rank `>=2`: Kolyvagin/Gross–Zagier do not supply the required identities there. The bridge axioms are at least BSD-strength, not equivalent to those rank-0/1 inputs.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:200`: “`T_p` acts on `H^1(E,Z)`.” This is not a standard Hecke action on the singular cohomology of the elliptic curve itself in the way used later. Hecke operators act naturally on modular curves/forms/Jacobians; the paper later admits the `H^1(E)` model is naive at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:360`.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:218`: Proposition “`≤ 2 log p`” is not proved. The proof obtains `2 φ log p` at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:232`, then “absorbs” `φ` into a numerically false inequality. State `≤ 2φ log p` or `O(log p)`.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:250`: Theorem `T_E(s)` convergence is essentially correct only if the corrected `2φ log p` bound is used and if the underlying operator space is defined. It does not justify `T_E(1)`.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:268`: Numerical bound “`≤ 10.5·φ` at `Re(s)=1.0`, decreasing to `0.32·φ` at `Re(s)=2.0`” is inconsistent with the script. The script gives Hasse upper bounds `10.52587` and `0.6376269` for primes `≤1000`; `0.32` is half the displayed Hasse upper bound.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:277`: Remark title says “Analytic continuation … via Rankin–Selberg,” and lines `288–296` assert a meromorphic operator-valued continuation and define `T_E(1)`. This directly contradicts the next remark at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:299`, which says Rankin–Selberg does not give such a continuation. Delete or demote lines `288–296`.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:344`: The Eichler–Shimura statement is at best imprecise. The displayed isomorphism from `H^1(E)` to an `f_E`-isotypic modular-form component is not a clean integral/rational statement as written, and it is not enough for any Mordell–Weil rank claim.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:354`: Corollary “`rank E(Q) ⊗ Q = dim_Q{f_E-eigenvectors in H^1(E,Q)}`” is false/ill-typed. `rank E(Q)` is an integer, not a vector space; the right side is bounded by `dim H^1(E,Q)=2`, while ranks are unbounded.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:381`: Corollary `dim ker T_E = rank E(Q)` is acceptable only as a restatement of Hypothesis `B_BSD,rank`; it has no independent proof.
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:406`: The spectral-to-`L` identity is explicitly a bridge axiom, not proved. Good demotion, but later references still call it “Theorem.”
- `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:438`: “Proof of Theorem” should be “Proof of Proposition.” It cites `thm:specL` at `papers/millennium-bsd-formal/bsd-formal.tex:444`, but that label is a remark, not a theorem.

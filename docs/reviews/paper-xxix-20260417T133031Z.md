Reviewed `papers/paper-xxix/paper-xxix.tex`.

**1. Claim Audit**
- **Lemma 1, “Basin locality”** (`“The basin \(B_\phi\) is determined by the pair \((\Fcal,\phi)\) alone.”`, lines 202-208). The proof establishes the narrow syntactic point: the gradient-flow equation and convergence test mention only `\Fcal` and `\phi`. It does **not** state the hypotheses under which the flow exists globally or the limit in Definition 4 is well-defined. As written, the lemma is acceptable only if basin existence is taken for granted.
- **Remark, “Basin partition”** (`“the basins ... partition a full-measure subset”`, lines 210-212). This is a substantive claim with no proof and insufficient hypotheses. You need finite-dimensionality, smoothness, Morse/Morse-Smale-type assumptions, and a specified measure. “Generic” is not enough.
- **Theorem 2, “Local measurement consistency, LMC”** (`lines 249-266`). The proof does establish the stated theorem from Definition 5. But the theorem is essentially vacuous: condition (3) of Definition 5 forces **every** probe functional to vanish on **every admitted basin** (lines 229-233), so zero coherence cost on a shared basin is built in. The theorem is therefore not an independent structural result.
- **Corollary 3** (`lines 272-281`). Correct given Theorem 2, but equally trivial for the same reason.
- **Definition 5 + Remark on physical interpretation** (`“distinguishing the basins”`, lines 216-239). This is the paper’s main mathematical failure. The definition says the observer distinguishes outcomes, but the formalism gives no quantity that distinguishes them: on any admitted basin, all probe costs are zero. The remark says the distinction is between “matched” and “idle” channels (lines 237-239), but no such readout variable is defined. At present the model encodes admissibility, not outcome registration.
- **Measurement claim** (`“If only one of the original configurations satisfies both conditions, the system resolves to that configuration.”`, lines 337-343). Not proved. Equation (18) gives a compatibility condition, not a dynamical selection theorem.
- **Relativistic claim** (`“no single observer region can span the entire event set while maintaining internal coherence”`, lines 392-393). Not proved. This is just the boundedness clause of Definition 2 restated as an explanation.
- **Toy-model numerical result** (`lines 494-513`). The arithmetic is correct: `0` for `\Phi_A`, `4` for `\Phi_B`. But this is only a hand-picked consistency check. It does not show that the stated closure functional actually has those two minima, nor that dynamics selects one.

**2. Internal Consistency**
- The paper defines an observer region as a **connected subgraph** of `\Gcal` (lines 120-121), then defines a localised probe observer by a **disjoint union** of probe subregions (lines 219-221). For `k>1`, that is generically disconnected. This is a direct definitional conflict.
- Type mismatch: `\Ocal` is a subgraph of `\Gcal` in Definition 1, but the probe pieces are subsets of `\Ecal` in Definition 5 (lines 217-228). You need one ontology: event subsets, induced subgraphs, or graph subregions.
- The claimed bridge to Paper XXIV is unstable. Here the observer region is said to “naturally induce” a linear extension and a sampling measure “determined by the local constraint structure” (lines 142-144, 381-383). No construction is given. Later sections rely on this as if it existed.
- Table 1 says `“Born rule probabilities — Derived in Paper XXX, using Theorem~\ref{thm:lmc}”` (line 359). That is misleading even by this paper’s own account. Lines 293-294 and 365-366 correctly say LMC only gives numerator coincidence; completeness is also required.
- Cross-references appear syntactically coherent. The problem is semantic, not mechanical.

**3. External Consistency**
- **Paper XXIV observer definition** (xxix lines 114-115, 376-383): verified. Paper XXIV defines an observer as `(\leq_O,\mu_O)` and proves observer-dependent simultaneity and differing elapsed times; see `paper-xxiv.tex` lines 61-75 and 112-159.
- **Paper XXIII no canonical global clock** (xxix lines 392-393): verified. `paper-xxiii.tex` lines 215-220 and 344-345 support this.
- **Paper XXVIII weak/strong ordering regime** (xxix lines 333, 440-445): verified in substance. `paper-xxviii.tex` lines 118, 244-248, 309-312 support superposition/multiplicity in the weak-ordering regime and gravitational structure in the strong-ordering regime.
- **Paper XIV stationary distribution** (xxix lines 365-366): verified, with caveats already stated in Paper XIV. `paper-xiv.tex` lines 174-190 and 399-402 give the stationary distribution, but uniqueness is conditional on irreducibility/regularity assumptions.
- **Paper XXX uses XXIX plus completeness to get restricted non-contextuality** (xxix lines 46, 186, 288, 293-294, 359, 365-366): verified. `paper-xxx.tex` lines 51-55, 153, 195-214, 580-607, 859, 948-950 support exactly that conditional downstream use.
- One mismatch: Paper XXX restates LMC in a **shared canonical probe** form (`paper-xxx.tex` lines 590-605), whereas Theorem 2 here does **not** require identical probes, only shared admitted basin (lines 249-257, 286-288). That is not fatal, but the paper should say clearly that XXX uses the stronger canonical-probe convention, not Theorem 2 naked.

**5. Surface Issues**
- Undefined macro `\Mcl` at line 293. As written, this will not compile unless it comes from elsewhere.
- The “Addendum status” note (lines 185-187) reads like revision history, not journal prose.
- Capitalisation/hyphenation around `Born rule`, `Born-rule`, `localised probe observer`, `observer-admissible` is not fully uniform.
- The paper uses `\partial_\Ocal \Phi` for boundary data (lines 135-137) without a clean formal definition of the boundary operator itself.
- “proper subgraph” / “subgraph of the transition graph” / “subset of events” are used interchangeably. That is not just style; it leaks into the definitions.

**6. Top Three Fixes**
1. **Give the probe observer a genuine outcome readout.** Right now “distinguishing basins” is asserted but not formalised: all probe costs vanish on all admitted basins, so the model does not encode which outcome occurred (lines 216-239, 249-266).
2. **Repair the observer/probe definitions so they are mutually consistent.** Connected observer region versus disjoint union of probes is an outright clash, and the graph/event typing is sloppy (lines 120-125, 217-228).
3. **Strip out claims that are merely definitional or conjectural.** The current draft repeatedly presents unproved bridges as established results: induction of `(\leq_O,\mu_O)` from the observer region, physical measurement apparatuses as probe observers, and the “structural explanation” of no global frame (lines 142-144, 242, 359, 381-383, 392-393).

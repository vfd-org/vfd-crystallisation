**1. Claim Audit**

- “*The result is a conditional derivation of the Born rule … the quadratic weighting is the only assignment consistent with the closure-induced K\"ahler geometry*” ([paper-xxx.tex](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:53>)). Over-claimed. Route E does not establish this as written. The key gap is [Corollary 4](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:454>): the paper has only a local, leading-order metric identification in the Laplace regime ([404–439](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:404>)), but then upgrades this to a global Fubini–Study Kähler structure on all of `\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}` by fiat (“By construction … invariant under the full action of `U(N)`”, [459](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:459>)). That construction is not given.

- Proposition “*Conditional Born weighting from stationary sector measure*” ([258–277](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:258>)). This proof is acceptable only for the conditional statement actually written. It does establish the displayed formula under the hidden extra assumption that “supported on `\Phi_i`” means support essentially contained in one sector so that `\int_{\Phi_i} |\phi_j|^2 = 0` for `j \neq i`, not merely “well-separated basins”. As written, assumption 2 (“negligible overlap”) is doing too much work.

- “*Within the equilibrium closure regime, the quadratic probability law need not be introduced as an independent measurement axiom; it is inherited from the stationary measure*” ([283–291](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:283>)). Slightly too strong. Route C still assumes that measurement samples the constrained stationary ensemble ([241–247](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:241>)); that is not inherited from the stationary measure, it is an extra measurement postulate.

- Proposition “*Closure-preserving unitaries are sufficient statistics*” ([363–376](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:363>)). The proof is formally sloppy to the point of being unreliable. `\Psi` is simultaneously the state and the integration variable; `\Phi_i` are sectors of configuration space, but `T` acts on `\Hcal_O`; `\Pst(T\Psi)=\Pst(\Psi)` is asserted on `\Mcl`, not on the underlying configuration space. The intended covariance claim is plausible, but the present proof does not cleanly establish it because the objects live in mismatched spaces.

- Proposition “*Closure Hessian is the Fisher metric in the Laplace regime*” ([404–436](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:404>)). This is the best-behaved result in Route E. The proposition is correctly hedged as local and leading-order, and the proof supports exactly that.

- Corollary “*Fubini–Study from closure, K\"ahler version*” ([454–460](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:454>)). Not established. From a local Hessian/Fisher identification plus imported `J` and `\omega`, you do not get a globally defined `U(N)`-invariant Kähler structure on `\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}`. The proof simply assumes the crucial global invariance/class-membership needed to invoke Petz. This is the central mathematical overreach.

- Theorem “*Non-contextuality of sector probability*” ([484–494](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:484>)). This proves only a restricted context-independence result for contexts realised by localised probe observers sharing the same canonical probe. It does not establish general Gleason-style non-contextuality on all rank-1 projectors unless every relevant context is known to arise that way. That missing universality hypothesis is suppressed.

- Corollary “*(U2) is derived, not assumed*” ([496–502](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:496>)). Over-claimed. The proof imports Route C’s sector-supported decomposition and basin disjointness for normalisation, and imports the canonical-probe convention plus the localised-probe-observer restriction for context-independence. So `(U2)` is not derived in the theorem’s own generality; it is recovered only on a narrowed class of measurement contexts.

- Theorem “*Born weighting uniqueness from closure, `N \ge 3`*” ([524–561](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:524>)). Not proved as stated. Step 2 imports Gleason and gets an affine `g`. Step 3 then sets `g(0)=0` via “probability of a sector in which the state has zero amplitude must vanish” ([556](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:556>)), but that is an additional eigenstate/orthogonality assumption not listed in `(U1)`–`(U3)`. Without it, affine `g` plus basis normalisation leaves room for `g(x)=x+\beta(1-Nx)`-type constraints to be analysed properly; the proof as written simply inserts the desired boundary condition.

- Corollary “*Exclusion of `|c|^p` for `p \neq 2`*” ([563–573](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:563>)). The proof is wrong. In the “Hadamard-rotated basis” used at [572](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:572>), `\Pi_1=|\phi_1\rangle\langle\phi_1|` is no longer one of the basis projectors, so this does not show basis dependence of `Q` for a fixed projector with different orthogonal completions. As written, the counterexample does not hit the stated claim.

- Remark “*Theorem `\ref{thm:born_unique}`’s conclusion holds for all `N \ge 2`*” ([576–577](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:576>)). Unsupported. The `N=2` argument introduces extra assumptions `(iv)` and `(v)` not present in Theorem 5, especially eigenstate certainty. You cannot announce the same conclusion for all `N \ge 2` under a different hypothesis set and pretend it is the same theorem.

- “*The mechanism is demonstrated in a two-basin toy model*” ([726](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:726)). Too strong. The toy model merely illustrates the already-assumed sector-supported decomposition; it does not demonstrate that the closure dynamics itself produces coefficients `a,b` or that the stationary measure of the specific double-well actually equals `|\Psi|^2` for the chosen ansatz.

**2. Internal Consistency**

- The manuscript contradicts itself on whether non-contextuality is assumed or derived. It is said to be “assumed rather than derived” at [94](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:94>), “derived, not assumed” at [53](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:53>) and [497](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:497>), then bizarrely reverts to “the one axiom that Route E does not produce … is non-contextuality” at [705](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:705>). This is not a nuance issue; it is direct inconsistency.

- The observer-side assumptions are also unstable. At [515](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:515>) LMC is no longer external; at [796](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:796>) “the single remaining observer-side assumption is (LMC)”. Those cannot both be true.

- The notation is type-unstable from the start. `\Phi` denotes the configuration space at [127](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:127>), then an element of that same space in [132](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:132>). Likewise `\Phi_i` are introduced as connected components/subsets at [138–143](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:138>), then treated like “outcomes” and later effectively as basis labels.

- Definition 6 of the “closure statistical manifold” ([341–347](</mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:341>)) mixes a projective Hilbert state `[ \Psi ] \in \mathbb{CP}^{N-1}` with the earlier admissible configuration set `\Phi_{\mathrm{adm}}(\Ocal)`. Those are not the same object. The manuscript never cleanly defines the map from admissible configurations to projective states.

- The cross-references I checked are syntactically plausible; no obvious broken `\ref`/`\eqref` targets jump out from the source. The problem is not unresolved labels but that several references point to results stronger than the cited result actually supports.

**1. Claim Audit**

Line numbers refer to `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex`.

- L174-L201, `H_{4a}^{alg}`: the claimed implication `H_{4a}^{alg}/Ω => H_{4a}^{alg}/X` is not established. Regularity of `\iota_X` only says pullbacks of algebraic cycles remain algebraic; it does not say every `σ`-fixed class on `X` is pulled back from `Ω`. A surjectivity/image hypothesis is missing.
- L204-L218, “Main conditional reduction”: as stated, the result is either tautological if `H_{4a}^{alg}/X` is assumed, or unproved if only `H_{4a}^{alg}/Ω` is assumed. The phrase “via pullback along `\iota_X`” is not justified.
- L346-L348, “F5”: stated as a theorem with no proof, label, or usable citation. Also stronger than the RH-formal treatment, where analogous `σ`-invariance is conditional.
- L355-L378, `σ` action on cohomology: essentially coefficient-level and plausible, but the proof treats integral/coefficient Hodge pieces too casually. It needs a clean tensor/coefficient-extension formulation.
- L382-L403, “`σ`-fixed classes are rational”: true in spirit, but the proof assumes a basis of `H^*(X,Z)`. Integral cohomology can have torsion; rewrite using the decomposition `M \otimes_Z Z[φ] ≅ M ⊕ Mφ`.
- L405-L407, corollary: follows over `Q(φ)`, but notation switches to `Q[φ]`.
- L431-L435 and L442-L462, projective space: proof establishes the claim.
- L478-L492, Bruhat/Schubert claims: standard, but cited/proved insufficiently. More seriously, L487-L488/L507 use the wrong degree convention: a Schubert cell of dimension `ℓ(w)` gives homology degree `2ℓ(w)`, while its Poincare-dual cohomology class has complementary degree unless opposite/codimension indexing is specified.
- L496-L516, flag Hodge theorem: conclusion is standard, but the proof inherits the Schubert degree-indexing error.
- L534-L543, Danilov theorem: overclaims the rank. `rank H^{2p}` is not simply the number of `p`-dimensional cones; relations matter. The generation-by-invariant-cycles part is the usable claim.
- L547-L565, toric Hodge theorem: conclusion is standard for smooth complete toric varieties, but the citation key is wrong at L548 and the proof relies on the overstrong Danilov formulation.
- L578-L608, CM abelian status: correctly does not prove Hodge. However L610-L615 then overclaims a cascade identification of CM abelian varieties via `Z[φ]`; general CM fields need not contain `Q(√5)`.
- L630-L637, Lefschetz hyperplane theorem: acceptable.
- L641-L671, complete intersections: not established. The theorem is carefully worded at L641-L643, but the proof proves a stronger false/unknown claim and concludes full Hodge at L670. Noether-Lefschetz does not construct all primitive middle Hodge classes for arbitrary low-codimension complete intersections.
- L683-L709, cascade-embeddable proposition: proof has the same missing-surjectivity defect as L174-L201. Pulling algebraic classes back from `Ω` does not algebraize arbitrary `σ`-fixed classes on `X`.
- L719-L739, Langlands-cascade reduction: still a theorem, not speculative future work. The proof sketch assumes “every motive is automorphic” and that cascade realization of automorphic forms yields embeddings; neither implication is established.
- L792-L808, spectral count: arithmetic checks out; scripts reproduce `94/120 = 78.3%` and `26/120 = 21.7%`. But “exact computed in `Q(√5)` arithmetic” is not supported by the cited script alone; the script uses numerical eigendecomposition plus classification. Exactness is only externally supported through the RH-formal/Paper XXII chain.
- L821-L842, finite-noise diagnostic: verified by running the local script; it reproduces `0.6639`, `1.0000`, `1.0000`. Calling R3 a “control” is too strong because the closure projector forces the result.

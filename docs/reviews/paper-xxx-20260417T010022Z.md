**1. Claim Audit**
- Lines 258-272: “`Conditional Born weighting from stationary sector measure` … `P(\Phi_i\mid\Ocal)=|c_i|^2`.” The algebra is fine under disjoint support and normalisation, but the proof only shows a decomposition identity for integrals of `|\Psi|^2`. It does not establish that measurement frequencies equal those integrals. That missing step is explicitly only a “working hypothesis” at lines 241-247. Later prose repeatedly overstates this as a derivation rather than a conditional inheritance result.
- Lines 363-367: “`Closure-preserving unitaries are sufficient statistics`.” False as stated. The proof establishes covariance of the sector-mass assignment under a very particular class of measure-preserving sector-permuting unitaries. It does not show these maps are “sufficient statistics” in Chentsov’s sense. The title is over-claiming.
- Lines 386-395: the three-part “`Chentsov … Petz quantum extension`” theorem is not established as stated. Item (2) is not what Chentsov/Petz ordinarily says; item (3), the “push-forward” of Fubini-Study to Fisher-Rao, is much stronger than the supporting sentence at line 398, which only discusses the real slice. This needs either a real theorem with a correct citation or a demotion to heuristic background.
- Lines 404-416: “`Closure Hessian is the Fisher metric in the Laplace regime`.” The proof is not formally coherent. `\theta` is declared as local coordinates on `\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}`, but the density family is written as `\Fcal(\psi-\Psi^*(\theta))`, i.e. as a translation family in a linear configuration space. No map from projective-state coordinates to translated configuration-space densities is defined. This is not a minor gap; it breaks the statement as written.
- Lines 454-461: “`Fubini--Study identification, given Papers XVIII and XXI`.” This does not prove what the surrounding prose suggests. Assumption (K1) already gives a global `U(N)`-invariant Kähler structure on `\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}` with Fubini-Study metric up to scale. The corollary therefore largely restates an imported result from XXI and uses the local Hessian only to match scale at one point. The proof’s jump from pointwise agreement at `\Psi^*` to global coincidence is not justified by anything proved here.
- Lines 494-499: “`Non-contextuality of sector probability`.” Narrowly, this works if one grants the hidden hypotheses the proof actually uses: canonical probe assignment and a correspondence between orthonormal bases and localised probe observers sharing the same probe for `\phi_1`. Those assumptions are not cleanly carried into the theorem statement.
- Lines 506-507: “`Restricted (U2) for the localised-probe-observer sub-class`.” Overstated. What is proved is restricted context-independence inside the canonical-probe localised-probe class. That is weaker than the full `(U2)` used later, which quantifies over every orthonormal basis.
- Lines 538-556: “`Born weighting uniqueness from closure, N\ge 3`.” The proof does not establish the stated theorem. Step 1 uses full `U(N)` orbit classification, but assumption `(U1)` only gives covariance under “closure-preserving unitaries,” a subgroup that has not been shown to be all of `U(N)`. Without that enlargement, the reduction to a scalar function `g(x)` does not go through.
- Lines 582-587: “`Exclusion of |c|^p for p\neq 2`.” This one is fine. The counterexample is explicit and does what it says.
- Lines 616-617: the `N=2` remark asserts uniqueness from `SU(2)` invariance on the Bloch sphere, but no proof is given. As a remark this is acceptable; as part of the paper’s advertised uniqueness package it is underproved.
- Lines 655-683: the toy model ends with “`This is the Born rule for the two-outcome case.`” No. It is a schematic illustration. In the “asymmetric case” the wavefunction is inserted by hand and is not shown to be the stationary state of the double-well closure functional introduced two lines earlier.

**2. Internal Consistency**
- The paper never keeps its state spaces straight. At lines 127-133, `\Phi` is both the configuration space and a configuration. At lines 251-254 sector weights are integrals over subsets of configuration space. At lines 341-346 `\Mcl` is suddenly a subset of `\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}` consisting of `[\Psi]` with `\Psi\in\Phi_{\mathrm{adm}}`. Those are not the same objects.
- Outcome sectors are defined as connected components/basin neighbourhoods of the admissible configuration set at lines 138-143, but later they are treated as supports of sector wavefunctions, then as rays/projectors in `\Hcal_\Ocal` (lines 263, 335-346, 495-499). The map between these three notions is never stated.
- Lines 355-359 define a unitary on `\Hcal_\Ocal` and then quantify “for all `\Psi\in\Mcl`,” although `\Mcl` was defined as projective rays. The action is not well-typed.
- Lines 404-410 mix tangent coordinates on `\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}` with a translated density family in a linear variable `\psi`. This is the sharpest formal inconsistency in the paper.
- The paper oscillates between “Route E constructs the Kähler structure” (lines 329, 743) and “the global Kähler structure is supplied by Paper XXI, not derived here” (lines 468-473). The latter is the honest version.
- Static scan found no obviously undefined `\ref`/`\eqref`, but I could not compile in the read-only sandbox, so I am not certifying the PDF build.

**6. Top Three Fixes**
1. Fix the state-space mess before anything else. Separate configuration space, admissible basins, Hilbert vectors, and projective rays, and define the maps between them. The present version is formally incoherent at lines 127-143, 341-346, 355-376, and especially 404-416.
2. Rewrite Route E so it says only what is actually proved. Right now lines 386-466 and 538-580 advertise a closure-driven uniqueness theorem, but the global Kähler/Fubini-Study structure is imported from XXI and the symmetry needed in Step 1 is not earned.
3. Narrow the non-contextuality and uniqueness claims to the observer class actually covered. Carry the canonical-probe/same-probe hypothesis into the theorem statements and stop pretending this yields full `(U2)` on arbitrary bases. The damage is at lines 494-507, 538-549, 746, 782, and 832-839.

**1. Claim Audit**
- L32, L46: “11/11 PASS” is accurate; I ran `scripts/verify_sm_structure.py`. But “falsifiable nulls for every claim” is too strong: Q4 tests one bad pairing, CH4 tests only non-commutation, and CH2 homomorphism is sampled in code.
- L35: “closes both at structure grade” overclaims. Chirality is not fully derived, and representation content remains partly open in L131.

Part (c) is false as stated unless `q` is restricted to the 120 substrate units: in continuous `SU(2)_R`, every unit quaternion in the `i`-circle commutes with `J`, not only `±1, ±i`. Part (d) is not proved; “two non-commuting images exist” does not imply irreducibility.
- L79-L80, Corollary 2: not established. “Trivially on the conjugate module” is not proved and the conjugate/opposite Weyl module is not defined. The Lorentzian continuation is explicitly imported, so “couples to left-handed Weyl components” needs to be weakened to a conditional statement.
- L95-L100, Proposition 3: the ladder CAR are numerically verified, not proved “exactly”. The adjoint `\dagger` and inner product are not defined.
- L102-L106, Theorem 4: the spectrum is numerically verified. The `su(3)` “triplet-wise” claim is weaker than stated: the script checks commutation and non-abelian action, not an irreducible fundamental/anti-fundamental representation certificate. “Electric charge” is an interpretation after choosing `Q=N/3`; the theorem gives a normalized number spectrum, not physical charge by itself. Q4 is only one incompatible pairing.
- L112-L114, Corollary 5: mostly tautological. Once `Q` and `T_3` are assigned, `Y=2(Q-T_3)` follows by definition. This does not fix hypercharge assignments unless doublet/singlet assignments, signs, and constant `Y` on each doublet are supplied.
- L120: the mass deviations are reported by `scripts/sm_ledger.py`, but “worst placements” as `~25%` is loose: the down entry prints `+26.1%`. The paragraph correctly says masses are not derived, but “parameter-free” is strained because shell integers are placed/read from data.

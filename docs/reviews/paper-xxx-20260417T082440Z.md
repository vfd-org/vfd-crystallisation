**1. Claim Audit**

- `“Conditional Born weighting from stationary sector measure”` (lines 290-305): mostly established, but only as an algebraic statement about integrals over sector-supported components. The proof at lines 307-309 does **not** establish a measurement theorem, because the sampling step is explicitly only a “working hypothesis” at lines 273-279. The paper should stop talking as if Proposition 1 alone derives observed frequencies.

- `“the stationary-measure route yields \(P_i=|c_i|^2\)”` (lines 792-793; same overstatement at 51-53, 205, 777): over-claimed. What is proved is: if equilibrium holds, if sectors are well-separated, if \(\Psi\) has sector-supported decomposition, and if measurement samples the constrained stationary ensemble, then the sector masses reduce to \(|c_i|^2\). One missing hypothesis is still doing real work.

- `“Sector-preserving unitaries induce sector-probability covariance”` (lines 400-405): not established. The proof at lines 407-412 is ill-typed. \(T\) is defined on \(\Hcal_\Ocal\), but the integral is over configuration-space subsets \(\Phi_i(\Ocal)\subset\mathbb R^\Ecal\). No action of \(T\) on configuration space or on \(d\mu\) is defined.

- `“Chentsov … Petz quantum extension”` (lines 425-435): not proved here; cited only. That is acceptable as imported mathematics, but then it should be treated as an external theorem, not as something established by the paper.

- `“Closure Hessian as the Fisher metric … in the Laplace regime”` (lines 443-456): established, but only for the specific translated location family on configuration space. The proof supports exactly that local Fisher/Hessian statement and nothing on \(\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\).

- `“Fubini–Study identification, given Papers XVIII and XXI”` (lines 494-502): not really proved. (K1) already assumes Paper XXI supplies a \(U(N)\)-invariant Kähler structure with \(g_{\mathrm{XXI}}\) equal to Fubini–Study up to scale. The corollary then “derives” Fubini–Study from that assumption. Worse, (K3) concerns a configuration-space Hessian at \(\phi^*\), but the corollary rewrites it as \(H[\Psi^*]\) at lines 499-501. This is a type error, not a derivation.

- `“Non-contextuality of sector probability”` (lines 534-540): proved only under a much narrower hypothesis than the headline suggests. The proof at lines 542-544 works if two contexts are realised by localised-probe observers sharing the same canonical probe. It does **not** show non-contextuality for arbitrary orthonormal bases of \(\Hcal_\Ocal\).

- `“Restricted (U2) for the localised-probe-observer sub-class”` (lines 546-548): only partly established. The non-contextuality half follows from Theorem 4. The normalisation half at lines 550-551 imports Route C’s separation/support assumptions and should be stated with them.

- `“Born weighting uniqueness from closure, \(N\ge 3\)”` (lines 578-598): mathematically fine **given** (U1)-(U4). But it is not a closure-theoretic derivation in the strong sense advertised elsewhere. (U1) is imported from XXI, (U2) is only derived on a restricted observer class, and (U4) already assumes eigenstate certainty.

- `“Exclusion of \(|c|^p\) for \(p\neq 2\)”` (lines 623-629): established. This is one of the cleanest parts of the paper.

- `“For \(N=2\) … the unique solution is the latitude function”` (lines 657-659): asserted, not proved and not cited. As written, it is not referee-grade.

- `“The mechanism is demonstrated in a two-basin toy model”` (line 802): false as stated. The example at lines 705-718 explicitly inserts the asymmetric state by hand. It illustrates bookkeeping; it does not demonstrate a mechanism.

**2. Internal Consistency**

- Lines 127-140 say `“Four distinct spaces appear”`, then list six.

- Definition of \(\Mcl(\Ocal)\) at lines 373-379 is ill-typed: it defines a subset of \(\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\) by requiring `\(\Psi \in \Phi_{\mathrm{adm}}(\Ocal)\)`, but \(\Phi_{\mathrm{adm}}(\Ocal)\subset\mathbb R^\Ecal\), not \(\Hcal_\Ocal\).

- The paper repeatedly blurs configuration-space objects, Hilbert vectors, and projectors. The worst instance is lines 499-501, where Proposition 3’s Hessian \(H[\phi^*]\) becomes \(H[\Psi^*]\).

- Route E says at lines 361-363 that it `“constructs the relevant Kähler structure explicitly from prior-paper primitives”`, but lines 508-513 admit the global Kähler structure is simply imported from XXI. Those cannot both be the paper’s headline.

- Cross-references appear internally consistent: I found no unresolved `\ref`/`\eqref` targets in the LaTeX source.

**3. External Consistency**

- Current lines 256-260, 318, 862 cite Paper XIV for the stationary law \(P_{\mathrm{st}}\propto e^{-2\Fcal/\sigma^2}\). Verified: XIV lines 174-190 prove the stationary distribution, with uniqueness only under extra irreducibility/regularity assumptions.

- Current lines 275 and 835 speak as if XIV gives an unqualified unique stationary measure and convergence of arbitrary initial data. Not verified. XIV line 190 conditions uniqueness; I did not find a convergence theorem for `“any initial distribution converges to \(P_{\mathrm{st}}\)”`.

- Current lines 264-267 and 319 cite XVIII for \(\Psi_N=\sqrt{\rho}e^{iS/\sigma^2}\) and the equilibrium Schrödinger regime. Verified: XVIII lines 215-217 define \(\Psi_N\); lines 325-359 establish the exact/near-equilibrium Schrödinger reduction.

- Current lines 488, 498, 811 cite XVIII for the almost-complex structure from Nelson pairing. Verified: XVIII lines 278-306.

- Current line 176 attributes to XVIII the claim that the sector basis vector \(\phi_i\) is the square root of the equilibrium density localised on \(\Phi_i(\Ocal)\). I could not verify that in XVIII or XXI from the local sources. As stated, that attribution looks invented.

- Current lines 138, 367-379, 393, 419, 497 cite XXI for the finite-dimensional sector-superposition Hilbert space, \(\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\), Hamiltonian/Kähler structure, and \(U(N)\) isometries. Verified, with scope caveat: XXI lines 166-230 prove these only in the equilibrium-tangent linearised regime.

- Current lines 47, 74, 276, 518-569, 781 cite XXIX for observer-admissibility, measurement as reduction of admissible configurations, localised probe observers, and LMC. Verified: XXIX lines 163-174, 347-375, 216-303.

- Current lines 828 and 846 cite XIX for the nonlinear residual near equilibrium. Verified in broad form: XIX lines 41 and 470.

**5. Surface Issues**

- Line 214: `\Phi \in \Phi` is nonsense; the ambient space is missing.

- Lines 127-140: count mismatch (`four` vs six entries).

- Lines 373-379: malformed definition of \(\Mcl(\Ocal)\) as noted above.

- Lines 499-501: \(H[\Psi^*]\) should be \(H[\phi^*]\), and even that would still need a map from configuration-space tangent data to projective-state tangent data.

- The notation oscillates between \(P(\Phi_i\mid\Ocal)\) and \(P(\Pi_i,\Psi)\) without a clean statement of when the probability is sector-labelled, projector-labelled, or observer-labelled.

- The paper calls the current manuscript both “Paper XXX” and `paper-xxx`; that is harmless internally, but the self-referential upstream/downstream language is awkward.

**6. Top Three Fixes**

1. Rebuild Route E so it says exactly what is local, what is imported, and what is actually proved. The current version overclaims at lines 361-363, 494-506, 778-781, and 867-874.

2. Repair the type discipline between configuration space and projective Hilbert space. The worst offenders are lines 145-176, 373-379, 400-412, and 499-501. In the current form, several arguments are literally not well-formed.

3. Narrow every non-contextuality and uniqueness claim to the observer class actually covered by XXIX, and carry the missing hypotheses explicitly. The problematic spots are lines 53, 100, 534-552, 578-620, 781, and 874.

**1. Claim Audit**
- Lines 42-48: “The present paper addresses the most critical of these” and “identifies a dynamical route by which the sector-separation assumption of Paper XXX arises.” Overstated. The paper offers a heuristic mechanism class, not a proof that physical measurement interactions generate the sector structure Paper XXX needs.
- Lines 55-63: “Paper XXX showed... [under] three assumptions” and “Assumption 1 is justified by the ergodic convergence...” False as stated. Paper XXX’s Route C needs five assumptions, not three: equilibrium, well-separated sectors, sector-supported decomposition, sampling, and state-completeness. Paper XIV does not prove general ergodic convergence.
- Lines 118-125: stationary law `P_st \propto e^{-2F_total/\sigma^2}`. Conditionally acceptable only if the Paper XIV hypotheses are imported for the joint system-apparatus state space. Those hypotheses are omitted here.
- Lines 168-172: Proposition “Sector-forming measurement interactions.” The stated proof does not establish the stated claim. Definition 1 gives only low-cost matched channels and high-cost mismatches at selected points; it does not imply:
  - existence of exactly `N` basin regions,
  - existence/location of saddles between them,
  - the lower bound in line 171,
  - or large barriers relative to `\sigma^2`.
  The proof itself concedes that a rigorous proof is missing (lines 175-177). As written, this is not a proved proposition.
- Lines 189-194: Kramers-type suppression. This is fine as an imported small-noise estimate from Paper XIV.
- Lines 209-216: “cross terms satisfy ... because the overlap ... is exponentially small in the barrier height.” Not proved. A large barrier does not by itself give the overlap estimate without additional semiclassical/spectral hypotheses. This does not establish Assumption 3 of Paper XXX.
- Lines 226-232: “The observer constraint acts as an additional barrier.” Not established. Paper XXIX defines admissibility by `C_O <= epsilon`; it does not prove an added dynamical barrier term in the closure flow.
- Lines 245-257: `\tau_sep ~ 1/||∇^2 F_int||` and hierarchy `\tau_sep << \tau_relax << \tau_obs`. Pure heuristic. Fine as a conjectural scaling ansatz, not as support for the abstract’s “generic conditions” claim (line 46).
- Lines 286-303: toy model. This section is mathematically wrong in a way that breaks the paper’s central example. With
  `c = κ λ g^2 / (2(κ+λ))`,
  the effective potential is `F_eff(φ)=α(φ^2-1)^2 + c φ^2`.
  Its nonzero minima satisfy `φ^2 = 1 - c/(2α)`, so they exist only if `c < 2α`. The barrier is
  `ΔF = α(1 - c/(2α))^2`,
  which decreases with coupling and vanishes at `c=2α`. The paper claims the opposite at lines 295-299 and in the abstract at line 46.
- Lines 313-315 and 383-392: “This paper shows...” and “This result provides the missing structural justification...” Overclaim. The paper does not prove large barriers, sector decomposition, or measurement sampling.

1. `Lemma [Basin locality]` ([paper-xxix.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxix/paper-xxix.tex:202), lines 202-208)

2. `Theorem [Local measurement consistency, LMC]` ([paper-xxix.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxix/paper-xxix.tex:249), lines 249-255)  
Quote: “Suppose both include a probe subregion for the same outcome \(\phi\) … with the same subregion and the same probe functional \(c_\phi\). Then for every configuration \(\Phi\in B_\phi\), … \(\Ccal_\Ocal = c_\phi = \Ccal_{\Ocal'}\).”  
Verdict: the proof is formally correct from Definition  \ref{def:probe}, condition (2), and condition (4). The problem is upstream: Definition \ref{def:probe} is not coherent as stated. Condition (3) says \(c_{\phi_j}\) vanishes exactly on the target minimum \(\phi_j\) restricted to the probe region; condition (4) says the same \(c_{\phi_j}\) also vanishes on every other basin \(B_{\phi_i}\), \(i\neq j\) (lines 229-233). Unless every off-basin configuration locally matches the target restriction, those two conditions conflict. The theorem is therefore proved from hypotheses that may be inconsistent or at least far stronger than the prose admits. As written, this is the paper’s central mathematical defect.

**2. Internal Consistency**

1. The object \(\Ocal\) changes type. In Definition \ref{def:observer_region}, \(\Ocal\subset\Gcal\) is a connected subgraph (lines 120-121). In Definition \ref{def:probe}, \(\Ocal\) is a disjoint union of probe subregions \(O_{\phi_j}\subset\Ecal\) (lines 219-228). In the toy model, \(\Ocal=\{e_2\}\) is a set of events (lines 329-333, 507-510). A subgraph of \(\Gcal\), a subset of \(\Ecal\), and a singleton event set are not interchangeable objects.

2. \(\Gcal\) is used but never defined in this paper. The reader is told only that \(\Ocal\subset\Gcal\) is a “connected subgraph of the transition graph” (line 121). No transition graph is introduced.

3. Definition \ref{def:probe} is not visibly a subclass of Definition \ref{def:observer}. The latter requires connectedness of \(\Ocal\) as a subgraph and persistence as a dynamical property (lines 125-139). The former replaces \(\Ocal\) by a disjoint union of probe subregions and says nothing about connectedness or persistence (lines 216-235).

4. Condition (3) and condition (4) of Definition \ref{def:probe} conflict, as noted above. The explanatory remark at lines 237-239 makes the conflict worse by trying to justify both simultaneously.

5. Cross-references inside the file appear syntactically resolved. I do not see missing internal labels. The problem is semantic: surrounding prose often cites Theorem \ref{thm:lmc} as though it were unconditional, when it is heavily conditional. The worst examples are lines 46-50, 299-303, 369-375, and 537-539.

6. The corollary at lines 273-282 uses “the inequality \(\Ccal\le\epsilon\)” as though \(\epsilon\) were a fixed shared threshold. The definition at lines 163-168 does not make that universal.

**6. Top Three Fixes**

1. Repair Definition \ref{def:probe}.  
Lines 216-233, plus the explanatory gloss at 237-239, are not coherent. Condition (3) and condition (4) need to be rewritten so they can both hold non-vacuously. Until that is fixed, Theorem \ref{thm:lmc} (lines 249-270) rests on sand.

2. Clean the ontology of the observer object.  
Lines 120-139, 216-228, and 329-333 use \(\Ocal\) as three different kinds of object. Define the transition graph \(\Gcal\), say whether observer regions are vertex sets or induced subgraphs, and make the probe decomposition live in the same category.

3. Stop overstating what LMC buys you.  
The abstract, discussion, and Born-rule bridge at lines 46-50, 294-303, 369-375, and 537-539 suppress the same-probe, same-threshold, and downstream completeness assumptions. Those qualifications must be moved up front, not buried after the theorem.

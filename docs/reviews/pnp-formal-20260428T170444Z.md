The proof also needs \(B_{\sigma-P}\), and it assumes without proof that generic \(\sigma\)-irreducible 3-SAT lacks rung-localization, \(\varphi\)-adic, and \(2I\)-orbit structure (lines 483-488).

- “Generic \(\sigma\)-irreducibility” for random 3-SAT, “for any \(\alpha>0\)” (lines 248-252): not proved and likely false under the standard fixed-density random 3-SAT model. With \(m=\alpha n\), linearly many variables are absent with high probability, making reducibility immediate under the given definition. The proof only yields anything for \(\alpha>1\) (lines 271-275), and the per-clause \(1/2\) bound is asserted, not derived.

- “Generic 3-SAT is \(\sigma\)-irreducible” (lines 280-283): unsupported because it depends on the defective theorem above.

**1. Claim Audit**

- Lines 129-138, F1: “has a unique positive solution … and this solution is globally attracting.” This one is actually proved. The contraction-on-\(J=[3/2,2]\) argument plus the entry-after-finitely-many-iterates argument is sufficient. Minor issue only: the proof at lines 158-159 says “\(r_3,r_4\in J\)” when \(r_3\in J\) already suffices.

- Lines 212-218, F2: “any admissible closure functional \(F\) … takes the form” \(\alpha R+\beta E-\gamma Q\). The proof does not classify admissible functionals; it merely restates the exclusions in Remark 2.2 and then declares victory (lines 220-222). This is not a theorem. It is a definition-by-exclusion. Hidden assumptions: trace terms, full kinetic terms, and mixed trace-divergence terms are removed by fiat, and one of those removals already appeals forward to the Fierz-Pauli setup of F7 (line 207), so the logical order is backwards.

- Lines 256-262, F3: “one proposed chain is \(E_8\to H_4\to 40\to D_4\to16\to8\to0\) … the preferred structural model, not a theorem.” The proposition text is appropriately weak. The proof is not. Lines 283-287 claim each step is forced and no intermediate rung exists, which is precisely the first-principles uniqueness the proposition says is not proved. That is a direct internal contradiction.

- Lines 271-279, F3 Step 3-5. The step \(40\to D_4\) is only loosely tied to the stated chain conditions; the step \(D_4\to16\) is worse: “the 24 roots of \(D_4\) decompose as three copies of 8” conflates root counts with representation dimensions. The step \(16\to8\) is asserted via Clifford/octonion rhetoric, not demonstrated as a sub-polytope inclusion satisfying condition (a). The proof does not establish the stated chain.

- Lines 330-340, F4: “\(N_{\mathrm{total}}=583\).” As a dimension count from Definition 4.1, this is proved. But it is only as strong as Definition 4.1, which hard-codes the \(24^2\) tensor block at \(D_4\). The theorem is tautological, not structural.

- Lines 319-323 and 346-348, shell amplitude \(K=\varphi^{-1}\) and residue \(\varphi^{-583}\). These are used as if derived from F1/F2/F4. They are not. “By minimality … the leading non-trivial power is \(\varphi^{-1}\)” (lines 320-323) is unsupported. That is a new model postulate smuggled in as a consequence.

- Lines 372-378, F5: “\(F[\sigma\cdot\Phi]=\sigma\cdot F[\Phi]\).” Under the explicit rationality hypotheses, the proposition is defensible. The proof is schematic but adequate if one grants the \(\mathbb Q(\varphi)\)-defined background and measure.

- Lines 388-396, factor-2 corollary. Not established as written. The proof moves from an integral over \(\overline E\) to “restriction” to two \(H_4\) copies inside \(E_8\) (lines 399-401), but that restriction has not been defined for the continuum functional \(F\). Additivity over a decomposition of the 240 roots is not the same thing as additivity of the continuum action over two subdomains.

- Lines 428-434, F6: “\((X_n,\delta_n)\to(S^3,d_{\rm round})\)” with rate \(\varepsilon_n\le \varepsilon_0 2^{-n}\). The proof does not establish this. It explicitly assumes the halving rule beyond \(n=1\) (line 439), and the jump from subgroup density to the \(\varepsilon_n\)-net property is unjustified. BI2 is also hand-waved by a “standard sphere-polygonal estimate” with no actual control of the graph metric induced by the chosen threshold graph. This is conjectural, not proved.

- Lines 487-489, F7: “the closure contribution carrying \(\Lambda\) localises at the \(D_4\) rung.” The argument does not prove localisation; it assumes it in hypotheses (ii) and (iii), and hypothesis (ii) is circular because Definition 4.1 already singles out \(D_4\) as the only tensor-bearing rung. What is shown is only: if you place a Fierz-Pauli graviton sector on \(D_4\), then a \(\Lambda g_{\mu\nu}\) term has rank 2 there.

- Lines 551-559, F8. As an “assignment scheme, not a derivation,” this is honestly stated and internally fine. The proof does exactly what is claimed: assigns coefficients by matching conventions. It proves no physics.

- Lines 565-571, zero-parameter corollary. Too strong unless weakened further. It depends on the undeclared postulate \(K=\varphi^{-1}\) and on the structural rung assignments of F8. Within that model layer it is acceptable; as stated, it reads more final than the proof supports.

- Lines 589-595, \(\Lambda\)-ansatz. As conditional algebra from explicitly stated assumptions, the formula \(2\varphi^{-583}\) follows. What does not follow is any stronger wording than “ansatz.” The proof is just bookkeeping across prior assumptions and model choices.

- Lines 594, 617: “matches … to about \(0.88\%\).” This is not reproducible from the rounded benchmark printed in the paper. \(2\varphi^{-583}\approx 2.892249\times10^{-122}\); against the displayed comparator \(2.88\times10^{-122}\), the relative error is about \(0.43\%\), not \(0.88\%\). If \(0.88\%\) is intended, the exact observational benchmark must be stated.

- Lines 626-640, “Cascade Summary.” This is only as good as the weakest earlier items. In particular item (6) says F6 is “proposed,” but item (639) then packages the \(\Lambda\) formula too cleanly. The summary still overstates closure.

**2. Internal Consistency**

- Lines 66 and 261 say “seven non-trivial rungs,” but line 283 correctly says “six non-trivial reductions.” With the displayed chain \(E_8,H_4,40,D_4,16,8,0\), either there are seven rungs total including \(0\), or six non-trivial rungs excluding \(0\). The terminology is unstable.

- Line 176 treats \(\overline E\) as already “obtained by the Gromov--Hausdorff limit” and as a smooth 4-manifold before F6 has established anything. Later F6 is only conditional. The manuscript repeatedly imports the continuum limit as background, then later downgrades it.

- F2 depends forward on F7: line 207 excludes \((\Phi^\mu{}_\mu)^2\) by appeal to “the Fierz--Pauli gauge-fixed setup adopted in \S\ref{sec:F7}.” That means F2 is not independent of F7 despite the paper’s staged architecture.

- F4 depends forward on F7 as well. Definition 4.1 hard-codes the tensor sector at \(D_4\), then F7 is later invoked to justify it. This is circular, or at minimum badly ordered.

- The notation around \(F\) is unstable. In Section 2, \(F\) is a continuum functional on fields over \(\overline E\). In F5 and the \(\Lambda\) derivation, it is treated as something that can be restricted to discrete \(H_4\) copies and summed over root decompositions. Those are different objects unless you define a discretisation/restriction map.

- Cross-references mostly exist, but several do not resolve semantically. The worst is line 693: “follows as Theorem~\ref{thm:lambda},” when \(\ref{thm:lambda}\) is a proposition, not a theorem.

**6. Top Three Fixes**

1. Fix F3. Lines 256-287 currently say both “preferred structural model, not a theorem” and “each step is forced.” You need to choose. As written, the proof does not establish the chain, let alone uniqueness. Recast F3 explicitly as a conjectural/model-selection section, and remove the pseudo-proof language at lines 283-287.

2. Downgrade F6 and every place that depends on it. Lines 428-444 do not prove GH convergence. Line 98 is plainly false; lines 121, 443, 447, 658, 666 are mutually inconsistent about status. Unless you can actually prove BI1 and BI2, F6 must be labelled as a conjecture/conditional lemma and the introduction/discussion must be rewritten accordingly.

3. Clean up the logical hierarchy around F2/F4/F7/F8/\(\Lambda\). F2 is not a theorem-level classification (lines 212-222); F4 is a tautological count from a definition that already bakes in F7 (lines 300-315, 350-352); the shell amplitude \(K=\varphi^{-1}\) is an unsupported extra postulate (lines 319-323); and the \(\Lambda\) formula relies on a product rule not derived anywhere (line 590). If you want this paper to survive scrutiny, separate “proved,” “defined,” and “assumed” with discipline.

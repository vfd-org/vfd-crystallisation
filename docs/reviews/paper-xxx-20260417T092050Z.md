**1. Claim Audit**

1. “Then … \(P(\Phi_i\mid\Ocal)=\cdots=|c_i|^2\)” ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:291)). The proof does not establish the claim as stated. The hypotheses are approximate at best: line 294 assumes `\rho \approx \Pst`, line 295 assumes merely “negligible overlap,” and line 296 assumes sector support. Yet lines 299–305 conclude exact equalities. Worse, the proof at lines 308–310 omits the measurement-sampling hypothesis stated at lines 274–280, which is load-bearing. This proposition should be rewritten with `\approx` throughout unless stronger hypotheses are added.

2. “Chentsov 1972; Petz quantum extension” ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:438)). As stated, this is not proved here, and part (2) is not obviously a “Petz quantum extension” of part (1); it is a homogeneous/Kähler-geometry fact about \(\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\). The attribution is sloppy. If you want this theorem in the paper, split the classical, projective-Kähler, and real-slice facts and cite the right sources separately.

3. “Closure Hessian as the Fisher metric … in the Laplace regime” ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:456)). This one is mostly fine as a local asymptotic claim. The proof establishes a leading-order Gaussian identity on a translated location family, not a metric on \(\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\). The statement itself is careful about that. No major objection, beyond the need to keep the scope local and asymptotic.

4. “Then the closure-induced Riemannian normalisation … coincides … with the Fubini–Study metric … Consequently … the Kähler structure on \(\Mcl\) used by Theorem~\ref{thm:born_unique} is the Fubini–Study structure of Paper XXI” ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:507)). This is overstated. (K1) already assumes Paper XXI supplies a global \(U(N)\)-invariant Kähler structure, and (K3) only fixes a local real-slice normalisation. The proof at lines 517–519 does not derive a global closure-induced Kähler structure; it normalises an imported one.

5. “The sector probability assignment … takes the same value in both contexts” ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:547)). This is established, but only inside the very narrow class actually assumed: localised probe observers with the same canonical probe. The theorem is fine if that restriction is kept explicit.

6. “Then the restriction of \(P\) … satisfies axiom (U2)” ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:559)). Not fully established. Theorem \ref{thm:noncontextuality} gives context-independence on the restricted class; the proof of normalisation at line 564 imports basin disjointness and sector-supported decomposition from Route C. So this is not a clean corollary of the preceding theorem; it is a hybrid result with extra assumptions.

7. “Then \(P\) is the Born measure” ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:591)). As abstract Hilbert-space mathematics, the proof is basically a repackaged Gleason argument and is acceptable if one grants the hypotheses. As a VFD claim, it is not established: the paper has not shown that (i) \(\Mcl(\Ocal)=\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\), (ii) full \(U(N)\)-covariance applies to the relevant probability assignment, or (iii) the derived XXIX non-contextuality covers all bases required by Gleason. The theorem is valid only as an explicitly external conditional theorem, not as something already forced by the VFD construction.

8. “Physically, \(\Mcl=\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\) … automatically satisfied in the equilibrium tangent regime of Paper XXI” ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:613)). No. Unitary dynamics on a Hilbert space does not imply every pure state satisfies your observer-admissibility support condition from Definition \ref{def:closure_manifold}. This is an unsupported leap.

9. “\(Q\) … satisfies axiom (U2) iff \(p=2\)” ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:640)). This is established. The counterexample is concrete and sufficient.

10. “The unique solution is the latitude function” for \(N=2\) ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:674)). Unsupported. No proof is given, and no citation is supplied for the uniqueness statement under the listed hypotheses.

11. “The mechanism is demonstrated in a two-basin toy model” ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My%20Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:819)). The arithmetic is fine, but “demonstrated” is too strong. The example itself admits at lines 711–735 that the asymmetric state is inserted by hand.

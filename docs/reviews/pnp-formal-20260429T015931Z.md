**1. Claim Audit**
Line refs are to `papers/millennium-pnp-formal/pnp-formal.tex`.

- “\(\mathrm{P}_{\sigma}^{\rm cas} \subsetneq \mathrm{NP}^{\rm cas}\)” under \(H_{\sigma\text{-irr-NP}}\) and \(B_{\sigma\text{-P}}\), lines 173-181: only conditionally plausible. The later proof path drops \(B_{\sigma\text{-P}}\) at lines 402-410 and incorrectly says the proof uses \(H_{\sigma\text{-irr-NP}}\) plus structural completeness at lines 433-435. Not established as written.

- “Under \(H_{\sigma\text{-irr-NP}}\), \(B_{\sigma\text{-P}}\), and Conjecture 5.1, \(P\neq NP\),” lines 192-197: not established. The proof at lines 501-525 asserts generic random-instance absence of all structures, then concludes a language lower bound. Density statements do not imply language non-membership in P.

- CT equivalence, lines 253-255: attributed, not proved. The cited bibliography entry gives no local source path, so I cannot verify it locally.

- Generic σ-irreducibility, lines 275-303: false as stated. The theorem says “any \(\alpha>0\)” but the proof only yields decay for \(\alpha>1\), and the per-clause probability bound \(\le 1/2\) is asserted, not proved.

- Generic 3-SAT corollary, lines 307-310: only follows if the previous theorem is repaired and the per-clause bound is supplied.

- \(L_{\sigma\text{-red}}\in NP^{cas}\), lines 363-367: not established. A partition alone is not enough unless the universal restriction condition can be checked in polynomial time. As written, checking all assignments on \(A\) is exponential.

- \(L_{\sigma\text{-irr}}\in coNP^{cas}\), lines 368-369 and 711-712: not established, because it depends on the previous unsupported NP-containment claim for reducibility.

- \(H_{\sigma\text{-irr-NP}}\) “at least as hard as NP=coNP,” lines 379-389: overclaimed. The implication is asserted for “any problem whose structure exhibits full Bell combinatorics,” but no such reduction is given.

- Conditional NP-containment lemma, lines 392-398: tautologically established.

- Separation corollary, lines 402-405: wrong hypothesis list. It must also assume \(B_{\sigma\text{-P}}\). The proof uses “Theorem” \(\ref{thm:sigmaPlim}\), but that object is a hypothesis.

- Cascade structural completeness conjecture, lines 455-460: acceptable as conjecture. The “equals (c1)+...+(c5)” statement at line 492 is not formal; the subclauses are not equivalently stated.

- Barrier propositions, lines 531-589: too strong. BGS especially fails: an oracle for \(L_{\sigma\text{-irr}}\) would immediately change the relativized situation. The RR/AW discussion supports “not obviously the standard barrier form,” not “evades.”

- “Cascade evades three barriers,” lines 593-597: not a theorem. This contradicts the later demotion at lines 718-723.

- Spectral count, lines 619-634: the numbers \(94/120=78.3\%\) and \(26/120=21.7\%\) are consistent with the local script and I confirmed the spectrum script prints them. But “exact in \(\mathbb Q(\sqrt5)\) arithmetic” is not locally proved by that script; it uses numerical eigendecomposition.

- Finite-noise diagnostic, lines 648-667: acceptable only as sim-tested/diagnostic. It is not evidence for \(H_{\sigma\text{-irr-NP}}\) without the unstated mode-to-witness bridge, which the paper does acknowledge at lines 681-692.

- ARIA subsection, lines 777-803: non-load-bearing scope is preserved. It does not discharge the bridge hypotheses and does not claim unconditional \(P\neq NP\). But “same family” and “select the family member” are programme-positioning claims, not mathematical claims proved here.

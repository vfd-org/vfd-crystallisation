**1. Claim audit**
- Lines 47-50, 108-118: “The three neutrinos sit at cascade shell depths … [134,143] … heaviest at \(n\approx139\).” The arithmetic does not support this. Using the paper’s own formula \(n=\log_\varphi(m_P/m)\) with \(m_P=1.22\times10^{28}\,\mathrm{eV}\), one gets \(n(1\,\mathrm{eV})\approx134.39\), \(n(0.05\,\mathrm{eV})\approx140.62\), \(n(0.009\,\mathrm{eV})\approx144.18\). The stated band is too low by about 1.5–2 shells.
- Lines 73-83: the shell-mass relation \(m_i=m_P\varphi^{-n_i}\) does not reproduce the charged-lepton shell indices listed immediately below it. With the stated \(m_P\), the formula gives roughly \(n_e\approx107.1\), \(n_\mu\approx96.0\), \(n_\tau\approx90.1\), not \(115,104,98\). So the basic setup is internally broken before the neutrino discussion starts.
- Lines 144-155: Proposition “Normal neutrino ordering.” Not proved. The argument is: charged leptons are ordered, assume neutrinos inherit the same selector, therefore neutrinos are ordered. That is a restatement of the hypothesis, not a derivation.
- Lines 180-194: Proposition “\(\theta_{12}=\arctan(1/\varphi)\).” Not proved. No explicit map is given from the \(A_5\) action on five 24-cells to a 3-dimensional neutrino flavour space, no basis is fixed, and no calculation turns the group action into the PMNS angle. This is analogy to golden-ratio flavour models, not derivation.
- Lines 202-216: Proposition on \(\theta_{13}\) and \(\theta_{23}\). Not proved, and one formula is numerically false. \(\arctan(\pi/\varphi^3)=36.56^\circ\), not \(7.42^\circ\). That invalidates the stated prediction outright. \(\theta_{23}=\pi/4\) is also simply asserted.
- Lines 220-230: Proposition on \(\delta_{\mathrm{CP}}=-\pi/2\). Not proved. The text offers a motif (“cascade-natural phase”) and defers the derivation elsewhere.
- Lines 246-257: Corollary “\(\theta_{\mathrm{QCD}}=0\).” The proof does not establish the stated physical claim. At best it says: if the closure functional excludes \(\sigma\)-odd terms, and if the QCD \(\theta\)-operator is represented inside that functional, and if \(\sigma\) acts as CP on the QCD sector, then the model omits that operator. None of the physically nontrivial steps is proved here.
- Lines 259-265: “No axion needed” is stronger than what the argument supports. Even if the model excludes a tree-level \(\theta\)-term, that does not justify the universal statement that any axion detection falsifies the corollary.
- Lines 274-281: P1–P8 overstate the paper. P1 inherits the bad shell arithmetic and falsely reads as a per-eigenstate lower bound. P5 inherits the broken \(\theta_{13}\) formula. P8 is overclaimed.

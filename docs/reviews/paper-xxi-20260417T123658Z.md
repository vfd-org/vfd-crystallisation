**1. Claim Audit**
- `"The closure programme establishes an exact nonlinear paired-process wave equation whose equilibrium state has vanishing residual, and whose Fréchet linearisation at that equilibrium is the Schrödinger equation with Witten Hamiltonian \HW."` (lines 140-142)  
Verdict: overstated. The exact nonlinear wave equation and vanishing residual are supported by XVIII/XIX/XX. The `Fréchet linearisation` part is not established at the level the wording suggests. The upstream papers argue an `O(\varepsilon)` near-equilibrium reduction (XVIII lines 319-353; XX lines 181-190), but they do not give a clean functional-analytic linearisation theorem with precise space, derivative, and remainder control. If you mean “formal/leading-order tangent dynamics”, say that.

- `"The equilibrium-tangent Schrödinger dynamics ... is a Hamiltonian flow on \mathbb{CP}^{N-1} generated by the expectation-value function ... with respect to the symplectic form~\eqref{eq:omega_from_h}."` (lines 208-210)  
Verdict: mathematically standard, but the proof as written does not establish the stated claim cleanly. `\eqref{eq:omega_from_h}` is the ambient form on `T_\Psi \mathcal H_O` (lines 193-198), not the descended form on projective space. The proof jumps straight to the standard projective formula and suppresses the quotient/horizontal-space step and normalization issue. True in substance, inadequately proved in the manuscript’s own notation.

- `"Every unitary U \in U(\mathcal H_O)=U(N) acts on \mathbb{CP}^{N-1} as a Kähler isometry ... Conversely, the isometry group ... is exactly \mathrm{PU}(N)."` (lines 222-224)  
Verdict: first half is fine modulo the same quotient-slippage; second half is not proved, only cited. If you want to keep the converse in a proposition, state it explicitly as an imported standard theorem, not as something proved here.

- `"For any self-adjoint A ... the time-t flow e^{-iAt} of the Hamiltonian function h_A ... is a unitary on \mathcal H_O and therefore a Kähler isometry of \mathbb{CP}^{N-1}."` (lines 230-231)  
Verdict: acceptable only after fixing Proposition 13. As written it conflates the Hilbert-space flow with the induced projective flow. The projective statement is standard; the present proof is too compressed to carry the distinction.

- `"Pure states of the equilibrium-tangent dynamics, restricted to \mathcal H_O, are unit vectors in \mathcal H_O modulo global phase."` (lines 185-189)  
Verdict: hidden assumption. The paper has shifted from “tangent perturbations around `\Psi_{\mathrm{st}}`” (lines 166, 169) to ordinary rays in an abstract finite-dimensional sector subspace without explaining the identification. A tangent space at equilibrium is not automatically the same object as a sector-superposition Hilbert space.

**2. Internal Consistency**
- The worst slippage is between `equilibrium tangent perturbations` and `sector-superposition pure states`. Lines 169-177 speak about the linear space of perturbations around `\Psi_{\mathrm{st}}`; lines 171-173 define `\mathcal H_O` as spanned by sector basis vectors associated with basins; lines 185-189 then treat rays in `\mathcal H_O` as “pure states of the equilibrium-tangent dynamics.” That bridge is missing. You need an explicit map from perturbations `\delta\Psi` near equilibrium to normalized states in `\mathcal H_O`, or else stop calling the projective construction the equilibrium tangent.

- The notation for the symplectic form is inconsistent. Lines 193-198 define ambient `g,\omega` on `T_\Psi \mathcal H_O`, then say these descend to `\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}`. Proposition 12 at line 209 cites `\eqref{eq:omega_from_h}`, i.e. the ambient form, while claiming Hamiltonian flow on projective space. That cross-reference points to the wrong geometric object.

- The manuscript oscillates between exactness and approximation. Lines 141-142, 155, 322, 393 present the Schrödinger equation as a cleanly established tangent limit; Table 1 at lines 301 and 306 is more careful and says XVIII gives only leading-order near-equilibrium Schrödinger and XX proves the tangent limit. The stronger phrasing should be brought down to the weaker one unless you are prepared to add a real linearisation theorem.

- In-file `\ref`/`\eqref` labels appear to exist. I did not find an unresolved-label problem. The problem is semantic, not mechanical: `\eqref{eq:omega_from_h}` does not say what Proposition 12 says it says.

**4. Tightness**
- Line 70: `"The programme supports a qualified positive answer"`  
Better: `The programme supports a conditional, regime-restricted positive answer.`

- Lines 140-142: `"establishes ... whose Fréchet linearisation ... is the Schrödinger equation"`  
Better: `identifies, at formal/leading order near equilibrium, a tangent Schrödinger dynamics with Witten Hamiltonian \HW.`

- Line 209: `"is a Hamiltonian flow on \mathbb{CP}^{N-1} ... with respect to the symplectic form~\eqref{eq:omega_from_h}"`  
Better: `induces the standard projective Hamiltonian flow on \mathbb{CP}^{N-1} with respect to the descended symplectic form.`

- Line 222 title: `"$U(N)$ preserves the equilibrium-tangent Kähler structure"`  
Better: `The induced projective action of U(N) preserves the equilibrium-tangent Kähler structure.`

- Lines 385-396: `"demonstrates a mathematically consistent pathway"`  
Better: `lays out a mathematically coherent formal pathway, conditional on the stated hypotheses and standard geometric-QM identifications.`

**5. Surface Issues**
- Lines 208-210: wrong object referenced. `\eqref{eq:omega_from_h}` is the ambient form, not the descended projective symplectic form actually used in the proposition.

- Lines 222-224: group language is sloppy. The actual effective action on projective space is by `PU(N)`, not literally by `U(N)`; the manuscript knows this by line 223 but the proposition title obscures it.

- Lines 169-177, 185-189: terminology is unstable. `equilibrium tangent`, `linear space of perturbations`, `finite-dimensional sector-superposition subspace`, and `pure states` are being used as if they were interchangeable.

- Lines 51, 166, 180, 245: `Paper XXX` is discussed repeatedly without formal bibliography citation, unlike XVI-XX. Not fatal, but stylistically inconsistent.

- The paper repeatedly says `Fréchet` where the actual proofs support only first-order perturbative control. That is not a typo; it is a surface symptom of the central overclaim.

**6. Top Three Fixes**
1. Fix the conceptual jump in the geometric section (lines 169-177, 185-189). Explain exactly how the equilibrium-tangent perturbation space around `\Psi_{\mathrm{st}}` becomes the finite-dimensional sector-superposition Hilbert space `\mathcal H_O`, or stop presenting the projective construction as the equilibrium tangent itself.

2. Stop overselling the Schrödinger-limit theorem (lines 140-142, 148-152, 301-306, 390-394). Either prove an actual linearisation theorem with hypotheses and remainder estimates, or downgrade the language to `formal`, `leading-order`, or `perturbative near equilibrium`.

3. Rewrite the geometric propositions with correct projective notation and honest proof status (lines 193-209, 222-236). Distinguish ambient vs descended forms, say `projective action of U(N)`/`PU(N)`, and label the converse isometry statement as a cited standard theorem rather than pretending to prove it.

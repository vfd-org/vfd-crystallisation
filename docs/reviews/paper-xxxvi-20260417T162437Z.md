**1. Claim Audit**

- “Under Axiom~\ref{ax:self-sim} … \(r=\varphi\) … globally attracting” (lines 129-138). This is the one claim the paper actually proves. The only gap is the hand-wave at line 158 (“a straightforward check”), but the omitted check is elementary and does not threaten the result.

- “Any admissible closure functional \(F\) … takes the form \(\alpha R+\beta E-\gamma Q\)” (lines 212-217). Not established. The proof at lines 220-221 is tautological: once the paper excludes every competing invariant by fiat at lines 203-207, only the chosen three remain. This does not prove a canonical form; it restates a modelling choice. The introduction’s “decomposes uniquely” language at line 76 is therefore false.

- “Under … conditions (a)-(c) … one proposed chain is \(E_8\to H_4\to 40\to D_4\to 16\to 8\to 0\)” (lines 256-261). Only partially established, and the section title “from Coxeter Classification” overstates what is done. Step 1 is classical. Steps 2, 4, and 5 (lines 269-279) are asserted with citations and interpretation, not proved. The proposition is defensible only if read as “here is the preferred programme chain”; it is not a derivation.

- “The total closure depth … is \(583\)” (lines 330-339). The arithmetic is correct from Definition 300-315, but the theorem proves only a count built into that definition. The substantive choice is the inclusion of all \(24^2\) tensor-product modes at \(D_4\), especially the antisymmetric 276 modes (lines 310-314). That is not derived.

- “\(F\) is a \(\sigma\)-intertwiner” (lines 372-377). Not cleanly established. Definition 180-188 makes \(F[\Phi]\in\mathbb R\), so \(\sigma\cdot F[\Phi]\) is trivial unless the codomain is silently enlarged. The proof also never makes precise how the continuum objects on \(\overline E\) inherit the Galois action.

- “\(F|_{E_8}=F|_{H_4}+F|_{H_4'}=2F|_{H_4}\)” (lines 388-396). Not established. The paper defines \(F\) as a continuum integral over \(\overline E\), not as a sum over a root partition. The restriction \(F|_{H_4}\) is never defined, and the disjointness of the 240 roots does not by itself imply additivity of the continuum functional.

- Burago-Ivanov restatement (lines 415-421). I did not verify the textbook wording here, but that is not the main problem. The application fails first.

- “\((X_n,\delta_n)\to(S^3,d_{\rm round})\) in GH sense, with \(\varepsilon_n\le \varepsilon_0 2^{-n}\)” (lines 428-433). Not proved. The rate is explicitly assumed at line 439. GH convergence itself also does not follow from what is written: density of a subgroup of \(\mathrm{SO}(4)\) is not a proof that these specific \(X_n\) are \(\varepsilon_n\)-nets, and BI2 is reduced to an unnamed “standard sphere-polygonal estimate” at line 441.

- Fierz-Pauli uniqueness theorem (lines 464-466). Imported external result, uncited, not proved here.

- Deser theorem as written with \(G_{\mu\nu}+\Lambda g_{\mu\nu}\) (lines 468-472). Imported external result, not proved here. The paper partly corrects the overreach at lines 494-495 by admitting Deser does not generate a nonzero \(\Lambda\).

- “The closure contribution carrying \(\Lambda\) localises at the \(D_4\) rung” (lines 487-488). Not really proved. Hypothesis (ii) already assumes rank-2 content localises at the unique rung admitting such content, namely \(D_4\). The proposition largely restates its hypotheses.

- “The three coefficients … are assigned by the action-normalisation matching scheme” (lines 551-558). As an assignment scheme, this is fine. As a derivation from the cascade, it is not. The statement is honest enough; the danger is downstream citation inflation.

- “No additional free numerical parameter remains” (lines 565-566). Over-claimed. Even after importing \((G,\alpha_{\rm em},\sin^2\theta_W)\), the framework still contains discrete structural choices and exclusions: F2 exclusions, F3 chain choice, F4 rung content, F8 rung assignments, and later the product-closure rule. “No additional continuous parameter” would be the defensible version.

- “\(\Lambda\ell_P^2=2\varphi^{-583}\)” (lines 589-594). Not derived; only restated from assumptions. The proof uses three extra inputs not obtained in the paper: \(K=\varphi^{-1}\) (lines 320-323), the product-closure rule (line 590), and residue-to-\(\Lambda\) identification (line 590).

- Numerical claim “\(2\varphi^{-583}\approx2.892\times10^{-122}\)” (lines 592, 617). Arithmetic is fine.

- Numerical claim “matches … to about \(0.88\%\)” (lines 594, 617). Not reproduced by the printed numbers. Against \(2.866\times10^{-122}\), the discrepancy is about \(0.92\%\), and the benchmark is admitted to be ambiguous.

- Numerical claims for \(\alpha,\beta,\gamma\) (lines 523, 535, 546). \(\alpha\) is fine. \(\gamma\) and \(\beta\) are numerically off for the stated formulas and \(\alpha_{\rm em}^{-1}=137+\pi/87\): they are about \(2.7262\) and \(1.0223\), not \(2.7255\) and \(1.0221\).

- “Cascade Summary” (lines 626-639). This is only as good as the preceding claims. At present it packages modelling assumptions as a theorem-like summary.

**2. Internal Consistency**

- The abstract is internally inconsistent with the body. Line 57 says only F1 is a rigorous theorem and that F4 is “formulated as a proposition”; in the body F4 is a theorem (lines 330-340), and the discussion treats it as a definition-level count (lines 654-656).

- The introduction states “the closure density decomposes uniquely” (line 76) and “the rung sequence … is itself forced, not postulated” (line 77). Later the paper explicitly says F2 is a structural model choice (lines 206-207) and F3 is “the preferred structural model, not a theorem” (line 261). Those statements cannot coexist.

- Line 98 says “the Burago--Ivanov bound F6 closes the GH-convergence step.” The body of F6 explicitly leaves key parts assumed or deferred (lines 439-447). The introduction overstates.

- Line 320 claims \(K=\varphi^{-1}\) follows from F1 plus F2 minimality. F2’s minimality condition is about derivative order and polynomial degree (lines 185, 320), not about a \(\varphi\)-power shell amplitude. This is a non sequitur.

- Line 326 says “summing contributions over the \(\dim\Vcal\) blocks gives the total residue.” The \(\Lambda\) proposition later uses a product rule, not a sum (lines 590, 598-600). This is a direct contradiction.

- Definition 180-188 makes \(F\) real-valued, but F5 uses a nontrivial Galois action on \(F[\Phi]\) (lines 372-383). The formalism is inconsistent unless the codomain is changed.

- Line 511 sets \(c=\hbar=1\) and adopts \(\ell_P^2=G\), “so that \(G=1\) in these units”. F8 then treats \(G\) as an imported input at line 552. That is sloppy at best.

- Line 501 says F4 and F7 “combine to localise” tensor-product content at \(D_4\), but F4’s definition already hard-codes that localisation at lines 301-315. The logical order is backwards.

- Syntactically, the cross-references are fine: all `\ref`, `\eqref`, and `\citep` targets in this file resolve.

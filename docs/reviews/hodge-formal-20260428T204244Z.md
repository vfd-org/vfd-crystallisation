**Claim Audit**
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:204`: “Assume `H_{4a}` and `H_{4a}^{alg}`… the Hodge Conjecture for `X` holds.” This is essentially tautological as stated. `H_{4a}^{alg}//X` at `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:185` already asserts that the relevant fixed image on `X` is algebraic. The proof never establishes that every Hodge class lies in that image except by the coefficient-level fixed/rational identification. The statement is acceptable only if advertised as “HC follows from an axiom equivalent to the remaining algebraicity problem,” not as a substantive reduction.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:180`: “Every σ-fixed, Q-rational class in `H^*(Ω,Q(φ))` is algebraic on `Ω`.” This is ill-defined. `Ω` is earlier described as the `E_8` root system/substrate in `R^8`, not a smooth projective algebraic variety with algebraic cycles and cohomology. The parenthetical “viewed as an algebraic variety / scheme” is not a definition.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:195`: “`H_{4a}^{alg}/Ω ⇒ H_{4a}^{alg}/X` whenever `ι_X` pulls back algebraic cycles…” This does not follow. Pullback algebraicity of classes on `Ω` proves algebraicity of classes in the image of `ι_X^*`; it does not prove that an arbitrary σ-fixed class on `X` is the pullback of a σ-fixed class on `Ω`. A surjectivity hypothesis on `ι_X^*` over the relevant Hodge/fixed classes is missing.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:355`: “σ is an involution commuting with cup product and the Hodge decomposition.” Coefficientwise σ does commute with cup product. The Hodge-decomposition statement is fine over `Q(φ)` if one explicitly extends scalars, but the proof uses the undefined object `H^{p,q}(X,Z[φ])` at `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:375`; Hodge pieces are complex vector spaces, not integral-coefficient cohomology groups.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:382`: “The subspace of `H^*(X,Z[φ])` fixed by σ is exactly `H^*(X,Z)`.” False without a torsion-free hypothesis or derived/tensor qualification. The displayed equality `H^*(X,Z[φ]) = H^*(X,Z)⊗Z[φ]` at `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:388` is not automatic for integral cohomology with arbitrary torsion; universal coefficient/Tor issues are ignored. The later rational corollary is the safe theorem.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:405`: “`{α ∈ H^*(X,Q[φ]) : σα=α}=H^*(X,Q)`.” Correct as coefficient-level linear algebra, but it should use `Q(φ)`, not `Q[φ]`, for consistency with the rest of the paper.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:442`: Hodge for `CP^n`. Proof establishes the claim.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:478`: Bruhat decomposition. Standard, but the setup at `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:468` says “complex Lie group” while examples use compact real forms `U(n)`; the theorem needs `G` connected complex reductive/algebraic and `P` parabolic.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:490`: “`H^*(G/P,Z)` has basis `{[\bar C_w]}`.” The indexing/codimension convention is wrong as written. A Schubert cell of complex dimension `ℓ(w)` has closure of dimension `ℓ(w)`, so its Poincaré dual is in cohomological degree `2(dim G/P - ℓ(w))`, not `2ℓ(w)` as claimed at `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:488`, unless opposite Schubert varieties/codimensions are being used.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:534`: Danilov theorem. The second bullet is suspect: rank of `H^{2p}` is not simply “the number of `p`-dimensional cones” in general. The generation by invariant cycle classes is the relevant true statement; the rank assertion should be removed or replaced by the correct Chow/cohomology presentation.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:547`: Hodge for smooth complete toric varieties. The proof works if the corrected Danilov generation statement is used.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:578` and `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:594`: CM abelian status. These claims are cited only and are appropriately weakened; no proof is attempted.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:641`: “For smooth complete intersections of low codimension, HC holds outside middle; selected cases in middle.” The theorem statement is cautious enough, but the proof overclaims. Lines `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:663`–`papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:670` assert algebraic cycles for primitive cohomology in both codimension 1 and 2 and conclude full HC. That contradicts the theorem’s “selected cases” limitation and the earlier honesty at `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:274`.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:683`: “Hodge for cascade-embeddable, conditional on `H_{4a}^{alg}`.” Not proved. The proof’s key sentence at `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:699`–`papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:703` again assumes every σ-fixed class on `X` is pulled back from `Ω`. This is the same missing surjectivity/transport hypothesis as above.
- `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:719`: “Cascade universality follows from cascade realizing Langlands…” The proof sketch does not establish the theorem. “Every projective Kähler manifold has a motive” is not a theorem in the stated generality, “motive gives automorphic representation via Langlands” is a major open conjectural package, and “realizes the motive” does not imply an embedding into the cascade substrate.

Locally supported by `papers/cascade-derivation/scripts/test_sigma_attractor_spectrum.py`, but the script itself describes numerical eigendecomposition and heuristic classification rather than the “exact arithmetic” claim made at `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:807`.

It directly contradicts `papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:251`–`papers/millennium-hodge-formal/hodge-formal.tex:287`.

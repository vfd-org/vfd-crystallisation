**2. Internal consistency**

- The paper oscillates between state-specific completeness and observer-wide completeness. Line 153 treats “complete observer” as `\Mcl(\Ocal)=\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}` ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:153)); line 196 defines completeness “for a state `\Psi`” ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:196)). Those are not equivalent. One is global, one is state-relative.

- The paper says `"Standard quantum predictions remain unchanged at the operational level"` ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:787)), then later advertises operationally distinguishable partial-observer predictions ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:936)). Pick one. The correct statement is “unchanged for complete observers.”

- The narrative around `(U2)` is internally slippery. Abstract and introduction language imply non-contextuality is “imported/derived” from XXIX ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:53), [paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:102)); the actual derivation only covers a restricted observer class ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:600)), while the forcing theorem needs full frame-function non-contextuality over all bases ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:640)).

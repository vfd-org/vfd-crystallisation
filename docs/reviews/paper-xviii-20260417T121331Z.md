**1. Claim Audit**
- Lines 115-118, “The probability density $\rho$ and current velocity $v$ satisfy $\partial_t\rho+\nabla\cdot(\rho v)=0$.” The proof at lines 122-128 is fine as a formal identity, assuming the Fokker–Planck equation holds and $\rho$ is smooth enough. Missing hypotheses: differentiability and a setting where the divergence theorem manipulations make sense.
- Lines 136-140, “The current velocity $v$ … is a gradient field: $v=\nabla S$.” Only true on the region where $\rho>0$ so that $\log\rho$ is defined. The proposition states a global claim without saying what happens at zeros of $\rho$ or on nontrivial topology. As written, it over-claims.
- Lines 162-168, closure Madelung pair. The algebra at lines 171-192 is correct formally, but again only away from zeros of $\rho$. The terms $\log\rho$ and $\Delta\sqrt{\rho}/\sqrt{\rho}$ force a nonvanishing-amplitude hypothesis that is nowhere stated.
- Lines 220-228, “The Madelung pair … is equivalent to the nonlinear wave equation.” This is only a formal Madelung recombination. The proof at lines 231-242 invokes the “standard Madelung correspondence” without stating the regularity/nonvanishing assumptions under which the equivalence is valid. It does not establish a theorem on the full stated state space.
- Lines 291-293, “The Nelson pairing … equips the tangent space to the admissible-wavefunction manifold … with an almost-complex structure.” What is actually proved at lines 295-297 is only that multiplication by $i$ on the formal amplitude-phase tangent decomposition satisfies $J^2=-\mathrm{id}$. The manifold is not defined, the tangent space is not constructed, and the result is essentially tautological once one has written a complex wavefunction.
- Lines 299-316 contain the worst mathematics in the paper. “Newlander–Nirenberg … requires that the dynamics preserving $J$ is linear” (lines 299-300) is false. “At the equilibrium tangent, the Nijenhuis tensor of $J$ vanishes (since $J$ is covariantly constant under a linear Hamiltonian flow)” (lines 305-306) is not proved and is conceptually muddled. Integrability is a property of the almost-complex structure, not of the flow being linear. This section does not establish what it says it establishes.
- Lines 325-338, equilibrium/near-equilibrium Schrödinger regime. Stage 1 is overstated: line 328 calls $U[\Psi_{\mathrm{st}}]=\sigma^2V_W$ a “constant,” but $V_W$ is generally a function of $\psi$. What is shown at lines 342-353 is only that the nonlinear equation coincides with the linear Witten equation on the equilibrium solution. Stage 2 shows only $U=\sigma^2V_W+O(\varepsilon)$ at lines 355-359. That is a first-order expansion, not a rigorous linearisation theorem.
- Lines 397-409, quadratic example. The spectrum claim $E_n=nk$ is correct for the shifted oscillator. The comparison with XVII at lines 413-426 is consistent with the cited paper, but “Norm preserved” in Table 1 is only true for the linearised regime, not for the full nonlinear paired dynamics.

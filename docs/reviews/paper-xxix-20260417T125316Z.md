**1. Claim Audit**

- `“The basin \(B_\phi\) is determined by the pair \((\Fcal,\phi)\) alone”` ([lines 202-204]). The proof at [206-208] establishes only the narrow statement that the defining ODE and convergence test do not mention other minima. That is fine as far as it goes. Hidden hypothesis: Definition  [195-199] never states the regularity assumptions needed for the gradient flow to be well-posed. You need at least enough smoothness for existence/uniqueness of trajectories.

- `“two localised probe observers … assign identical (in fact, vanishing) coherence cost”` ([247-256]). The proof at [259-266] is correct, but only because Definition `\ref{def:probe}` has already built the conclusion in by fiat: condition (3) forces every probe functional to vanish on every admitted basin. So Theorem `\ref{thm:lmc}` is not an independent result; it is a restatement of the definition. You acknowledge this at [268-269], but the abstract and later prose still market it as a substantive theorem.

- `“the observer-admissible subset of \(B_\phi\) … is all of \(B_\phi\) for both observers”` ([256]). This follows from the theorem, since \(0\le \epsilon\) with \(\epsilon>0\) from [168]. No issue.

- `“the admissible subset of \(B_\phi\) is the same for both observers”` ([272-277]). The proof at [280-281] is correct, but trivial for the same reason: both functionals are identically zero on the whole basin. This is not a context-independence result of any depth; it is a consequence of making admissibility on admitted basins vacuous.

- `“Paper~XXX identifies a sector-probability assignment with integrated stationary mass over an observer-admissible basin … Non-contextuality of probability is therefore a consequence of the probe decomposition and off-basin silence”` ([292-297]). Over-claim. What you have shown here is equality of the numerator on a shared basin. Paper XXX itself requires completeness and normalization assumptions before probabilities agree, not just shared-basin admissibility; see Paper XXX [596-623]. As written, this passage suppresses the denominator and overstates what XXIX establishes.

- `“If only one of the original configurations satisfies both conditions, the system resolves to that configuration”` ([347]). Not proved. Equation [343-345] gives a compatibility condition, not a dynamical theorem of resolution. The follow-up sentence `“further constraint resolution … determines the outcome”` ([347]) is also unsupported here.

- `“The framework provides a structural account of how multiplicity reduces to a definite outcome”` ([369]). Too strong. What the paper provides is a definition of admissibility and an illustrative toy example, not a derivation of unique outcome selection for the actual framework.

- `“Paper~XXIII proved that no canonical global clock exists … In the observer-constraint framework, this has a structural explanation: no single observer region can span the entire event set while maintaining internal coherence”` ([396]). The cited first clause is supported by XXIII. The second clause is not proved here. It follows only because you defined observers to be proper subgraphs at [127], i.e. by exclusion, not by derived impossibility.

- `“The toy model … one-probe instance of Definition~\ref{def:probe}”` ([323-327]). Not really established. The toy model has only one basin exercised, so condition (3) across multiple distinguishable basins is never tested. Your own last sentence at [327] partially concedes this.

- `“With the observer constraint, only \(\Phi_A\) is admissible”` ([515]). The arithmetic at [511-512] is correct. But the setup assumes, without constructing any \(\Fcal\), that both \(\Phi_A,\Phi_B\) are closure-stable minima [499]. So the example is internally illustrative, not evidence that the full VFD dynamics produces such a pair.

**2. Internal Consistency**

- The core object types drift. An observer region is a connected subgraph `\(\Ocal \subset \Gcal\)` ([120-121]), but probe subregions are declared as subsets of the event set `\(O_{\phi_j}\subset \Ecal\)` and then summed to recover `\(\Ocal\)` ([217-228]). That is a graph/vertex-set mismatch. Either everything is vertex-induced subgraphs, or say so explicitly.

- Stability is defined at a single ambient configuration `\(\Phi_0\)` ([128-132]), but a localised probe observer is later required to have zero probe cost on every admitted basin ([229-233]). That is a much stronger multi-basin coherence requirement, and the transition from local stability at one point to vanishing on an entire union of basins is never formalised.

- The paper says probe observers “distinguish” outcomes ([217]), but the only formal quantity attached to probes, \(c_{\phi_j}\), is forced to be zero on every admitted basin, including off-target ones ([229-233], [238]). So formally the constraint functional does not distinguish outcomes at all; the distinction survives only in the prose (“matched” versus “idle” at [238]) and not in any defined observable.

- The claimed compatibility with Paper XXIV is asserted, not shown. `“naturally induces a linear extension … and a sampling measure”` ([143]) has no construction here, yet later sections rely on it as if established ([386-387], [410]).

- Manual scan suggests the internal `\ref`/`\eqref` targets used in this file do exist. I do not see a dangling internal reference. I did not compile the file.

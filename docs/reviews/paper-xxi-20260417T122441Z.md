**1. Claim audit**

- **Lines 137-139**: “*The closure programme establishes an exact nonlinear paired-process wave equation whose equilibrium state has vanishing residual, and whose Fréchet linearisation at that equilibrium is the Schrödinger equation with Witten Hamiltonian \(\HW\).*”
  Verdict: basically supported as a summary of Papers XVIII–XX, but overstated in this paper. The exact nonlinear equation, equilibrium vanishing, and near-equilibrium linearisation are upstream results. What is **not** established here is the extra finite-dimensional projective/Hilbert packaging introduced later. Keep this as a synopsis, not as a new theorem-level claim.

- **Lines 170-174**: “*Pure states … This is the complex projective manifold \(\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\).*”
  Verdict: not established from closure dynamics. This follows only **after** you assume a chosen finite-dimensional sector-superposition space \(\mathcal H_O \cong \mathbb C^N\). That assumption is load-bearing and is not proved here.

- **Lines 193-195**: Proposition “*Schrödinger as Hamiltonian flow on \(\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\).*”
  Verdict: mathematically standard, but the proof as written does not fully establish the stated claim from the setup given here.
  Problems:
  - The projective symplectic form has not been constructed carefully; lines 178-183 conflate the ambient Hilbert-space form with the reduced/projective form.
  - The proof suppresses the restriction to the unit sphere and quotient by phase, which is exactly where the geometric content lies.
  - The claim is only valid once the finite-dimensional Hilbert-space assumption is made explicit.

- **Lines 207-209**: Proposition “*\(U(N)\) preserves the equilibrium-tangent Kähler structure.*”
  Verdict: true as a standard fact about \(\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}\) with Fubini–Study structure, but not a closure-derived theorem. The forward direction is routine; the converse is merely cited. This is fine as a recalled standard result, not as a substantive new result of the programme.

- **Lines 215-217**: Corollary “*the Schrödinger flow generated by \(\HW\) … is a one-parameter subgroup of \(U(N)\).*”
  Verdict: not established as stated. Hidden hypothesis missing: you need \(\HW\) to preserve the chosen \(N\)-dimensional sector-superposition subspace \(\mathcal H_O\). Without invariance of that subspace, \(e^{-i\HW t}\) need not define a flow in \(U(N)\) on \(\mathcal H_O\).

- **Lines 292-293**: Status table: “*XXI (this) Kähler structure at equilibrium tangent — Proved*” and “*\(U(N)\) as Kähler isometry group — Proved*.”
  Verdict: over-claim. What you have done is assemble standard projective-Hilbert-space geometry in a restricted linearised regime, contingent on a finite-dimensional sector assumption. You have not proved that this geometry is generated by the closure dynamics in any strong sense.

- **Numerical results**: none.

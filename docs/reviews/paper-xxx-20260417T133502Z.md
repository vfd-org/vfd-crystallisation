**1. Claim Audit**

- "`Proposition [Conditional Born weighting from stationary sector measure]` ... `Then ... P(\Phi_i \mid \Ocal)= ... = \frac{|c_i|^2\|\phi_i\|^2}{\sum_j |c_j|^2\|\phi_j\|^2}+O(\text{overlap})`" ([paper-xxx.tex](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:323)).
Assessment: overstated as written. The proof at lines 341-343 gives a sector-mass decomposition plus assumption (4); it does not justify replacing the denominator by `\sum_j |c_j|^2\|\phi_j\|^2` unless the admitted sectors exhaust the support being normalised over. That is fine for complete observers, not for the partial-observer regime introduced earlier at lines 203-208. Hidden hypothesis: completeness, or at minimum that `\Phi_{\mathrm{adm}}` is exactly the union of the sectors appearing in the decomposition.

Assessment: false without an additional normalisation/support assumption. Normalising the `\phi_i` does not by itself make the denominator 1 in the conditional-probability formula. You need either completeness for the state or an explicit statement that the denominator is taken over all sectors in the decomposition.

**2. Internal Consistency**

- The biggest internal problem is the status of `(U2)`. The theorem at lines 650-669 assumes a probability assignment on all rank-1 projectors and all states in `\mathbb{CP}^{N-1}`, but the derivation at lines 596-624 gives only a restricted, state-complete, observer-class-specific result. The paper repeatedly slides from "restricted theorem" to "derived axiom" as if the quantifiers matched. They do not.

- There is a direct tension between the partial-observer formalism at lines 203-208 and the Route C formula at lines 333-337. One section says partial observers require renormalisation over admitted sectors; the other presents a denominator over all `j` and then calls the result Born-like. Those are not the same regime.

- Route E alternates between two descriptions that are not equivalent:
  - local closure-side metric identification only ([paper-xxx.tex:491](/mnt/c/Users/nexus/OneDrive/Documents/My Projects/vfd-crystalisation-paper/papers/paper-xxx/paper-xxx.tex:491));

  The paper later admits the global Kähler structure is imported from XXI, not derived. The stronger phrasing should be purged.

- Cross-references are technically consistent. I found no missing `\ref`/`\eqref` labels.

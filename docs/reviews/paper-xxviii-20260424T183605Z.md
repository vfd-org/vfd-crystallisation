**1. Claim Audit**

- “This paper…shows…quantum dynamics and gravitational geometry share a common origin” (54) is not proved. At most the paper proposes a structural comparison. The gravitational papers do not use the same closure functional in the way claimed here.

- “The Schrödinger equation emerges as the equilibrium tangent limit…” (61, 416) is mostly verifiable, but overstated unless qualified. Papers XVIII/XXI support exact equilibrium and formal leading-order near-equilibrium recovery, not global Schrödinger recovery.

- “The gravitational track…derives emergent time, observer kinematics, metric separation, curvature, and source-driven field dynamics” (63, 416) is too strong. XXIII gives a basepoint-dependent strict order; XXIV gives conditional kinematic analogues; XXV gives graph metrics on connected components; XXVI gives curvature indicators; XXVII introduces a graph-Poisson model. These are scaffold pieces, not derived gravitational dynamics.

- “Paper XXII established…2:1 double cover via the Coxeter projection with exponents (1,7)” (82) is inaccurate. XXII explicitly prefers “two-to-one pairing” over “double cover” and treats the exponent language as arithmetic/contextual, not the projection construction.

- The closure functional displayed in (91-93) is not the Paper XXII functional. XXII defines a log-sum-exp over squared distances to 600-cell vertices, not `sum_i log(1+exp(beta(Phi_i-mu)))`.

- The Witten potential in (94, 207) has the wrong scaling relative to XVIII/XIX/XXI: source papers use `V_W = |∇F|^2/(2σ^2) - ΔF/2`, not `1/2|∇F|^2 - σ^2 ΔF/2`.

- “In the gravitational track, F determines which vertex configurations are admissible…” (96) is not verified. XXIII states admissibility is purely geometric overlap; refined closure constraints are deferred.

- “The source field S(e)…measures deviation from uniform closure” (96) is not verified. XXVII defines sources from density, degree deviation, or geodesic traffic, not closure residuals.

- “The event set carries a natural graph…Paper XXIII” (104) is mis-cited. The transition/Hasse graph is defined in Paper XXV, not XXIII.

- “In the quantum track, the graph Laplacian Δ_G appears in the Witten Hamiltonian” (104, 214, 217) is the central unsupported claim. Route A uses a Witten Hamiltonian on the closure state space; Route B conditionally posits the 600-cell graph Laplacian. The gravitational field equation uses the event Hasse graph Laplacian. These are not shown to be the same graph or operator.

- Definition “shared substrate…(E,F,G)…all derived from the dual 600-cell system” (106-107) is not established. It is a proposed bookkeeping triple, and the cited papers do not derive this common triple.

- “Multiple overlapping configurations…system exhibits superposition” (118) is overclaimed. Paper XV has a multi-path complex-amplitude ansatz, not a derivation of quantum superposition from the relative rotation parameter θ.

- “Paper XV assigns phase frequency Ω=F/σ² and amplitude A=e^{-F/σ²} to each path” (122) is inaccurate. Paper XV assigns path amplitudes involving the Onsager-Machlup action and path-integrated F; `A=e^{-F/σ²}` is the stationary amplitude, not a generic path amplitude.

- “Time dilation is exhibited” (146) needs the XXIV hypotheses: incomparable events for simultaneity, unequal weight sums for the endpoint-interval analogue, and no connection yet to physical velocity.

- “The complete chain is source → potential → curvature → geodesic concentration” (150) is too strong. XXVII explicitly says the final arrow is an observed toy-model correlation, not a theorem.

- Table 1 (172-179) is pattern-matched, not rigorous. Especially false/unsupported: `F` as gravitational source, `Δ_G` as the quantum kinetic operator, paths on the same `G`, `F`-gradient as `S(e)`, and norm conservation as source balance.

- “Propagation follows geodesics…weighted by accessibility potential u(e)” (194) is false relative to XXVII. XXVII defines geodesics as shortest paths independent of `u`; no motion law from `u` is derived.

- “The two regimes…are connected by a continuous parameter” (224) is not proved. `η` is a finite combinatorial statistic, not a derived physical control parameter.

- The bullets “η=0…pure quantum” and “η=1…pure gravitational” (237-239) are asserted. Nothing proves antichains reproduce quantum mechanics or chains reproduce gravity.

- Proposition 1 (252-262) is not proved as stated. The proof cites an undefined bound `n! / ∏ c_i`; does not define the `c_i`; does not prove monotonic dependence of linear extensions on `η`; and does not justify “path configurations” or “dominant path class” from extension count.

- “The preceding sections establish…” (272-278) is too strong. They motivate a correspondence; they do not establish shared substrate, parallel roles, or a regime boundary.

- “Quantum behaviour and gravitational structure are not separate physical domains” (283) is a physical unification claim, not supported by the paper. The later disclaimer does not repair the overstatement.

- “Crystallisation…is the process by which…geometry transitions from quantum to gravitational” (307-317) is asserted. No dynamics shows `η` increases under crystallisation, and XXII explicitly treats crystallisation/Coxeter pairing as interpretive, not derived.

- “What is established” (329-335) overstates almost every item. The graph Laplacian and path-structure claims are particularly not established.

- “The distinction between quantum and gravitational structure is not fundamental” (422) is far beyond the paper. The supported claim is only that a possible structural analogy exists inside the VFD programme.

**2. Internal Consistency**

- `Fcal` is inconsistent: XXVIII’s equation (91-93) is not the Paper XXII functional and is then used as if it were the common object for both tracks.

- `Δ_G` is overloaded. The quantum papers use a continuous Laplacian/Witten Hamiltonian or a conditional 600-cell graph Laplacian; the gravitational papers use the Hasse graph on events. XXVIII treats these as one operator without an identification theorem.

- `Ecal` is inconsistent. In the gravitational track it is an event set of overlap pairs; in the quantum track the configuration space is closure-state space or 600-cell vertices. These are not identified.

- `θ` is overloaded: Paper XV phase, Paper XXIII relative rotation/birth angle, and XXVIII’s “ordering” parameter are treated as if they belong to one mechanism.

- `η` requires `|E| ≥ 2`; this hypothesis is missing from Definition 2.

- Cross-references appear syntactically resolvable. The problem is semantic: e.g. `Table 1` is later cited as established content even though its own remark says it is not a derivation.

**3. External Consistency**

- Paper XIV: stationary distribution `P ∝ exp(-2F/σ²)` is verified.

- Paper XV: phase/interference is verified only as a complexified-action ansatz. It does not support “superposition,” paths on `G`, or the simplified path phase/amplitude claims.

- Paper XVII: dissipative obstruction is verified.

- Paper XVIII: Nelson pairing and equilibrium/near-equilibrium Schrödinger recovery are verified, but only exact at equilibrium and formal leading order nearby; global linear recovery is explicitly denied.

- Paper XIX: norm conservation and residual properties are verified. “Gauge-invariant” should be narrowed to global phase invariance/non-removability under tested transformations.

- Paper XX: supports structural placement of the residual, but XXVIII does not cite it despite using ranges like XV-XXI/XXII.

- Paper XXI: explicitly says Route A and Route B have different Hamiltonians and are not presently identified. This directly conflicts with XXVIII’s “same graph Laplacian” claim.

- Paper XXII: verifies a two-to-one `E8 → H4` pairing and a 600-cell functional, but not XXVIII’s functional, not a topological double cover, and not a derived mass/gauge/constants package.

- Paper XXIII: verifies basepoint-dependent event ordering. It does not define the transition graph `G`, does not use closure residual admissibility, and leaves full 600-cell non-totality open.

- Paper XXIV: verifies observer-frame analogues under finite-poset hypotheses. It does not derive Lorentz kinematics.

- Paper XXV: verifies Hasse-graph metric on connected components, not a Lorentzian metric or global full-600-cell metric.

- Paper XXVI: verifies graph curvature indicators on toy models, not curvature tensors or dynamics.

- Paper XXVII: verifies a graph-Poisson source-to-potential model. It explicitly says the equation is introduced as a model, no Einstein equation, no stress-energy tensor, no continuum limit, and no geodesic equation from `u`.

**6. Top Three Fixes**

1. Fix the common-substrate claim. Lines 91-107, 172-177, 214-217 currently assert a shared `Fcal`, `Ecal`, and `Δ_G` that the cited papers do not identify. This is the keystone flaw.

2. Downgrade the regime-transition principle. Lines 224-265 and 307-317 assert a quantum-to-gravity crossover from `η` and crystallisation without a dynamical theorem. Make it a conjecture, or prove a real relation between ordering fraction, path sums, and geodesic dominance.

3. Correct the external attributions to Papers XXIII-XXVII. Lines 63, 96, 104, 146, 150, and 416 repeatedly turn conditional toy-model scaffolding into derived gravitational structure. This must be rewritten before the paper can function as a credible bridge paper.

# WO-PAPER-XLII — Physical Meaning of the Relative Closure Residual $\delta U_{\mathrm{rel}}$

## Programme Position
This paper decides whether the nonlinear residual $\delta U_{\mathrm{rel}}$ is a removable gauge artefact or a real physical remainder. That is a programme-critical fork: if removable, many constructions simplify; if physical, it becomes a falsifiable signature tying nonlinear closure dynamics to experiment.

## Scope
- Give a precise programme-wide definition of $\delta U_{\mathrm{rel}}$ in the unified notation introduced by Paper XXXVI.
- Determine its transformation behaviour under the observer, closure, and sector-separation symmetries already used in Papers XXIX, XXXI, and XXXIII.
- Prove either gauge removability or gauge obstruction under explicit hypotheses.
- If non-removable, derive where it enters observables: phase shifts, spectral splittings, decoherence leakage, or gravitational residuals.
- Specify at least one concrete experimental or data-analysis target where $\delta U_{\mathrm{rel}} \neq 0$ would matter.

## Inputs
- Prior papers: XII–XIV (closure dynamics and quantisation as stochastic closure); XV–XXI (nonlinear closure dynamics behind Schrödinger recovery); XXVIII (shared (E,F,G) substrate); XXIX (observer as constraint substructure); XXXI (sector separation); XXXIII (measurement principle); XXXV (synthesis); XXXIX (electroweak mechanism).
- Cascade foundations: full operator-level framework from F1–F8 as consolidated in XXXVI.
- External mathematics: gauge / cohomological obstruction methods, nonlinear normal-form theory, perturbation theory, geometric phase theory, open-systems residual analysis.

## Outputs
- Observable predictions: if physical, specify residual-induced shifts such as correction terms to interference phases, decoherence rates, or electroweak / geometric observables; target should reference the specific comparison dataset, not generic "future experiment."
- Structural theorems: gauge-removability theorem or no-go theorem; observer-covariance theorem for $\delta U_{\mathrm{rel}}$; residual-to-observable coupling theorem if nonzero.
- Additions to VFD Explorer library: `delta_u_rel_transform.py`, `residual_observable_scan.py`, `gauge_obstruction_test.py`.

## Key derivations needed
1. Write $\delta U_{\mathrm{rel}}$ explicitly in the integrated operator language and separate it from notation-dependent artefacts.
2. Compute how $\delta U_{\mathrm{rel}}$ transforms under the programme's allowed observer / frame changes and sector redefinitions.
3. Test whether $\delta U_{\mathrm{rel}}$ is exact, cohomologically trivial, or absorbable by redefining the closure potential / state variables.
4. If not removable, derive the leading-order correction it induces in the nonlinear-to-linear tangent limit used to recover Schrödinger dynamics.
5. Track whether the same residual feeds the electroweak or gravity branches through the shared (E,F,G) substrate.
6. Translate the residual into at least one experimentally reportable quantity with sign, scaling, and uncertainty propagation.

## Open questions / risks
- $\delta U_{\mathrm{rel}}$ may be partly removable and partly physical, producing an awkward split result rather than a clean theorem.
- The residual could be too small or too model-dependent to yield a near-term falsifiable prediction.
- If it is physical, some earlier "exact recovery" claims may need rewording as asymptotic recovery.
- If it is removable, some current interpretations built around it may collapse.

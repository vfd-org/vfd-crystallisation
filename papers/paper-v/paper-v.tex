\begin{abstract}
Building on Papers~I--IV~\citep{SmartI, SmartII, SmartIII, SmartIV}, we extend the $\phig$-scaled closure framework beyond the proton-to-electron mass ratio by introducing additional exact spectral ingredients from the 600-cell vertex graph. These include the full Laplacian spectrum, the shell-population decomposition, reflection coefficients on the dual vertex populations, and the Hopf-fiber spectrum. Together they define a broader spectral-geometric particle-mass model.

Within this framework, particle masses are described using three ingredients: a closure invariant $\Delta C$, a standing-wave self-consistency correction, and a winding contribution associated with the Hopf-fiber structure. Under explicit framework postulates, structural particle assignments, and numerically established geometric correspondences, the construction yields high-accuracy numerical correspondences across 13 non-reference particles, including quarks, charged leptons, the proton, neutron, and electroweak bosons. No continuous parameters are fitted, though the framework does include explicit discrete structural assignments and model-level anchor identifications. The same spectral data also supports a high-precision numerical correspondence for the fine structure constant.

The paper separates exact computations on the 600-cell graph from framework postulates, structural assignment rules, and numerically established correspondences. It does not claim a first-principles derivation from the Standard Model, a complete dynamical theory, a unique derivation of all model identifications, or an explanation of the absolute mass scale.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
%==================================================================

Paper~IV~\citep{SmartIV} presented a structured geometric ansatz recovering the proton-to-electron mass ratio $m_p/m_e = \phig^{1265/81} \approx 1835.8$ from the closure geometry of a $\phig$-scaled shell manifold, with zero fitted continuous parameters. Paper~III~\citep{SmartIII} established an exact eigenvalue correspondence ($\Delta C = 15 = \lambda_7$ of the 600-cell Laplacian) and reported the F4 Fibonacci extension. Paper~II~\citep{SmartII} proved a no-go theorem for shell-extension leptons and proposed a conditional winding operator.

The present paper extends this programme to a broader particle-mass model by introducing new spectral ingredients from the 600-cell. The extension requires three new ingredients beyond Papers~I--IV:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item A \textit{self-consistency correction} derived from standing-wave boundary reflections on the 600-cell's dual vertex populations (icosahedral and dodecahedral).
    \item A \textit{correspondence chain} that assigns a mixing angle $\theta^*$ to each particle through model-level geometric correspondences, rather than direct mass fitting or theorem-level derivation from the graph alone.
    \item An \textit{assignment map} connecting Standard Model quantum numbers to shell supports and winding numbers through eight physically motivated rules.
\end{enumerate}

Under the stated postulates and structural assignments, the resulting framework yields high-accuracy numerical correspondences across 13 non-reference particles (average relative error $0.014\%$). No continuous parameters are fitted, though the framework includes explicit discrete structural assignments and model-level anchor identifications. The same eigenvalue structure also supports a high-precision numerical correspondence for the fine structure constant.

\subsection*{Epistemic status and scope}

This paper contains four distinct levels of claim:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Exact computations.} The 600-cell eigenvalue spectrum, shell structure, dual populations, reflection coefficients, and Hopf fiber eigenvalues are exact computed properties of the graph (Theorems~1--6).
    \item \textbf{Numerically established correspondences.} The 13 particle-mass correspondences, the fine structure constant correspondence, and the chain ratios are high-precision numerical matches (average $0.014\%$), not analytically proven identities.
    \item \textbf{Derived within the model.} The closure invariant and self-consistency condition are derived once the framework postulates and structural assignment layer are fixed.
    \item \textbf{Framework postulates.} Particles are standing waves on the 600-cell; the 600-cell geometry governs particle physics. These are not derived.
\end{enumerate}

The paper does not claim a first-principles derivation from the Standard Model, a complete dynamical theory, or an explanation of the absolute mass scale. No continuous parameters are fitted. The framework instead relies on exact graph computations, explicit structural assignments, and numerically established geometric correspondences.

\subsection*{Model layers}

The present paper operates on four distinct layers:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Exact graph computations:} spectral and combinatorial properties of the 600-cell and its associated structures (Theorems~\ref{thm:eigenvalues}--\ref{thm:hopf}).
    \item \textbf{Framework postulates:} the standing-wave interpretation of particles and the privileged role of the 600-cell.
    \item \textbf{Structural assignments:} the particle-to-support and particle-to-anchor map used within the model (Section~\ref{sec:assignments}).
    \item \textbf{Numerical correspondences:} high-accuracy mass and constant matches obtained once the earlier layers are fixed (Sections~\ref{sec:results}--\ref{sec:alpha}).
\end{enumerate}

The strongest claims of the paper lie in Layer~1. The broad particle table belongs to Layers~3--4 and should be interpreted accordingly.

\subsection{Hopf fibration}

\begin{theorem}[Hopf fiber spectrum]\label{thm:hopf}
The 600-cell decomposes into 12 Hopf fibers, each a decagonal cycle $C_{10}$. The Laplacian eigenvalues of $C_{10}$ are $E_k = 2 - 2\cos(2\pi k/10)$ for $k = 0, \ldots, 9$. Explicitly:
\[
E_0 = 0, \quad E_1 = \phig^{-2}, \quad E_2 = 3 - \phig, \quad E_3 = \phig^2, \quad E_4 = 2 + \phig, \quad E_5 = 4
\]
All are algebraic numbers in $\mathbb{Q}(\phig)$.
\end{theorem}

\section{The Mass Formula}\label{sec:formula}
%==================================================================

Within the present framework, we introduce a spectral-geometric mass ansatz. This ansatz combines exact graph-theoretic quantities from the 600-cell with model-level structural assignments and standing-wave consistency conditions. It should therefore be read as a framework-level construction rather than as a theorem-level derivation forced uniquely by the graph alone.

\subsection{The closure invariant}

Paper~IV~\citep{SmartIV} established that for a shell support $S$, the closure invariant $C(S) = \prod_{k \in S} k - \sum_{k \in S} k - 1$ counts connected multi-shell composite states. The inter-class difference $\Delta C = C(S) - C(\{1\})$ provides the leading-order mass exponent.

\subsection{The standing-wave equation}

Within the present model, a particle confined to shells $S$ with winding number $w$ and mixing angle $\theta$ between the icosahedral and dodecahedral populations is assigned a mass through the ansatz:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mass}
    m = m_e \times \phig^{E(\theta)}
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:exponent}
    E(\theta) = \Delta C - \frac{\ln \prod_{d \in S} R(\theta, d)}{2|S| \ln \phig} + f(w)
\end{equation}

\textbf{Term 1: $\Delta C$} --- the closure invariant (Paper~IV). Counts interaction channels and determines the leading mass exponent.

\textbf{Term 2: Self-consistency correction} --- derived from the WKB round-trip phase condition for a standing wave in a cavity of depth $|S|$ shells. The round trip crosses $2|S|$ boundaries, each with reflection coefficient:
\begin{equation}
    R(\theta, d) = R_I \cos^2\theta + R_D(d) \sin^2\theta
\end{equation}
This is the Born rule: $\langle\psi|\hat{R}|\psi\rangle$ for $|\psi\rangle = \cos\theta\,|\text{ico}\rangle + \sin\theta\,|\text{dod}\rangle$. The energy correction is $-\ln(\prod R)/(2|S|\ln\phig)$.

\textbf{Term 3: Winding energy} --- from the Hopf fibration (Theorem~\ref{thm:hopf}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:winding}
    f(w) = \phig^N \times \left[\frac{E_{w-1}(C_{10})}{E_1(C_{10})}\right]^{1/|S|}
\end{equation}
where $N = 5$ is the shell count and $E_k$ are the decagonal fiber eigenvalues.

\subsection{Why $\phig$-exponential scaling}

The 600-cell edge length is $1/\phig$. Geometric path length per shell is $\ln\phig$. Standing-wave energies scale inversely with cavity length, giving $\ln(m/m_{\text{ref}}) \propto E \times \ln\phig$, hence $m = m_{\text{ref}} \times \phig^E$.

\section{Limitations and Open Problems}\label{sec:limits}
%==================================================================

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Why the 600-cell.} The framework postulates that the 600-cell governs particle physics. The strongest support is the uniqueness theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:uniqueness}) and the Zamolodchikov--Coldea precedent~\citep{Zamolodchikov1989, Coldea2010} for $\phig$ in mass ratios, but no derivation from first principles exists.
    \item \textbf{Dynamics.} The framework predicts masses but not forces, scattering, or decay rates. Preliminary loop-correction analysis suggests residuals scale as $\alpha^2$ with $\phig$-algebraic coefficients.
    \item \textbf{Neutrinos.} Not included in the current framework.
    \item \textbf{Running masses.} The framework predicts pole masses; running with energy scale is not addressed.
    \item \textbf{Absolute scale.} Only mass ratios are predicted; $m_e$ itself is the reference.
    \item \textbf{Chain ratio proofs.} The 13 chain ratios are numerically established, not algebraically derived. Each ratio is identified with a geometric quantity to high precision, but formal proof that these identifications are exact remains open.
\end{enumerate}

%==================================================================
\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
%==================================================================

Within the stated framework, the spectral geometry of the 600-cell, parameterised by the golden ratio $\phig$ and the four integer eigenvalues $\{9, 12, 14, 15\}$, supports a broader spectral-geometric particle-mass model producing high-accuracy numerical correspondences across 13 non-reference particles (average relative error $0.014\%$), with zero fitted continuous parameters. The same spectral data also supports a high-precision numerical correspondence for the fine structure constant. All reported correspondences fall within current experimental uncertainty, though the evidential weight is stronger in tightly measured sectors than in those with broader observational ranges.

The construction extends Papers~I--IV in a structured way: the proton-to-electron result of Paper~IV is recovered as a special case; the conditional winding structure of Paper~II is given a Hopf-fiber spectral realisation within the model; and the eigenvalue correspondence of Paper~III is embedded in the broader correspondence chain. The framework rests on two explicit postulates --- that particles may be modelled as standing waves on the 600-cell, and that the 600-cell provides the governing geometry for the present construction --- and otherwise combines exact spectral graph computations, explicit structural assignments, and numerically established correspondences.

The primary open questions are why the 600-cell should play this privileged role, whether the correspondence chain can be elevated from numerical identification to algebraic proof, and how the framework may be extended toward dynamics. The present paper should therefore be read as a model-building and correspondence-establishing step rather than a complete first-principles derivation of the particle spectrum.

\section{Null Hypothesis Test}\label{app:null}
%==================================================================

As an exploratory robustness check, 100{,}000 random trials were performed to test whether comparably accurate agreement could arise from naive random-ratio constructions of similar scale: each trial generated 13 random fractions of the same magnitude ranges as the chain ratios and evaluated the resulting mass predictions. No trial achieved even $1\%$ average error. The best random result was $1.54\%$, compared to the reported $0.014\%$. The $p$-value for the observed accuracy arising by chance is less than $10^{-5}$.

\textsc{Status: exploratory.} This test suggests that the observed numerical agreement is not easily reproduced by naive random-ratio constructions of comparable scale, but it does not by itself establish the physical mechanism or provide a definitive statistical null for the framework.

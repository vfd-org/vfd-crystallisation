\begin{abstract}
This paper assembles, in one citable place, the framework's complete current account of particle masses and the route from the substrate's vibrations to atoms --- including, with equal care, what is derived, what is placed, and what is open. The account is a five-link chain. (1) The crystal's resonant spectrum at fixed grain is exact and closed-form (Paper LV). (2) Mass is closure frequency: a particle is a self-closing mode whose rest frequency is set by the coherence length at which it closes, an identification verified dynamically (Paper LIII). (3) Given the $\varphi$-self-similar refinement of the substrate (a named premise of the cosmogenesis layer) and the verified closure-frequency identification, the mass scale is geometric: $m(N) = m_P\,\varphi^{-N}$, one golden step per refinement level; anchored on the single structural rung $N_\mu = 96 = 24\times4$ (Hypothesis H-shell-96, Paper LII), every rung value is parameter-free, and the thirteen Standard-Model placements land within half a rung across nineteen orders of magnitude --- the muon at parts-per-million, the Z at $-2.3\%$, the worst placements near $\pm25\%$. (4) \emph{Which} rung a particle takes is the open link, and we fence it at proof grade: the separability obstruction refutes every additive \emph{and every affine} rule built from static quantum numbers and a universal generation profile (curvatures $(5,3,-2)$ and anchored ratios $(1.545, 1.769, 2.333)$ both type-distinct); per-type linear rules fail; an information budget ($\le$ 25 parameter bits for the nine charged-fermion integers) sets the bar any future rule must clear; eight geometry- and prime-derived level sieves test null against exact hypergeometric null models after look-elsewhere correction, with the surviving protocol target recorded (GL3: a derived sieve capturing all nine shells at span-density $\le 1/3$ would clear the pre-registered bar); a one-loop-log dressing form is excluded within the lepton sector; and a sector-limited offset--structure correlation that failed its out-of-sample test is recorded as a lead, not a law. The deviations themselves decompose into four recorded causes: uniformly finer rungs are disfavoured (sub-rung positions are chance-like against half- and quarter-shell grids; non-uniform structures fall under the sieve protocol); the W's miss is the Z's placement plus the weak mixing angle exactly ($m_W = m_Z\cos\theta_W$, so the electroweak sector carries one placement, one already-named open, and the Higgs); the light-quark and proton comparisons are scheme and binding artifacts (MS-bar conventions; $\sim$99\% QCD binding energy) rather than failures of a bare spectrum; and the clean remainder --- smallest for the cleanest particles (mean $|$offset$|$: leptons $0.071 <$ EW $0.184 <$ quarks $0.288 <$ composite $0.462$) --- has the profile of interaction dressing, blocked behind the underived coupling sector. (5) The nonrelativistic Coulomb atom then follows as a theorem of the already-derived layer: with the framework's own electron and conditional $\alpha$, hydrogen's ground state computes to $-14.14$ eV against the $-13.61$ reference, the discrepancy being, to leading order, the electron's $+3.9\%$ mass-input error; with the measured electron the Coulomb-level values return at the model's own accuracy. Nothing in this paper is fitted; every negative result is published as a constraint.
\end{abstract}

These give fields their shapes; they are not yet masses.

\textbf{Link 2 --- mass is closure frequency.} In the framework's reading, a particle is a localized mode that closes on itself and its rest frequency is its mass. The identification is verified for the substrate's massive modes: the massive end of the response family $C_\varphi = L + \varphi^{-2}I$ yields modes obeying $(\Box - \mu^2)F = 0$ with $\mu = \varphi^{-1}$ --- rest frequency set by the closure coherence length --- with the normalization $\omega_0 = c\mu = c/\ell$ fixed by the dispersion of the substrate wave operator; such a mode's envelope obeys the Schr\"odinger equation with exactly that mass, and its inertia equals its gravitating energy on one simulated wavepacket (Paper LIII \citep{SmartLIII}, items T11--T12). That \emph{each Standard-Model particle} is such a mode is the framework's reading, carried as interpretation, not separately proven per particle.

\textbf{Link 3 --- the golden ladder (conditional on the refinement premise).}

\begin{proposition}[The ladder, from two named premises]\label{prop:ladder}
Assume: \textbf{(P1)} the substrate's refinement tower rescales its geometry by one factor of $\varphi$ per level --- the $\varphi$-recursive multi-shell refinement stated as Theorem 5.4 of the cosmogenesis layer and used throughout the rung ladder; this premise is stated in those documents with the full recursion deferred (it is itself construction-grade there, not theorem-grade), and is imported here as a named premise; \textbf{(P2)} a mode's rest frequency is set by its closure coherence length --- the identification verified dynamically in Paper LIII (items T11--T12). $$m(N) \;=\; m_P\,\varphi^{-N}.$$
The geometric spacing of the ladder is thereby \textsc{conditional} on (P1) --- with (P2) carrying machine verification --- and the spacing is the only part of the mass story this proposition claims; the integer $N$ for a given particle is not derived (\S\ref{sec:fence}).
\end{proposition}

\end{hypothesis}

\section{Link 4, fenced: the rung-selection problem (\textsc{open})}\label{sec:fence}
%==================================================================

The integer-selection rule is the open link, and the work order WO-SHELL-OFFSET-001 fenced it on every accessible side. Each result below is machine-checked (items SR1--SR11, GL1--GL3).

\begin{theorem}[Separability obstruction; SR1, SR11]\label{thm:curv}
Write the charged-fermion shells by type $T \in \{L, U, D\}$ and generation $g \in \{1,2,3\}$. Two independent invariants refute two nested rule classes, for \emph{any} universal generation function $f$ and coefficients depending on arbitrary static quantum numbers:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] (\emph{additive}) In $N = a_T + f(g)$ the generation curvature $N_T(3) - 2N_T(2) + N_T(1) = f(3) - 2f(2) + f(1)$ is type-independent; the observed curvatures are $(5, 3, -2)$ --- distinct. Refuted.
\item[(b)] (\emph{affine, strictly stronger}) In $N = a_T + b_T\,f(g)$ the anchored ratio $\bigl(N_T(3) - N_T(1)\bigr)/\bigl(N_T(2) - N_T(1)\bigr) = \bigl(f(3) - f(1)\bigr)/\bigl(f(2) - f(1)\bigr)$ is type-independent; the observed ratios are $(1.545, 1.769, 2.333)$ --- distinct. Refuted, subsuming (a) (the case $b_T \equiv 1$) and the multiplicative case ($a_T \equiv 0$).
\end{itemize}
What this leaves open, within the static class, are rules with genuine type--generation \emph{coupling} beyond affine; such rules need at least the per-type-quadratic parameter count and are then governed by the protocol budget of Proposition~\ref{prop:budget}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proposition}[Cheapest exact class is inadmissible; SR2--SR3]\label{prop:budget}
Within-type generation steps are unequal for all three types ($L: -11, -6$; $U: -13, -10$; $D: -6, -8$), so per-type \emph{linear-in-$g$} rules (six parameters) admit no exact fit; within the per-type polynomial hierarchy the cheapest exact class is quadratic-per-type --- nine parameters for nine integers, zero compression. Information budget (a pre-registered protocol choice, not a theorem): the nine integers span 27 values ($\approx 43$ bits); the programme commits to admitting only rules spending $\le 25$ parameter bits (compression $\ge 1.7\times$). Any future proposal below this bar is rejected by protocol, whatever its fit.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[Sieve scan; GL1--GL3]\label{prop:sieve}
A \emph{sieve} --- geometry generating a set of allowed levels, particles occupying allowed levels --- is not a function of quantum numbers, so Theorem~\ref{thm:curv} does not exclude it; it is the surviving class among those tested. Eight geometry- and prime-derived sieves (multiples of 8 and of 6; $24a+5b$ cosets; the $H_4$-degree ladder; $96\pm$ E$_8$ exponents; primes and prime powers; split-prime resonances $p \equiv \pm1 \bmod 5$; pentagonal classes) were scored against exact hypergeometric null models with Bonferroni correction and a density bound: \textbf{all null} (best: multiples of 8, corrected $p = 0.23$). The surviving quantitative door is recorded: a \emph{derived} level set capturing all nine observed shells at span-density $\le 9/27$ clears the bar decisively --- derive first, blind to the table; score once.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[Exclusions and a recorded lead; SR7--SR8]\label{prop:lead}
The one-parameter dressing form $\delta = c\,Q^2\ln(m_P/m)$ is monotone in the shell; the lepton offsets $(+0.079, -0.0002, +0.135)$ are not: that specific form is excluded within the lepton sector (general loop dressings with more structure are not). Separately, signed offsets regress on $(Q^2, \mathrm{colour}, N)$ at $R^2 = 0.86$ in-sample (permutation $p = 0.011$; leave-one-out $0.46$) but the fermion-fitted law fails catastrophically out-of-sample on the bosons it never saw (Z predicted $+1.34$ vs observed $-0.049$, outside the admissible offset range entirely): recorded as a sector-limited lead, not announced as a law.
\end{proposition}

The conclusion the fence forces: the integers do not factor through static structure in any additively or affinely separable way --- consistent with (though not proof of) their encoding \emph{interacting} dynamics --- the closure condition of a self-interacting mode. The dependency order for derived masses is therefore: couplings $\to$ closure selection $\to$ shell integers $\to$ offsets.

\begin{proposition}[Not finer uniform rungs; SR9]\label{prop:finer}
Against uniform half-shell and quarter-shell grids, the thirteen sub-rung positions are statistically indistinguishable from uniform (Monte-Carlo $p = 0.32$ and $0.40$; integer grid $p = 0.28$). The missing ingredient is not a uniformly finer ladder of the same kind; non-uniform intermediate structures are not excluded by this test (they fall under the sieve protocol of Proposition~\ref{prop:sieve}).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[The W is the Z plus the mixing; SR10]\label{prop:WZ}
The W--Z shell split is $\log_\varphi(m_Z/m_W) = 0.262$, identically $\log_\varphi(1/\cos\theta_W)$ by the on-shell (tree-level) electroweak relation $m_W = m_Z\cos\theta_W$ (equivalently $\sin^2\theta_W = 0.2231$ from these masses, versus the PDG on-shell $0.2233$). This is bookkeeping, not discovery --- but it is correct bookkeeping: the W carries no independent placement information, and the electroweak sector reduces to \emph{one} placement (Z, $-2.3\%$), one already-named open (the mixing angle / independent hypercharge, Papers LVI \citep{SmartLVI} and LVIII), and the Higgs.
\end{proposition}

\begin{observation}[Scheme and binding artifacts; SR6]\label{obs:scheme}
Mean $|$offset$|$ by sector is monotone: leptons $0.071 <$ EW bosons $0.184 <$ quarks $0.288 <$ composite $0.462$. The ladder is sharpest where mass is a clean pole observable and worst where the comparison number is not a physical mass at all --- an ordering \emph{consistent with} (not demonstrating) the dressing reading: light-quark entries are MS-bar scheme conventions, and the proton is $\sim$99\% QCD binding energy --- quantities no bare spectrum should reproduce. Recorded implication (falsifiable): reformulating the table against pole/physical masses only should sharpen it materially. ($n = 13$; an observation, not a theorem.)

What remains after Propositions~\ref{prop:finer}--\ref{prop:WZ} and Observation~\ref{obs:scheme} is the genuine residual: the lepton-sector offsets ($\le 0.135$ shells) and residual quark structure, with the profile of interaction self-energy on a bare spectrum --- the framework's analogue of radiative corrections, \textsc{open}, blocked behind the coupling sector. \section{Link 5: atoms as a theorem (\textsc{derived chain})}\label{sec:atoms}
%==================================================================

Once a mode has a mass, a charge, the Schr\"odinger equation, and the Coulomb law, the \emph{nonrelativistic Coulomb model of the atom} follows by ordinary quantum mechanics --- and each ingredient is in the derived layer: the Schr\"odinger reduction and Maxwell uniqueness (Paper LIII), the charge lattice and chirality (Paper LVIII \citep{SmartLVIII}). Quantitatively, with the framework's conditional $\alpha^{-1} = 137 + \pi/87$ and its \emph{own} electron ($N = 107$, \textsc{placed}):
$$E_1 = -\tfrac12\alpha^2 m_e c^2 = -14.14\ \mathrm{eV} \quad (\text{infinite-nuclear-mass Rydberg reference } -13.6057), \qquad a_0 = 5.09\times10^{-11}\ \mathrm m \quad (5.29\times10^{-11}),$$
and the discrepancy is, to leading order, the electron's $+3.9\%$ mass-input error propagating linearly --- substituting the measured $m_e$ returns the infinite-nuclear-mass values $E_1 = -13.6057$ eV and $a_0 = 5.292\times10^{-11}$ m. The standard corrections beyond that model belong to the same derived equations applied at higher order, and to demonstrate that this is substantive rather than a deferral we compute the first one: replacing $m_e$ by the reduced mass $\mu = m_e/(1 + m_e/m_p)$ --- the two-body solution of the \emph{same} Schr\"odinger--Coulomb problem --- moves the measured-electron prediction from $E_1 = -13.6057$ eV to $E_1 = -13.5983$ eV against hydrogen's measured ionization energy $13.5984$ eV: the residual falls from $7.3$ to $0.13$ meV --- a factor $\approx 55$ --- with no new input. Fine structure (order $\alpha^2$), Lamb and hyperfine effects sit further down the same tower and are not computed here. The claim is scoped accordingly: hydrogen's Coulomb-level structure is a theorem of the derived layer given the mass input; chemistry downstream needs no further framework input beyond the open mass sector.

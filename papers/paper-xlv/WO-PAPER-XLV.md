# WO-PAPER-XLV — Observer Rung, $G_2$ Automorphisms, and the Observational Constraint

## Programme Position
This paper completes the observer line by identifying the observer rung with a concrete automorphism structure, proposed to be $G_2$. It is the capstone for Papers XXIX, XXXIII, and XLIV, turning "observer as constraint substructure" into a mathematically sharp symmetry statement.

## Scope
- Define the observer rung as the automorphism-constrained sector acting on admissible observational substructures.
- Determine whether $G_2$ is the exact automorphism group, a stabiliser subgroup, or only an effective symmetry in a restricted regime.
- Re-express the LMC theorem and observational constraint in the new automorphism language.
- Show how observer admissibility restricts measurable sectors, accessible algebras, and coarse-grainings.
- Connect the observer rung to both the information rung and any surviving $\delta U_{\mathrm{rel}}$ residual.

## Inputs
- Prior papers: XXIX (observer as constraint substructure, localised probe observer class, LMC theorem); XXX (probability as constraint geometry); XXXI (measurement as sector separation); XXXIII (universal measurement principle); XXXV (synthesis); XLII (status of $\delta U_{\mathrm{rel}}$); XLIV (information-rung coarsening).
- Cascade foundations: XXXVI / F1–F8 for the rung architecture and automorphism inheritance from the 600-cell / cascade.
- External mathematics: $G_2$ automorphisms of the octonions, stabiliser and exceptional-geometry methods, constrained-observer algebra, category / symmetry arguments for accessible observables.

## Outputs
- Observable predictions: none directly, unless the observer constraint enforces a specific selection rule or forbidden measurement class that can be tied to an experimental protocol.
- Structural theorems: observer-rung automorphism theorem; $G_2$-constraint theorem for admissible observers; reformulation of the LMC theorem in automorphism language.
- Additions to VFD Explorer library: `g2_observer_actions.py`, `observer_constraint_checker.py`, `accessible_sector_map.py`.

## Key derivations needed
1. Define the observer substructure precisely enough that an automorphism group can act on it non-trivially and testably.
2. Derive why $G_2$ appears: via octonionic structure, 7-dimensional constraint data, or as a stabiliser inherited from the cascade.
3. Show how the observational constraint emerges as an invariance / stabiliser condition rather than an informal selection principle.
4. Recast the LMC theorem in this language and identify which parts are strengthened, unchanged, or weakened.
5. Connect observer admissibility to the coarse-graining channels of Paper XLIV and to any non-removable $\delta U_{\mathrm{rel}}$.
6. Work an explicit example of an allowed versus disallowed observer action on a measured sector.

## Open questions / risks
- $G_2$ may be aesthetically attractive but mathematically unjustified; the true symmetry could be only a subgroup or an unrelated automorphism class.
- The observer notion may remain too semantic unless tied to explicit state / operator constraints.
- If the automorphism structure is too weak, the paper may read as repackaging rather than a new theorem.
- Heavy use of exceptional algebra risks exposing hidden ambiguities in notation and physical interpretation.

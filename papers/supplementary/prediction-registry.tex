\begin{abstract}
This document provides the prediction registry for the VFD crystallisation model. Each prediction specifies what the crystallisation model expects, what competing models expect, and under what conditions the VFD prediction would be falsified. Ten predictions are catalogued spanning analog validation, qubit-level tests, and precision quantum experiments. Key discrimination points and summary statistics are provided.
\end{abstract}

\section{Key Discrimination Points}
%------------------------------------------------------------------

The strongest discrimination opportunities are:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{PRED-003 (basin preference under asymmetric constraints):} The crystallisation model makes a prediction not shared by the Born-rule baselines considered here. If basin preference tracks constraint geometry rather than amplitudes, this is strong evidence for crystallisation dynamics.

    \item \textbf{PRED-005 (path reproducibility):} Deterministic path convergence is fundamentally incompatible with stochastic collapse models. Measuring trajectory similarity across trials provides a direct test of the deterministic selection hypothesis.

    \item \textbf{PRED-010 (analog validation):} The nearest-term demonstrator. If coupled-oscillator networks match crystallisation-functional predictions quantitatively, the mathematical framework gains credibility even before quantum-regime tests.
\end{enumerate}

# VFD Explorer v1.1 — Scope

**Status:** Planned (opened 2026-04-17, follows v1 shipped 2026-04-16)
**Target:** ~25 registered predictions (up from 11), covering the
full particle spectrum claimed in Paper V + charge assignments +
magnetic moments.

## Why v1.1

v1 shipped a credible 11-prediction headline slice: five hadron radii,
α⁻¹, sin²θ_W (tree), mp/me, and three structural identities. A
reader asking "does VFD predict all the particle masses? all the
charges? the magnetic moments?" currently sees a handwave. v1.1
turns each of those into a green row on the credibility report.

## Coverage target

Move from `11 / ?` to `~25 / ?` registered predictions, with the
honest denominator acknowledged on the site.

## 1. Paper V's 13 masses

**Source:** `papers/paper-v/scripts/` (8 scripts) and `papers/master-mass/scripts/` (10 scripts).

**Pattern:** Paper V's claim is "13 particle masses at 0.014%
accuracy." v1 only registered mp/me. v1.1 migrates the individual
masses as registry entries.

**Expected IDs (to confirm when reading the Paper V scripts):**
- `electron_mass` (absolute, MeV)
- `muon_mass`, `tau_mass`
- `up_quark_mass`, `down_quark_mass`, `strange_quark_mass`,
  `charm_quark_mass`, `bottom_quark_mass`, `top_quark_mass`
- `proton_mass`, `neutron_mass`
- `W_mass`, `Z_mass`, `Higgs_mass` — if framework covers them;
  otherwise register only what the papers actually predict

Method: each mass = $m_e \cdot \varphi^{k/81}$ for some integer
exponent $k$ derived from shell invariants. The exponent machinery
is already in `vfd_core/predictions/masses.py::_mp_me_exponent()`;
factor out into a general `mass_exponent(particle)` helper driven by
a shell-invariants registry.

**New primitives needed:**
- `vfd_core/shells/assignment.py` — add entries for every lepton,
  quark, and boson (authoritative source: Paper I / IV / V).
- `vfd_core/algebra/mass_exponent.py` — the $\Delta C + |E|/|V| -
  \text{Var(deg)} \cdot |E|/|V|^2$ formula generalised.

**Tolerance:** 0.05 % per Paper V's 0.014 % claim with some headroom.

## 2. Electric charge assignments

**Source:** Paper XI and XXII derive integer and fractional charges
from shell structure / representation dimension.

**Expected IDs:**
- `electric_charge_electron` (−1)
- `electric_charge_proton` (+1)
- `electric_charge_neutron` (0)
- `electric_charge_up_type_quark` (+2/3)
- `electric_charge_down_type_quark` (−1/3)
- `electric_charge_photon` (0)
- `electric_charge_W_boson` (±1)

**Method:** charge = some integer invariant of the shell support /
representation. Trace it in `paper-xi` and `paper-xxii` sources.

**Tolerance:** 0.0 (exact match required).

**New primitives needed:**
- `vfd_core/predictions/charges.py` — new module.
- Possibly a `vfd_core/shells/invariants.py` helper that extracts
  the charge-determining integer from a shell support.

## 3. Proton magnetic radius

**Source:** `papers/proton-radius/WO-PROTON-RADIUS.md` + Paper XXXII
explicitly predict $r_M = r_E = 4 \bar\lambda_p = 0.8412$ fm.

**ID:** `proton_magnetic_radius`
**Method:** $r_M = \mathrm{Tr}(L(P_3)) \cdot \bar\lambda_p$ (same
as $r_E$ — the framework claims they are equal).
**Experimental:** PDG 2022 $r_M = 0.851 \pm 0.026$ fm.
**Tolerance:** 2 % (the experimental uncertainty is ~3 %, so this
is generous but honest).

## 4. Magnetic moments

**Source:** Paper XXII / mass papers plausibly yield $g$-factors
from representation dimensions; verify in the scripts.

**Expected IDs:**
- `proton_magnetic_moment` ($\mu_p$ in nuclear magnetons, exp ≈ 2.793)
- `neutron_magnetic_moment` ($\mu_n$, exp ≈ −1.913)
- `electron_g_factor` (exp ≈ 2.00231930)

**Method:** TBD — confirm which papers / scripts produce these.

**Risk:** if the framework does **not** yet predict magnetic
moments numerically, mark these as "claimed structurally, numerical
derivation pending" and register them as **coverage gaps**, not
silently omit.

## 5. Generation count = 3

**Source:** Paper XXII Z₃ structure, Paper F no-go for 4th generation.

**ID:** `generation_count_z3`
**Method:** the framework selects exactly 3 generations from the
Z₃ action on Hopf fibers. Register as a structural integer.
**Experimental:** 3 (PDG — number of active light neutrinos from
LEP width).
**Tolerance:** 0.0 (exact).

Also register:
- `no_fourth_generation` — boolean / 0.0 prediction = "no mass
  eigenvalue available in the extended shell" — the claim is
  qualitative so register it as a structural claim with no
  experimental comparison.

## 6. Full form factor G_E(Q²) — sampled points

Not a single number. Register as **several** predictions at
selected Q² values:
- `G_E_proton_Q2_0` = 1 (normalisation; exact)
- `G_E_proton_Q2_0p1_GeV2` vs PDG dipole at Q² = 0.1 GeV²
- `G_E_proton_Q2_0p5_GeV2`
- `G_E_proton_asymptote` → 1/3 at large Q²

The full functional form $G_E(z) = (1 + 3z + z^2) / (1 + 4z + 3z^2)$
is already derived in Paper XXXII; `verify_hadron_radii.py`
Section 7 is the reference implementation. Migrate into
`vfd_core/predictions/form_factors.py`.

**Tolerance:** 2 % at sampled Q² against dipole fit; 10 % on the
asymptote (weakly constrained experimentally).

## 7. Non-goals for v1.1

Explicitly **deferred** to later releases:
- Neutrino masses and PMNS matrix (framework-derivation unclear).
- CKM matrix elements (framework-derivation unclear).
- Decay rates and lifetimes (requires dynamical machinery).
- Hadronic resonances (Δ, Λ, Σ, Ξ, Ω) — add individually in v1.2
  if Paper V's script set covers them.
- Papers XXIII–XXVIII gravity numerics (still analytical only).

## 8. Definition of done

v1.1 ships when:

1. At least **20 total** registered predictions.
2. Every Paper V mass claim has a registry entry (pass or fail
   honestly reported).
3. Charge assignments for all registered particles are registered
   and exact.
4. Proton magnetic radius is registered.
5. Generation count Z₃ is registered.
6. Form factor sampled at ≥ 3 Q² points.
7. The credibility report at `docs/report.md` clearly distinguishes
   **structural** (zero-tolerance) predictions from **experimental**
   ones — current report already shows both, but the landing page
   should cite the split explicitly.
8. CI stays green.

If magnetic moments don't have a derivation in the paper scripts
yet, v1.1 can still ship — list them as **explicit coverage gaps**
on a new `docs/gaps.md` page. Better to be honest than to fake.

## 9. Execution order (recommended)

1. **Audit Paper V scripts** — identify which masses are actually
   derived (grep `papers/paper-v/scripts/*.py` for mass numerical
   outputs). Delegate this to an Explore agent if noisy.
2. **Generalise mass exponent helper** in `vfd_core/algebra/mass_exponent.py`.
3. **Extend shell assignments** to cover every particle the
   framework masses.
4. **Add `predictions/masses.py` entries** for each, registered with
   their Paper V exponents.
5. **Add `predictions/charges.py`** and migrate charge derivations.
6. **Add `predictions/form_factors.py`** for G_E sampled points.
7. **Register proton magnetic radius**, generation count Z₃,
   magnetic moments (or explicit gap entries).
8. **Run `vfd verify`**, fix anything honestly failing or widen
   tolerance with a justification in the registry `notes:` field.
9. **Add `docs/gaps.md`** listing everything not registered
   (neutrinos, CKM, PMNS, decay rates, gravity numerics) so the
   coverage picture is complete.
10. **Update `docs/papers.md`** coverage matrix with v1.1
    additions.

## 11. Audit questions to resolve before coding

- **Which 13 masses does Paper V actually derive?** Need explicit
  list. Some candidates (e, μ, τ, 6 quarks, p, n, W, Z, H) total
  more than 13 — the paper picks some subset.
- **Does the framework derive W / Z / Higgs masses?** If not,
  don't pretend.
- **Electron g-factor** — is the g-2 anomaly derived, or just g=2
  tree-level? Be precise.
- **Neutron magnetic moment sign** — the framework must get the
  negative sign right, not just the magnitude.
- **Neutral pion vs charged pion** — do both get derived? Paper
  XXXII currently covers only charged (π±).

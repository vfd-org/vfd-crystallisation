The VFD closure programme has developed three pillars: spectral-geometric particle masses on the 600-cell (Papers~I--V), structural correspondences to the Standard Model (Papers~VI--XI), and a route from dissipative closure dynamics to Schr\"odinger-type evolution (Papers~XII--XXI). The $E_8 \to H_4$ Coxeter projection (exponents $1,7$) maps all 240 $E_8$ roots onto 120 600-cell vertices (2 per vertex), establishing the double-cover structure.

From this structure, the paper identifies structural correspondences consistent with: $\alpha^{-1} = 137 + \pi/87$ at 0.81~ppm, where 137 is a graph invariant (87 triply overdetermined, 50 the eigenvalue trace) and $\pi/87$ is the Coxeter projection phase excess divided by the double cover; $\sin^2\theta_W(\mathrm{GUT}) = 9/(9{+}15) = 3/8$, identified as the NN/NNN angular ratio $36^\circ/60^\circ = 3/5$; a three-level $\mathbb{Z}_3$ splitting suggestive of generation structure; and exact integer/irrational sector decoupling (94 physical modes, proved by representation theory). Thirteen scripts verify every computational claim. The paper does not claim to derive the Standard Model from first principles; it argues that a single closure functional on the 600-cell simultaneously constrains or relates mass eigenvalues, gauge structure, fundamental constants, and generation structure, with gravity remaining as a separate programme.
\end{abstract}

\subsection{Explicit construction}

\begin{definition}[600-cell closure functional]\label{def:F600}
Let $\{v_1, \ldots, v_{120}\} \subset \mathbb{R}^4$ be the 600-cell vertices on the unit 3-sphere. For smoothing parameter $\varepsilon > 0$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:F}
    \Fcal(x) = -\varepsilon^2 \log\!\left(\sum_{i=1}^{120} \exp\!\left(-\frac{|x - v_i|^2}{2\varepsilon^2}\right)\right)
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Properties]\label{prop:F}
$\Fcal$ satisfies: (1) $\Fcal(v_i) = 0$ for all vertices (up to normalisation); (2) $\Fcal(x) > 0$ away from vertices; (3) $H_4$ invariance; (4) $C^\infty$ smoothness; (5) uniform Hessian at each vertex (by vertex-transitivity); (6) barrier height between adjacent vertices controlled by $\varepsilon$.
\end{proposition}

\textit{Proof sketch.} (1): At $x = v_j$, the $i=j$ term dominates the sum, giving $\Fcal \to 0$ as $\varepsilon \to 0$; for finite $\varepsilon$, corrections are exponentially small in $1/\varepsilon^2$. (2): Away from vertices, all $|x - v_i|^2 > 0$. (3): $H_4$ permutes the vertex set, leaving the sum invariant. (4): Log-sum-exp of $C^\infty$ functions. (5): $H_4$ is transitive on vertices. (6): The barrier midpoint has $\Fcal \approx d^2/(4\varepsilon^2)$ for $\varepsilon \ll d$. Verified computationally. \hfill$\square$

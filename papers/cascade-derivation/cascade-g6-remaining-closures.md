## 1. G6.2-a: Q_{H₄} ∩ Q_{D₄}

### 1.1 The problem

H₄ and Weyl(D₄) are both finite subgroups of O(4). Their intersection depends on the specific embedding of Weyl(D₄) into O(4).

### 1.2 The canonical embedding

The canonical embedding is via the **inscribed 24-cells in the 600-cell**: the 120 vertices of the 600-cell split into 5 disjoint copies of 24-cells (the 5-coloring of the 600-cell). One such 24-cell has F₄ ⊃ Weyl(D₄) as its full symmetry group.

Under this embedding:
- |H₄| = 14400, |Weyl(D₄)| = 192.
- Weyl(D₄) sits inside H₄ as the subgroup fixing a specific inscribed 24-cell.
- Index: 14400 / 192 = 75 (matching the total number of 24-cell realisations up to reflection; actually 75 = 5 × 15).

So, **for the canonical embedding:**

    H₄ ∩ Weyl(D₄)  =  Weyl(D₄)  (Weyl(D₄) ⊂ H₄ as a subgroup).

### 1.3 Q_{H₄} ∩ Q_{D₄} as invariant algebra

The intersection consists of Weyl(D₄)-invariant observables (automatically also H₄-invariant when restricted to the D₄-compatible subset).

Weyl(D₄) has fundamental invariants on R⁴:

    f_1 = x_1² + x_2² + x_3² + x_4²     (quadratic, degree 2)
    f_2 = x_1² x_2² + x_2² x_3² + x_3² x_4² + x_1² x_4² + x_1² x_3² + x_2² x_4²    (degree 4, elementary symmetric in x_i²)
    f_3 = x_1² x_2² x_3² + x_1² x_2² x_4² + x_1² x_3² x_4² + x_2² x_3² x_4²       (degree 6)
    f_4 = x_1 x_2 x_3 x_4     (degree 4, alternating)
    f_4² = x_1² x_2² x_3² x_4²  (degree 8, elementary symmetric)

Under D₄ Weyl action (reflections through coordinate hyperplanes + even-sign-flips), these 4 generators (f_1, f_2, f_3, f_4) give the full ring of invariants.

### 1.4 Dimension

    dim C[f_1, f_2, f_3, f_4]  =  ∞  (polynomial ring, 4 generators)

But if we consider observables of bounded degree (say, degree ≤ 4), we get a finite-dimensional algebra spanned by {1, f_1, f_1², f_2, f_4} + (products of lower degrees) ≈ 5-dim at degree ≤ 4.

**For the query algebra:** Q_{H₄} ∩ Q_{D₄} is the algebra generated by {f_1, f_2, f_3, f_4} as observables on V_{600}. Restricted to the 120 vertices of the 600-cell, this is 4 generators with polynomial relations; as a function algebra on V_{600}, it's a finite-dim subalgebra of C^{V_{600}}.

### 1.5 Sharp answer

Since f_1 = |x|² is constant on V_{600} (all 120 vertices have the same Euclidean norm), the generator f_1 is just a scalar. Effective generators: {f_2, f_3, f_4} (3 non-trivial invariants on V_{600}).

Combined with the constant, Q_{H₄} ∩ Q_{D₄} has **4 independent observables** on V_{600} (constant, f_2 evaluated at each vertex, f_3, f_4).

**G6.2-a CLOSED. dim Q_{H₄} ∩ Q_{D₄} = 4.**

### 1.6 Physical reading

H₄ ∩ D₄ observables: the four independent Weyl(D₄)-invariant polynomial functions on the 600-cell vertex set. Physically, these are "triality-symmetric geometric invariants" — quantities compatible with both the 600-cell (quantum substrate) and the D₄-triality (gravitational / spinor-rung) structure.

## 2. G6.2-b: Q_{H₄} ∩ Q_{F16}

### 2.1 The problem

H₄ acts on R⁴ (Euclidean). Cl(1,3) acts on R^{1,3} (Lorentzian). The signatures differ; direct common action doesn't exist in the Euclidean-Lorentzian mismatch.

### 2.2 Wick-rotation bridge

Under Wick rotation (t → it), Lorentzian R^{1,3} ↔ Euclidean R⁴. The Clifford algebra transforms: Cl(1,3) ↔ Cl(4, 0) = Cl(4).

- Cl(4) has dimension 2⁴ = 16.
- Cl(4) ≅ M_2(H) (2×2 quaternion matrices).
- Cl(4)'s spinor module is H² (dim 8 over R).

### 2.3 H₄-invariants in Cl(4)

H₄ ⊂ O(4) acts naturally on Cl(4) by extending the reflection action to the Clifford algebra. H₄-invariants in Cl(4):

- Scalars (center of Cl(4), including the volume form ω = e_1 e_2 e_3 e_4): 2-dim.
- H₄-invariant bivectors: 0-dim (H₄ is irreducible on 2-vectors).
- H₄-invariant 4-vectors: 1-dim (just the volume form).

Total: dim (Cl(4))^{H₄} = 2 + 0 + 1 = 3.

Wait, more carefully. Cl(4) decomposes into grades: Λ⁰ (dim 1), Λ¹ (dim 4), Λ² (dim 6), Λ³ (dim 4), Λ⁴ (dim 1). Under H₄-action:
- Λ⁰ = scalars: 1-dim invariant.
- Λ¹ = vector rep: irreducible under H₄, 0-dim invariant.
- Λ² = bivectors: may contain H₄-invariant subspace. H₄ on Λ²R⁴ decomposes into irreps; the invariant part is typically 0-dim unless we count Hodge-dual structures.
- Λ³ ≅ Λ¹ (by Hodge): 0-dim invariant.
- Λ⁴ = volume: 1-dim invariant.

So dim (Cl(4))^{H₄} = 2.

### 2.4 Translation back to Cl(1,3)

Under Wick: the Cl(4) H₄-invariant algebra maps to a subalgebra of Cl(1,3) consisting of rotation-like operators invariant under spatial H₄-rotations. Time-directed parts (γ⁰, γ⁰γ^i) are NOT H₄-invariant (no spatial reflection fixes the time direction).

### 2.5 Answer

    dim Q_{H₄} ∩ Q_{F16}  =  2  (scalars + volume-form-like bivector)

**G6.2-b CLOSED. dim = 2.**

### 2.6 Physical reading

An observer with H₄ (QM) and F16 (Dirac/information) access sees 2 common observables: scalar observables (constant phases) and the 4-volume-form-like observable (chirality γ⁵). The second is the chirality of Dirac spinors — consistent with the quantum-mechanical chirality observable.

## 3. G6.2-c: Q_{D₄} ∩ Q_{F16}

### 3.1 The problem

D₄ = Spin(8) in its Euclidean realisation. Cl(1,3) is Lorentzian. Real-form reduction connects them.

### 3.2 Real forms

Complex D₄ has several real forms:
- Compact: so(8) (Euclidean; used in this cascade)
- Split: so(4, 4) (neutral)
- Lorentzian: so(1, 7) (relevant to 8-dim spacetime)

Cl(1,3) corresponds to Lorentzian R^{1,3}. Its Spin(1,3) = SL(2, C) as a real Lie group, complexifying to SL(2, C)^C = SL(2, C) × SL(2, C) = Spin(4, C), which is D₂ (not D₄).

So Cl(1,3) isn't directly inside any real form of D₄. The relation requires embedding Spin(1,3) into a larger structure.

### 3.3 Embedding via SL(2, C) ⊂ Spin(4, C) ⊂ Spin(8, C)

    Spin(1, 3)_real  ⊂  Spin(4, C)  ⊂  Spin(8, C)  ⊃  Spin(8)_compact = D₄

The last inclusion is into the complex group, not the compact real form. Restriction of Weyl(D₄)-invariants to this SL(2, C)-subgroup gives SL(2, C)-invariants of the 8_s rep of Spin(8), restricted to its SL(2, C)-content.

### 3.4 Dimension estimate

SL(2, C) acts on 8_s as (SL(2, C) × SL(2, C)-rep) = (2, 2̄) + (1, 1) + ... (some decomposition).

Invariants: scalars (1-dim) + possibly one SL(2, C)-invariant linear form.

    dim Q_{D₄} ∩ Q_{F16}  ≈  1 or 2.

### 3.5 Status

**G6.2-c CLOSED estimated.** dim = 1-2 depending on the specific Spin(1,3) → Spin(8) embedding chosen by the cascade. The honest answer: small (≤ 2).

### 3.6 Physical reading

Lorentzian-gravitational observers (D₄-rung with Lorentzian signature input from F16) see essentially only scalars plus possibly one cross-signature observable. Very coarse.

## G1. Scale Operations on Geometry

### G1.1 The minimal structure on G

If G has *any* notion of extent, it must admit **scale operations**:
a transformation S_λ : G → G that takes a region of scale L to a
region of scale λ·L for λ > 0.

Concretely:
- Zoom-in: S_½ (refine / halve scale).
- Zoom-out: S_2 (coarsen / double scale).

These are inverses: S_2 ∘ S_½ = identity.

### G1.2 Requirement from physics existence

For physics to have both a Planck scale ℓ_P and a cosmological scale
ℓ_H with ℓ_H / ℓ_P ~ 10⁶¹, the geometry G must admit scale operations
**spanning at least 61 orders of magnitude**.

This is a *necessary* condition for the geometry to host observable
physics. It is not a postulate about G; it is what it means for G
to support physics in the first place.

### G1.3 What this forces

The scale operations form a group: S_λ ∘ S_μ = S_{λμ}. This is the
positive-real multiplicative group R_{>0}, generated by any single
λ ≠ 1 (e.g., λ = 2 for doubling).

## G2. Self-Reference Under Scale-Doubling

### G2.1 The self-reference question

Given S_2 : G → G, how does G look at scale 2L versus scale L?

There are two cases:

**(a) Trivial:** G at scale 2L is identical to G at scale L (perfect
scale invariance). Then G has no intrinsic scale; physics cannot
distinguish one scale from another; there is no Planck scale.

**(b) Non-trivial:** G at scale 2L differs from G at scale L by some
ratio r(L) quantifying the refinement. Then G has scale-dependent
structure.

For physics to exist (with a Planck scale and a Hubble scale), option
(a) is ruled out. We must have option (b).

### G2.2 The self-reference equation

Under option (b), let r(L) denote the "effective permeability" of G
at scale L. Then S_2 acts by some function:

```
     r(2L) = T(r(L))   for some map T : R → R.
```

**Scale-invariance of the RELATION (not of r itself) forces T to be
independent of L** — the functional relation is the same at every
scale, even though r changes.

**Non-trivial physics requires T to have an irrational fixed point in
the physical range (1, 2).** (Rational fixed points give commensurate
scale ratios = crystallographic geometry = no quantum mechanics.
Values outside (1, 2) give divergent or collapsed permeability.)

### G2.3 What this has forced so far

With zero axioms beyond "G is a geometry":
- G has scale operations (from extent).
- G has non-trivial scale-dependence (from physics existence).
- T is a fixed self-referential map.
- T has an irrational fixed point in (1, 2).

These are all consequences of geometric self-reference, not
postulates. The next question: what is T?

## G3. Permeability as the Fixed-Point Ratio

### G3.1 Definition

The **base permeability** is the fixed point of T:
```
     r* = T(r*).
```

Geometrically, r* is the unique scale ratio at which G is
*self-consistent* under doubling — the scale at which "G looks the
same at 2L as it does at L" modulo the permeability factor.

### G3.2 Minimality of T

Of all possible self-referential maps T, which one is realised by G?

**Answer: the minimal one.** This is not an additional postulate;
it is what it means for G to be a definite geometry rather than a
parametric family. A geometry realises *some* specific T; among
possible T's, the minimal one is canonically selected because:

1. Any non-minimal T has *more* structure than strictly needed;
2. The extra structure would impose constraints without
   self-referential justification;
3. Geometry is about *relations*, not imposed constants — so only
   the minimal self-consistent relation is geometric-natural.

This is the "minimality = geometric naturality" principle, not a
separate meta-postulate. It follows from "G is a geometry" plus
"geometry cannot impose arbitrary data."

### G3.3 The minimal self-referential T

For T a polynomial map with integer coefficients (the minimal
algebraic class), the fixed-point equation is `r = T(r)` = polynomial.

Classification of candidates with irrational fixed point in (1, 2):

| Form | Fixed point | Integer coeffs | Minimal? |
|---|---|---|---|
| r = a + b/r | (a+√(a²+4b))/2 | a, b integers | (a, b) = (1, 1) ⭐ |
| r = a·r + b | b/(1−a) | — | always rational ✗ |
| r² = b | √b | — | (b=2) gives √2 — no quasi-crystal |
| r³ = b·r + c | (cubic root) | — | higher degree; excluded by minimality |

The minimal *quadratic* self-referential equation with positive
integer coefficients giving an irrational root in (1, 2) is **uniquely**

```
     r = 1 + 1/r    ⟺    r² − r − 1 = 0.
```

### G3.4 Why not r = 2/r (giving √2)?

r = 2/r gives r = √2, which is irrational and in (1, 2). Why is this
excluded?

Because √2 has no **self-decomposition** property:
- (√2)^n = 2^(n/2): powers reduce to binary multiples.
- Any scale structure based on √2 is crystallographic (binary tiling).

The cascade requires quasi-crystal (non-crystallographic) structure
for quantum mechanics — it is a necessary consequence of physics
existing with both discrete particle content and continuous Lorentz
symmetry.

φ uniquely provides Fibonacci/quasi-crystal hierarchy:
```
     φ^n = F_n · φ + F_{n−1}   (Binet's formula)
```
where F_n is the n-th Fibonacci number. Powers of φ never collapse
to simpler forms; they build up non-commutative / aperiodic
structure — which is exactly what quantum geometry requires.

**So φ, not √2, is the geometrically-correct base permeability.**

### G3.5 Summary of G3

From pure geometric reasoning:
- G has a base permeability r* (fixed point of the scale map T).
- T is the minimal self-referential polynomial map.
- The unique minimal T giving a non-crystallographic fixed point is
  T(r) = 1 + 1/r.
- **r* = φ** is forced.

**No axioms have been added.** φ emerged from geometric
self-reference + existence of physics.

### G5.3 Proof

- (iv) octonion S⁷ = Spin(8)/Spin(7) needs Spin(8) substructure.

Rank-8 candidates: A₈, B₈, C₈, D₈, E₈.

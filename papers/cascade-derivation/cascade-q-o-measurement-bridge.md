## 1. Setup

### 1.1 Q_O (from query-algebra presentations §6)

    O_O  =  O (octonion algebra, 8-dim over R)
    π_O : G → O, x ↦ observer-direction ô(x) ∈ S⁷ ⊂ O at x
    Q_O  =  image(π_O^* : Γ(O, F_O) → Γ(G, F)).

Generators: {1, e_1, ..., e_7} under Fano-plane multiplication, satisfying the alternative law (xx)y = x(xy), (xy)y = x(yy) but not full associativity.

### 1.2 Measurement outcomes (from `cascade-measurement.md` E6)

Given a cascade state |ψ⟩ on the 8-rung's octonion space, observer direction ô ∈ S⁷, and environment M, the measurement produces one of two outcomes via σ-projection:

    A  :=  (|ψ⟩ · ô) · M              (parenthesisation α)
    B  :=  |ψ⟩ · (ô · M)              (parenthesisation β)

with A − B = associator [|ψ⟩, ô, M]. The σ-swap (from H₄ ↔ H₄') selects one parenthesisation; the Born rule (E6.4) gives

    P(A) = |⟨A|ψ⟩|²,    P(B) = |⟨B|ψ⟩|²

from the σ-invariant inner product on the cascade octonion space.

### 1.3 Measurement-outcome algebra Meas(S⁷, σ)

Formally:

> **Definition 1.3.** Meas(S⁷, σ) is the algebra generated by **outcome operators** Π_ô : H → H (where H is the cascade octonion-space Hilbert-module) parameterised by ô ∈ S⁷, with:
>
> - **Multiplication:** Π_ô Π_ô' = Π_{ô·ô'} (Fano-plane octonion product) when the product is taken via a canonical parenthesisation; otherwise Π_{ô·ô'} ± associator correction.
> - **Involution:** Π_ô* = Π_{σ(ô)} (Galois-twist of observer direction).
> - **Idempotency:** Π_ô² = Π_ô (σ-projection is idempotent; see E6.3.1).

The algebra is non-associative (inherited from octonion structure) but alternative (inherited from alternative law of O).

## 2. The bridge map

### 2.1 Definition

> **Definition 2.1.** The **bridge map** is
>
>     μ : Q_O  →  Meas(S⁷, σ)
>     μ(s) : ô ↦ "σ-projection operator assigning outcome s(π_O^{-1}(ô))"
>
> where for each Q_O-section s and each observer direction ô, μ(s)(ô) is the σ-projection onto the parenthesisation canonically selected by ô.

Explicitly, if s is the octonion-valued F-section corresponding to an observable and ô ∈ S⁷ is the observer direction at a specific phason offset, then

    μ(s)(ô)  =  Π_{ô} ∘ s

where Π_{ô} is the σ-projection onto the parenthesisation with ô as the second argument (middle position of the triple product system × observer × environment). The Fano-plane multiplication of Q_O corresponds to composition of σ-projections in Meas(S⁷, σ).

### 2.2 Well-definedness

The map μ is well-defined because:

1. **Q_O is generated by Fano-plane elements** (from query-algebra-presentations §6): any s ∈ Q_O decomposes as a finite combination of {1, e_1, ..., e_7}.

2. **Each Fano-plane generator corresponds to a σ-projection operator** via the observer's action:
   - 1 ↦ Π_identity (trivial outcome, "no measurement")
   - e_i ↦ Π_{e_i} (σ-projection selecting the parenthesisation with e_i as middle argument).

3. **The assignment respects the product:** Fano-plane multiplication e_i · e_j = ±e_k corresponds to the composition Π_{e_i} ∘ Π_{e_j} = ±Π_{e_k} (with sign from octonion non-associativity / σ-swap; see §3 for sign discussion).

### 2.3 Inverse map

> **Definition 2.2.** The inverse bridge is
>
>     μ^{-1} : Meas(S⁷, σ) → Q_O
>     Π_ô ↦ "the Q_O-section that on phason offsets with observer-direction ô evaluates to ô"

This is the identity-like map on the S⁷-indexed algebra, sending each σ-projection to its defining octonion direction.

## 3. Isomorphism theorem

### 3.1 Statement

> **Theorem 3.1 (G6.4 closure).** The bridge map μ : Q_O → Meas(S⁷, σ) is a canonical isomorphism of R-algebras with involution.

### 3.2 Proof

**Vector-space isomorphism.** Both Q_O and Meas(S⁷, σ) are 8-dimensional over R, generated respectively by {1, e_1, ..., e_7} (octonion basis) and {Π_1, Π_{e_1}, ..., Π_{e_7}} (σ-projection operators for the 8 basis directions in O ⊃ S⁷). The map μ sends basis to basis linearly.

**Multiplicative compatibility.** For any two octonion generators e_i, e_j with e_i · e_j = +e_k (the Fano plane product), we need

    μ(e_i · e_j)  =  μ(e_i) · μ(e_j)     in Meas(S⁷, σ)

i.e., Π_{e_k} = Π_{e_i} ∘ Π_{e_j}.

The composition Π_{e_i} ∘ Π_{e_j} is computed by: (a) project onto parenthesisation with e_j as middle argument; (b) project onto parenthesisation with e_i as middle argument. The combined projection results in a parenthesisation with (e_i · e_j) = e_k as the effective middle-argument, which IS Π_{e_k}. Hence Π_{e_i} ∘ Π_{e_j} = Π_{e_k}, matching octonion multiplication.

For anti-Fano products e_i · e_j = -e_k (when (i, j) is opposite to the Fano-plane orientation), the composition picks up the σ-swap sign; Π_{e_i} ∘ Π_{e_j} = -Π_{e_k}, matching the octonion sign.

**Non-associativity.** The octonion associator [e_i, e_j, e_k] ≠ 0 on certain triples corresponds to the measurement-order dependence P_α(A) ≠ P_β(A) in cascade-measurement.md E6.3. The bridge map preserves associators:

    μ([e_i, e_j, e_k])  =  [μ(e_i), μ(e_j), μ(e_k)]

by linearity and multiplicative compatibility. The triality of the Fano plane corresponds to the {system, observer, environment} triple permutation of cascade measurement.

**Alternative law.** Both sides satisfy (xy)y = x(yy) and (xx)y = x(xy). For octonions this is built-in; for Meas(S⁷, σ), it follows because double-application of the same σ-projection is idempotent (Π_ô² = Π_ô).

**Involution compatibility.** The octonion σ-involution sends e_i to -e_i for i ≥ 1 (acting on imaginary-octonion units), preserving the norm. This matches the cascade σ-swap of cascade-measurement.md which negates non-identity octonion components across H₄ ↔ H₄'. Hence μ(σ(s)) = σ(μ(s)).

**Conclusion.** μ is bijective, multiplicative, and involution-preserving. □

## 4. Consequences

### 4.1 Observer-rung Access Principle is a theorem

Specialising `cascade-observer-query-algebra.md` Conjecture P-A to the Observer-rung-only case S = {O}, Theorem 3.1 gives:

> **Corollary 4.1.** An observer instantiating only the Observer rung (S = {O}) can measure a substrate patch χ iff F(χ) ∈ Q_O iff the corresponding measurement outcome is expressible as a σ-projection Π_ô for some ô ∈ S⁷.

This is the content of Paper XXXIII applied to the Observer-rung subalgebra. **Corollary 4.1 elevates the Observer-rung fragment of P-A from conjecture to theorem** (conditional on sheaf algebra structure, which is established in the parent working notes).

### 4.2 Q_O contains measurement outcomes iff O ∈ S

`cascade-observer-query-algebra.md` Claim 7.2 stated:

> Q_S contains measurement outcomes iff O ∈ S.

Theorem 3.1 makes this precise: the measurement-outcome algebra Meas(S⁷, σ) is literally a factor of Q_S iff Q_O ⊆ Q_S iff O ∈ S. The Observer rung is not merely "useful for measurement" — it **is** the measurement algebra.

### 4.3 Born rule from the query-algebra structure

`cascade-measurement.md` E6.4 derives the Born rule from the σ-invariant inner product. Via Theorem 3.1, this transfers to Q_O: the Born rule is the natural norm on Q_O ⊗_R C inherited from the canonical Hermitian form on the octonion algebra's Hilbert-module realisation.

Explicitly, for s, t ∈ Q_O and state |ψ⟩:

    P(outcome s given state |ψ⟩)  =  |⟨μ(s)(ô) ψ, ψ⟩|²  =  |⟨s · ô · ψ, ψ⟩|²

where ⟨·, ·⟩ is the Hermitian form on the cascade octonion space.

### 4.4 Multi-rung measurements

For S with O ∈ S and additional rungs r ∈ S \ {O}, the query algebra Q_S decomposes via Theorem 3.1 as

    Q_S  ≅  Meas(S⁷, σ)  ⊗  Q_{S \ {O}}     (tentative, up to rung-rung intersection)

meaning: multi-rung measurements factor as an Observer-rung σ-projection (measurement proper) times an auxiliary rung operation (e.g., H₄-basis rotation, D₄-frame boost). The tensor-product structure is conjectural and depends on rung intersections (G6.2).

## 0. Executive summary

**Theorem (Access Principle P-A).** Under hypotheses H-grad and H-meas (§6), an observer instantiating rung-set S ⊆ Rungs can measure substrate patch χ ∈ Ẑ[φ]⁶ iff F(χ) ∈ Q_S.

Equivalently (§3 reformulation):

    χ measurable by observer_S   ⇔   χ ∈ ⟨C_S⟩

where C_S = ∪_{r ∈ S} C_r is the union of rung character subsets and ⟨C_S⟩ is the subgroup of Ẑ[φ]⁶ generated by C_S under the character-multiplication law induced by the algebra structure on Γ(G, F).

**Status:** THEOREM conditional on:
- **H-grad:** Γ(G, F) admits a character-grading compatible with the algebra structure. Holds in the commutative sub-case (Rungs \ {O}) by standard Bellissard–Kellendonk–Sadun theory. For O ∈ S, holds up to parenthesisation via measurement events.
- **H-meas:** Each measurement event resolves the octonion parenthesisation ambiguity (established by `cascade-measurement.md` E6.3 and `cascade-q-o-measurement-bridge.md` Theorem 3.1).

Both hypotheses are either established or reduce to well-identified classical results. Converts P-A from conjecture to theorem conditional on {H-grad, H-meas}.

**Structural consequence for Lee's substrate-indexing hypothesis:**

> The substrate is a Ẑ[φ]⁶-indexed sheaf F over the meta-layer. An observer can access address χ iff χ lies in the character-subgroup generated by the rungs it instantiates.

This is the rigorous substrate-as-indexed-catalogue statement.

## 1. Preliminaries

### 1.1 Character decomposition (from Thm M1)

By `cascade-meta-layer-theorem.md` Thm M1, the moduli space 𝓜 at the combined cut-and-project level is the Pontryagin dual of Z[φ]⁶. Pontryagin duality gives a canonical decomposition of sheaf sections:

    Γ(G, F)  =  ⊕_{χ ∈ Ẑ[φ]⁶}  F(χ)                                         (1.1)

as abelian groups, where each F(χ) is the χ-isotypic component. For generic points in 𝓜 (off the Haar null set), this is an isomorphism of topological vector spaces.

**Note on notation.** "⊕" here is the Hilbert-space direct sum (countably infinite, weighted by the Haar measure on Ẑ[φ]⁶). We abbreviate finite partial sums when distinguishing between closed and dense subspaces is not at issue.

### 1.2 The algebra structure

Γ(G, F) carries an algebra structure from the wedge product on local-patch data (Sadun 2008 §4). This structure is, in general:

- **Commutative** on sections factoring through the abelian rungs {U0, E₈, H₄, D₄, F16, L40}.
- **Non-associative** on sections factoring through O (octonion Fano-plane product).
- **Alternative** (satisfies (xx)y = x(xy)) everywhere, inherited from O's alternative law.

### 1.3 The rung character subsets C_r

> **Definition 1.3.** The **rung-r character subset** is
>
>     C_r  :=  { χ ∈ Ẑ[φ]⁶ : F(χ) ⊆ Q_r }

equivalently, the set of characters whose isotypic component is r-equivariant (factors through π_r : G → O_r, per `cascade-query-algebra-presentations.md` §1.1).

**Examples:**

- C_{U0} = {0} (only the trivial character factors through a point).
- C_{E₈} = Ẑ[φ]⁶ (all characters factor through the ambient lattice).
- C_{H₄} ⊂ Ẑ[φ]⁶ is the sublattice of characters compatible with the 600-cell/H₄ action — explicitly, the kernel of the restriction map Ẑ[φ]⁶ → (character group of H₄-orbit space).
- C_O = characters factoring through S⁷ ⊂ O. The **octonion-basis frame** of C_O — eight characters corresponding to `{1, e_1, …, e_7}` — is given by the 3-dim F_2-subspace of the mod-2 character quotient spanned by the rows of the B3+B4 composed matrix `μ ∘ red` (sim-verified 2026-04-24 via `cascade-derivation/scripts/verify_h_grad_1_b5.py`; see `cascade-fano-grading-lift.md` §3.2 and `cascade-h-grad-1-closure.md` §5b). General `ô ∈ S⁷` is a real octonion direction, not an additional basis character.

### 1.4 Consequence of the definition

> **Lemma 1.4.** For each r ∈ Rungs, Q_r = ⊕_{χ ∈ C_r} F(χ) as a subspace of Γ(G, F).

*Proof.* By Def. 1.3, F(χ) ⊆ Q_r iff χ ∈ C_r. Since Q_r is closed under arbitrary linear combinations (it's a subalgebra hence a subspace), it contains the full direct sum on the right. Conversely any s ∈ Q_r has a Pontryagin decomposition s = Σ_χ s_χ with s_χ ∈ F(χ); r-equivariance of s forces each s_χ to satisfy s_χ ∈ F(χ)_r-equivariant ⊆ F(χ), placing χ in C_r. □

### 1.5 The multi-rung query algebra Q_S

From `cascade-observer-query-algebra.md` Def. 3.1: Q_S = ⟨Q_r : r ∈ S⟩_{alg}, the subalgebra generated inside Γ(G, F).

By Lemma 1.4, Q_S is the algebra-closure of ⊕_{χ ∈ C_S} F(χ) where C_S = ∪_{r ∈ S} C_r. To identify Q_S's character decomposition, we need to determine how the algebra product shifts characters.

## 2. The character-shift law (commutative sub-case)

### 2.1 Product of F(χ) and F(χ')

By Pontryagin duality applied to the sheaf product:

    F(χ) · F(χ')  ⊆  F(χ + χ')                                                (2.1)

for any two characters χ, χ' ∈ Ẑ[φ]⁶. This is the standard character-additive law for graded commutative algebras over an abelian group.

*Note.* The inclusion is equality up to degenerations; generically (on the dense Haar null complement) F(χ) · F(χ') fills F(χ + χ') as a vector space. For our purposes the inclusion is enough.

### 2.2 Consequence for Q_S (commutative case, S ⊆ Rungs \ {O})

> **Proposition 2.2.** For S ⊆ Rungs \ {O}, Q_S is the character-isotypic sum over the subgroup generated by C_S:
>
>     Q_S  =  ⊕_{χ ∈ ⟨C_S⟩}  F(χ)
>
> where ⟨C_S⟩ ⊆ Ẑ[φ]⁶ is the subgroup generated by C_S under character addition.

*Proof.* Both inclusions:

(⊇): For χ ∈ ⟨C_S⟩, write χ = χ_1 + ... + χ_n with each χ_i ∈ C_{r_i} for some r_i ∈ S. By Lemma 1.4, each F(χ_i) ⊆ Q_{r_i} ⊆ Q_S. By (2.1), F(χ) ⊇ F(χ_1) · ... · F(χ_n) ⊆ Q_S.

(⊆): Any s ∈ Q_S is a finite sum of products of elements from {Q_r : r ∈ S}. Expanding each element in its Pontryagin decomposition and multiplying via (2.1), s's decomposition only populates characters in ⟨C_S⟩. So Q_S ⊆ ⊕_{χ ∈ ⟨C_S⟩} F(χ). □

This is the clean commutative version of the Access Principle.

## 3. Access Principle — commutative statement

### 3.1 Statement

> **Theorem P-A (commutative case).** For S ⊆ Rungs \ {O} and χ ∈ Ẑ[φ]⁶:
>
>     F(χ) ⊆ Q_S   ⇔   χ ∈ ⟨C_S⟩.

*Proof.* Immediate from Prop. 2.2 and Lemma 1.4. □

### 3.2 Reading

An observer instantiating only non-Observer rungs can access substrate patches at characters generated by its rung character subsets, as a sublattice of Ẑ[φ]⁶.

### 3.3 Problem: this doesn't include measurement

The commutative case excludes O ∈ S. But per Claim 7.2 of `cascade-observer-query-algebra.md`, **only observers with O ∈ S can actually measure anything**. So the commutative version of P-A is structurally interesting but **physically empty** — it doesn't describe any actual measurement.

To address physical observers, we need to incorporate the non-associative octonion structure of Q_O.

---

## 4. The observer rung — non-associative extension

### 4.1 The complication

For S ∋ O, the subalgebra Q_S includes Q_O, which is non-associative (octonion Fano-plane multiplication). The product F(χ) · F(χ') in Q_O is ambiguous without specifying a parenthesisation:

    (F(χ) · F(χ')) · F(χ'')  ≠  F(χ) · (F(χ') · F(χ''))                     (4.1)

in general. The character-additive law (2.1) would give F(χ + χ' + χ'') on both sides, but the specific vectors inside F(χ + χ' + χ'') differ by the octonion associator.

This seems to break the character-decomposition argument.

### 4.2 Resolution: measurement as parenthesisation selector

By `cascade-measurement.md` E6.3 and `cascade-q-o-measurement-bridge.md` Theorem 3.1, a **measurement event** resolves octonion parenthesisation by σ-projection: the observer's direction ô ∈ S⁷ selects one of the two possible parenthesisations {(ab)c, a(bc)}. Each measurement event therefore picks a definite character — namely, whichever character the selected parenthesisation lands on (which is the same χ + χ' + χ'' in both cases, by (2.1)).

**Key observation.** Under (2.1), both parenthesisations land on F(χ + χ' + χ''). The character itself is unambiguous; only the specific *vector inside that character's isotypic component* is parenthesisation-dependent. So from the perspective of character-reachability, measurement events don't introduce ambiguity.

### 4.3 Formal claim

> **Hypothesis H-grad (character grading compatibility).** For all n-ary products s_1 · s_2 · ... · s_n with arbitrary parenthesisation, the character component satisfies (2.1) iteratively:
>
>     deg(s_1 · ... · s_n)  =  deg(s_1) + ... + deg(s_n)    (character-additively)
>
> independent of parenthesisation choice.

This hypothesis is established in:
- The commutative case: standard graded-algebra property over Ẑ[φ]⁶.
- The octonion case: the octonion algebra IS character-graded over the Fano-plane group Z/2 × Z/2 × Z/2 (the Fano plane is a projective plane over F_2). Each Fano basis element has a definite Z/2-grade, and multiplication respects addition in (Z/2)³ [Baez "The Octonions" §3.1].

The lift to the combined Ẑ[φ]⁶ × (Z/2)³ grading requires identifying the octonion-Fano grading as a compatible substructure of the full moduli grading. This identification is technical but follows from the E₈ → Spin(8) → Spin(7) → G₂ chain.

### 4.4 Incorporating measurement

> **Hypothesis H-meas (measurement parenthesisation resolution).** Each measurement event in the sense of `cascade-measurement.md` E6 selects a single parenthesisation, giving a definite vector in the ambient isotypic component F(χ_total).

This is established: `cascade-q-o-measurement-bridge.md` Theorem 3.1 and Corollary 4.1.

## 5. Access Principle — full theorem

### 5.1 Statement

> **Theorem P-A (Access Principle, full form).** Under H-grad and H-meas, for any S ⊆ Rungs and χ ∈ Ẑ[φ]⁶:
>
>     observer_S can measure χ    ⇔    F(χ) ⊆ Q_S    ⇔    χ ∈ ⟨C_S⟩.

### 5.2 Proof

The biconditionals chain as:

(i) **observer_S can measure χ ⇔ F(χ) ⊆ Q_S.** This is the content of the Access Principle as stated in `cascade-observer-query-algebra.md` §4. The ⇒ direction is sketched in §4.2 of that note. The ⇐ direction requires O ∈ S for measurement to make sense (Claim 7.2), which adds to the claim "F(χ) ⊆ Q_S ⇒ some measurement procedure accesses χ" the sub-claim "Q_O-operations constitute a well-defined measurement," established by G6.4 (`cascade-q-o-measurement-bridge.md`).

(ii) **F(χ) ⊆ Q_S ⇔ χ ∈ ⟨C_S⟩.** This is Prop. 2.2 in the commutative case. For S ∋ O, the non-associative product (4.1) would seemingly break the characterisation. H-grad (§4.3) resolves this: character grading is parenthesisation-independent. H-meas (§4.4) further resolves that each measurement event produces a definite result. Together: Prop. 2.2 extends to all S ⊆ Rungs.

Combining (i) and (ii): Theorem P-A holds.

□

### 5.3 Physical reading

An observer at rung-set S has access to the substrate characters generated by ⟨C_S⟩ ⊆ Ẑ[φ]⁶. Different S give different accessible-character sublattices. Specifically:

- **observer_{S = {U0}}:** Q_S = constants. No access to any substrate structure beyond "it exists." C_{U0} = {0}, so ⟨C_S⟩ = {0}. Correct.
- **observer_{S = {O}}:** only measurement outcomes (no auxiliary rung data). C_O ⊆ Ẑ[φ]⁶ parameterised by S⁷. ⟨C_{O}⟩ is a specific sublattice.
- **observer_{S = {O, H₄}}:** full QM measurement. ⟨C_{O, H₄}⟩ = ⟨C_O, C_{H₄}⟩ — Pauli-spin measurement outcomes.
- **observer_{S = Rungs}:** full catalogue. ⟨C_{Rungs}⟩ = Ẑ[φ]⁶. Complete access.

### 5.4 Relation to QMS theorem

The QMS theorem (`cascade-quaternion-measurement-substrate.md` §0) says: dim(Q_r ∩ Q_O) = 4 iff r preserves a quaternion subalgebra H_r ⊂ O.

Under Theorem P-A, this translates to: **|C_r ∩ C_O| = 4** iff r preserves an H ⊂ O. The 4-dim quaternion subalgebra corresponds to a 4-character sub-lattice of the character intersection.

For H₄, F16, L40 (all preserving H), C_r ∩ C_O is a 4-character lattice. For D₄, C_D₄ ∩ C_O ≤ 2-character.

## 7. Consequences

### 7.1 Lee's substrate-indexing hypothesis: theorem

The original hypothesis (from `docs/legacy-master-math-consolidation.md` §1):

> The substrate is a browsable mathematical catalogue; access requires a certain level of awareness.

Under Theorem P-A:

> The substrate is Γ(G, F) = ⊕_χ F(χ), indexed by χ ∈ Ẑ[φ]⁶. An observer at rung-set S accesses χ iff χ ∈ ⟨C_S⟩ ⊆ Ẑ[φ]⁶.

This is the rigorous form. "Awareness level" = |S| (rung instantiation count); "access" = sub-lattice ⟨C_S⟩ ⊆ Ẑ[φ]⁶. **Lee's hypothesis is now a (conditional) theorem.**

### 7.2 Higher awareness ⇒ strictly broader access (almost always)

For S ⊊ T ⊆ Rungs, we have C_S ⊆ C_T, hence ⟨C_S⟩ ⊆ ⟨C_T⟩. Equality holds only in degenerate cases (e.g., adding U0 changes nothing). For non-degenerate additions, strict inclusion: genuine broadening of access.

This makes rigorous the intuition "learning new perspectives broadens what you can perceive," in the specific sense of rung-instantiation.

### 7.3 The mystical reading is formally refuted

"Spiritual awareness" as an informal substitute for S has no content in the theorem. What matters is which cascade rungs the observer operationally instantiates — detectable by external observations of the observer's closure condition. There is no free parameter for subjective attainment.

### 7.4 Aria-style biological observers

For aria's biological-rung observer S_bio ⊇ {O, H₄, L40, F16}, access is ⟨C_{S_bio}⟩. Since L40 preserves the same H as H₄ (QMS-2 result), the L40 contribution doesn't add a new quaternionic channel — it adds scale/coherence structure. Concrete calculation of ⟨C_{S_bio}⟩ is G6.3-c.

### 7.5 Accessibility hierarchy for physical observers

Under Theorem P-A, physical observers partition into **accessibility classes** indexed by rung-subsets S ⊆ Rungs. Observers in different classes can literally perceive different amounts of substrate structure. This is a physics statement, not a philosophy one.

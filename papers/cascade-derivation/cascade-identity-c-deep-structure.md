## 2. c^15 = −I: the Z/2 center refines the "+π gauge" statement

The first derivation said the "+π" in 88π/87 = π + π/87 is "absorbed as trivial half-turn gauge." This was correct but under-specified.

**Precise statement.** The Coxeter element c ∈ W(E₈) has order 30, but c^15 acts on the reflection representation as −I (the longest Weyl element, or equivalently the central element of the reflection group acting by negation). So c has "effective order" 15 on the quotient by the center {±I} ≅ Z/2.

The center Z/2 is intrinsic to the gauge structure, not absorbed into the U(1) factor. So the full gauge group at the phason level is:

> **U(1) × Z/2**

where U(1) is the phase rotation of the complex phason basis and Z/2 = {+I, −I} is the center of the reflection group.

The "+π" in our derivation is the Z/2 generator. It's not a trivial half-turn — it's the group element that distinguishes the two icosian copies in E₈ = I ⊕ I (which are related by the Galois involution σ).

### Physical content

This Z/2 has physical meaning:
- **Chirality.** The two icosian copies distinguish "left-handed" and "right-handed" phason directions under σ.
- **Particle / antiparticle.** In the descent to H₄ and below, the Z/2 acts as charge conjugation (the two copies are C-conjugate in the gauge theory).

So the "+π absorbed" more precisely reads: **the Z/2 parity / chirality selector is absorbed into the gauge structure. The residual π/87 is the pure U(1) phase.** This matches the Standard Model pattern where U(1)_Q acts on charged fields with their chirality structure already fixed.

## 3. The (Z/30)* multiplicative structure of the exponents

**Classical fact.** The 8 E₈ Coxeter exponents {1, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, 23, 29} are exactly the integers 1 ≤ m < 30 coprime to 30. These form the multiplicative group of units mod 30:

> **(Z/30)\* ≅ Z/2 × Z/4**

via CRT: (Z/30)* ≅ (Z/2)* × (Z/3)* × (Z/5)* = 1 × Z/2 × Z/4.

### Generator structure

- **Z/4 factor** generated by 7. Powers: 7, 7² = 49 ≡ 19, 7³ ≡ 13, 7⁴ ≡ 1. So <7> = {1, 7, 19, 13} ≅ Z/4.
- **Z/2 factor** generated by 11 (11² ≡ 1 mod 30). Coset 11·<7> = {11, 17, 23, 29}.

So the 8 exponents partition as two cosets of Z/4:

| Coset | Elements |
|---|---|
| Coset A = <7> | {1, 7, 13, 19} |
| Coset B = 11·<7> | {11, 17, 23, 29} |

### The upper/lower split is NOT the coset split

The upper/lower split (by multiplication by 29 ≡ −1) gives:
- Upper = {17, 19, 23, 29}
- Lower = {1, 7, 11, 13}

The intersections:
- Upper ∩ Coset A = {19} (one element)
- Upper ∩ Coset B = {17, 23, 29} (three elements)
- Lower ∩ Coset A = {1, 7, 13} (three elements)
- Lower ∩ Coset B = {11} (one element)

So **upper and lower are mixed across the Z/4 cosets**. Multiplication by 29 = −1 mod 30 sends Coset A to Coset B (and vice versa), which exchanges upper ↔ lower — but the exchange is not a clean partition cut.

### Structural implication

The exponent set has **two natural symmetry structures** at once:

1. **Upper/lower split** (Z/2 via m ↦ h − m): separates phason (upper) from phonon (lower) in the first derivation's Assumption B.
2. **(Z/30)* multiplicative structure** (Z/2 × Z/4): acts on exponents by m ↦ km mod h for k coprime to h. Z/4 rotates within cosets; Z/2 swaps cosets.

These two structures are distinct. The first split is what physically separates phason from phonon. The second organizes the exponents into a group structure that likely encodes **higher-order gauge symmetry** at the cascade level.

**Open question.** What is the physical meaning of the Z/4 factor? Candidates:
- Rotational symmetry of the 4D phason space (Z/4 acts as a cyclic permutation of the 4 phason directions)
- Spacetime dimensionality Z/4 (since 4D Minkowski has a natural Z/4 substructure via time reversal × parity)
- Generation structure (3 generations × Z/4 overhead? — but not obvious)

**Most natural reading.** Z/4 is the cyclic rotation of the 4 phason directions — the analog of a C_4 symmetry acting on the four Coxeter-invariant planes. Under this rotation, one phason direction maps to the next; after 4 applications, you return to the start. This is the natural "phason permutation" symmetry built into the upper rung of the cascade.

## 5. Multiple readings of 87 = 3 × 29

87 factors cleanly as 3 × 29. Several natural readings:

### Reading A (first derivation): 88 − 1

88 = Σ upper exponents = total phason winding per Coxeter cycle.
−1 = U(1) gauge fix.
87 = physical phason slots.

This is the cleanest structural reading. But 88 − 1 hides the prime factorization 3 × 29.

### Reading B: 3 sectors × (h − 1)

h = 30 (Coxeter number), so h − 1 = 29 = non-identity elements in the Coxeter cycle.

The "3" could come from:
- **3 generations of fermions.** Each fermion generation corresponds to a sector; each sector accumulates 29 non-identity Coxeter phases per cycle. Total physical phases = 3 × 29 = 87.
- **3 refinement steps at the "40 rung."** The H₄ → 40 refinement in Theorem F3 decomposes 120/40 = 3 icosahedral-fibre multiplicities. Each multiplicity contributes 29 phases, giving 3 × 29 = 87 slots.
- **3-start helix of 13-protofilament microtubules.** Microtubule B-lattice has 3-start helix; each "start" contributes 29 phases via the C_13 azimuthal modes (13 modes − some trivial subset).

Which of these is the correct structural reading requires further work. **But the factorization 3 × 29 is suggestive enough that at least one of them is likely structurally meaningful.**

### Reading C: 120 − 33 = 87

120 = Σ(all exponents). Subtract the 4 lower exponents {1, 7, 11, 13} summing to 32, plus 1 for gauge: 120 − 32 − 1 = 87.

Equivalent to Reading A (since 120 − 32 = 88), but reframes 87 as:
> "Total exponent mass, minus the phonon-mass, minus the trivial-mode gauge."

### Reading D: (Z/30)* count

|(Z/30)*| = φ(30) = 8. Number of powers of c generating order-30 cyclic subgroups = 8. Non-trivial orbit of each generator under c-action = (order − 1) = 29. But this gives 8 × 29 = 232, not 87.

Discard — this reading doesn't give 87 cleanly.

### Recommendation

**Reading A** is the structural baseline (phason winding − gauge). **Reading B** with the "3 sectors = 3 fermion generations" interpretation is the most physically meaningful; if generational structure emerges from the cascade (plausible — see cascade-generations.md), then 87 directly counts phase-slots across all three generations per Coxeter cycle.

**This suggests a new programme direction: derive the three-generation structure from E₈ Coxeter arithmetic.** Not in the current theorem tree but natural next target after C1–C3.

## 6. The pivot exponent 13

13 is the largest lower exponent (<h/2) and 17 is the smallest upper exponent. Together they form the Plane-4 conjugate pair {13, 17}, which has the **largest per-step rotation angle 26π/30 = 13π/15** — the "most aggressive" Coxeter rotation.

### 13 as the "pivot" number

- 13 is the largest integer in {1, 7, 11, 13} that is still below h/2 = 15.
- 13 is a prime (together with 7 and 11, but not 1).
- 13 is the 7-fold power image: 7² = 49 ≡ 19, 7³ ≡ 13 (mod 30). So 13 = 7³ in the Z/4 factor of (Z/30)*.
- 13 is the order-30-generator that gives the largest phason winding per Coxeter cycle among lower exponents (same magnitude as its upper partner 17, just oriented opposite).

### Connection to 13-protofilament microtubules

**Conjectural, structural match.** If the microtubule's 13-fold C_13 symmetry is the biological instantiation of the 13D translation layer, and the 13D translation is generated by the pivot exponent 13, then:

- The 13 protofilaments correspond to the 13 integer steps of the pivot rotation angle 13π/15 before it wraps (since 13 × 13π/15 = 169π/15 = 169π/15; 169 = 11×15 + 4, so 169π/15 = 11π + 4π/15, which modulo 2π depends on parity of 11 — 11π = π + 10π = π mod 2π, so = π + 4π/15 = (15 + 4)π/15 = 19π/15, which is not zero, so 13 steps don't close).

Actually let me reconsider. The rotation by 13π/15 per Coxeter step, after 13 steps, gives 13 × 13π/15 = 169π/15. And 169 = 15 × 11 + 4, so 169π/15 = 11π + 4π/15 ≡ π + 4π/15 mod 2π (since 11π = 11π − 5×2π = π mod 2π). So after 13 applications, the phason coordinate has rotated by π + 4π/15 ≈ π + 48°.

Hmm, that's not 13 protofilaments cleanly. The match I wanted would require 13 applications of some rotation to produce a full 2π cycle (= 1 complete protofilament traversal). The rotations that do this are the ones by 2π/13, which isn't one of our Coxeter angles.

**So the 13-protofilament / exponent-13 match is less tight than I initially thought.** The 13 appearing in both places might be a coincidence — or might reflect a deeper structural fact about "the 13-protofilament chamber being the biological implementation of the pivot exponent." The latter requires more careful work. For now, flagged as **conjectural and weaker than the first derivation suggested.**

## 0. Executive summary

**H-FIC-O.** For any descending chain (S_n) of rung-structures with pointwise intersection S_∞,
$$
    \mathcal{G}_O^{(S_\infty)} \;=\; \bigcap_n \mathcal{G}_O^{(S_n)}  \quad\text{in}\quad \mathcal{G}_O.
$$

**Route taken (replaces the old graded-groupoid route).**
1. Under the bridge $Q_O \cong \text{Meas}(S^7, \sigma)$ (`cascade-q-o-measurement-bridge.md` Thm 3.1, now theorem-grade after G6.4-a), each $g \in Q_S$ has a **canonical support region** $\mathcal{O}(g) \subseteq S^7$ — the set of observer-directions $\hat{o}$ at which the σ-projection $\mu(g)(\hat{o})$ is non-trivial. This is not new structure; it is the $S^7$-indexing already used to define $\mathcal{G}_O^{(g)}$ in capstone Def `def:min-O`.
2. The subgroupoid $\mathcal{G}_O^{(g)}$ is by construction generated by morphisms $\gamma \in \mathcal{G}_O$ whose support-in-$S^7$ (via the bridge) lies inside $\mathcal{O}(g)$. Equivalently, $\mathcal{G}_O^{(g)} = \pi_{S^7}^{-1}(\mathcal{O}(g))$ where $\pi_{S^7}: \mathcal{G}_O \twoheadrightarrow \mathcal{O}(\mathcal{G}_O) \subseteq 2^{S^7}$ is the support-projection natural to the bridge.
3. **Join-on-S⁷ compatibility.** $\mathcal{G}_O^{(S)} := \bigvee_g \mathcal{G}_O^{(g)}$ corresponds under $\pi_{S^7}$ to the union $\bigcup_g \mathcal{O}(g) =: \mathcal{O}(Q_S) \subseteq S^7$. Explicitly:
$$
    \mathcal{G}_O^{(S)} \;=\; \pi_{S^7}^{-1}\big( \mathcal{O}(Q_S) \big).
$$
4. **Intersection-on-S⁷.** The intersection $\bigcap_n \mathcal{G}_O^{(S_n)} = \pi_{S^7}^{-1}\big( \bigcap_n \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_n}) \big)$ by the preimage-preserves-intersection axiom for any set-valued map.
5. **What remains:** show $\mathcal{O}(Q_{S_\infty}) = \bigcap_n \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_n})$. This is **a question about support regions of subalgebras of Meas(S⁷, σ) under descending intersection**, not a groupoid-theoretic question, not a graded-groupoid question, and crucially not dependent on H-lift-fin.

**Why this route avoids H-lift-fin.** The old sketch's step (ii) said "lift the Fano grading to $\mathcal{G}_O$ using morphism-to-measurement-event correspondence, which itself requires H-lift-fin." The morphism-to-measurement-event correspondence is what $\pi_{S^7}$ supplies — but $\pi_{S^7}$ is forced by the **definition** of $\mathcal{G}_O^{(g)}$ in capstone Def `def:min-O` (the lift of $g$ to $\mathcal{G}_O$ is by construction support-region-indexed). We do not need the finite-cover morphism bridge of H-lift-fin; we need only that the support-region assignment exists, which is the content of Def `def:min-O` itself.

**Why we also avoid the graded-groupoid hypothesis.** The old sketch's step (iii) said "subgroupoid joins respect the graded structure." That claim is false in general (as codex flagged). But **we do not need it**: the support-region argument replaces direct-sum decomposition by set-theoretic union on $S^7$, for which preimage-commutes-with-union is automatic, and the only non-trivial step is the descending-intersection commutation on $2^{S^7}$ — a question about the **collection** $\{\mathcal{O}(Q_S)\}_{S \in \catC}$ of support regions, not about grading.

**Residual sub-gap (H-FIC-O).** One named combinatorial sub-gap:

- **G-FIC-a (support-region descending-intersection).** For any descending chain of rung-structures $(S_n)$ with pointwise-intersection limit $S_\infty$,
$$
    \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_\infty}) \;=\; \bigcap_n \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_n}) \quad\text{in}\quad 2^{S^7}.
$$

This is a statement about how the rung-structure-to-support-region map behaves under filtered intersection in $\catC$. It is strictly weaker than the original H-FIC because:
- It concerns only subsets of $S^7$, not subgroupoids of $\mathcal{G}_O$.
- The $\subseteq$ direction is monotonicity (trivial).
- The $\supseteq$ direction requires that if $\hat{o} \in \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_n})$ for every $n$, then $\hat{o} \in \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_\infty})$ — i.e. that a witness-measurement at $\hat{o}$ available at every $S_n$ is available at the pointwise limit. This follows from: (a) each $Q_{S_n}$ is a sheaf-section subalgebra of $\Gamma(G, F)$, so $\bigcap_n Q_{S_n}$ is closed under the Pontryagin-character decomposition; (b) support in Meas(S⁷, σ) is compatible with characters (via the H-grad-Fano lift of `cascade-fano-grading-lift.md`); (c) a descending family of support regions with common accessor-point $\hat{o}$ on every $S_n$ localises to a common accessor-point on $S_\infty$ **provided the support-region assignment $S \mapsto \mathcal{O}(Q_S)$ is upper-semicontinuous** with respect to the intersection-filter on $\catC$.

G-FIC-a is tractable via:
- **Rung-by-rung decomposition.** $\mathcal{O}(Q_S) = \bigcup_{r \in \underline{S}} \mathcal{O}(Q_r)$. The union commutes with intersection iff the family $\{\mathcal{O}(Q_r)\}_{r \in \text{Rungs}}$ is finite (7 rungs — Observer, H_4, D_4, F_16, L_40, U_0, E_8 — all finite) and each $\mathcal{O}(Q_r)$ is closed in $S^7$. The former is immediate; the latter reduces to the closed-support property of sheaf-section subalgebras arising from closed subgroupoids $\mathcal{G}_r \subseteq \mathcal{G}$ (Gap G6.1 prerequisite).
- **Finite-intersection reduction.** Because the rung-index is finite (7 rungs), any descending chain of rung-structures stabilises after at most 7 steps on its rung-index component. The residual descending-chain structure is on the closure-data component $\Phi$, which assigns subgroupoids $\mathcal{G}_r^\Phi \subseteq \mathcal{G}_r$. Intersection commutation on a **finite** index-set composed with per-rung closed-subgroupoid intersection gives G-FIC-a at strength of the Gap G6.1 + closedness infrastructure.

**H-FIC-E_8.** Analogous derivation via the FAN-restriction construction (capstone Def `def:rung-structure`, clause (c) for $r = E_8$), with one analogous sub-gap:

- **G-FIC-b (FAN-restriction intersection commutation).** The FAN-restriction preimages on $E_8$ commute with descending intersection of rung-structures. This reduces to: the FAN-restriction maps $\pi_{r' \to E_8} : \mathcal{G}_{r'} \to \mathcal{G}_{E_8}$ have closed image, and the preimages under $\pi_{r'}^{-1}$ commute with finite union.

**Downgrade status.**
- **H-FIC-O** is downgraded from "hypothesis" to "derived from bridge + Def `def:min-O` + G-FIC-a." The principal content is proved; G-FIC-a is a smaller, sharper combinatorial sub-gap.
- **H-FIC-E_8** is downgraded from "hypothesis" to "derived from FAN-restriction + G-FIC-b," an analogous reduction.
- The capstone's Theorem `thm:F-limits` ($F$ preserves $\omega^{op}$-limits) now depends on {G-FIC-a, G-FIC-b} rather than on H-FIC itself. G-FIC-a and G-FIC-b are substantially smaller claims.
- **H-lift-fin is no longer required for the H-FIC derivation.** It remains required separately for Lemma `lem:S7-coverage` on S⁷-coverage at the terminal, which is a different use.

## 1. Setup

### 1.1 Standing data

From `cascade-capstone-coalgebra.tex` §4.2 (Def `def:rung-structure`, Def `def:min-O`):

- $\catC$ = category of rung-structures with morphisms = inclusions + closure-data-compatibility.
- For $S \in \catC$, $Q_S \subseteq \Gamma(G, F)$ is the query algebra.
- $\mathcal{G}_O^{(g)} \subseteq \mathcal{G}_O$ = the minimum subgroupoid generated by morphisms supporting the σ-projection region $\mathcal{O}(g) \subseteq S^7$.
- $\mathcal{G}_O^{(S)} := \bigvee_{g \in Q_S} \mathcal{G}_O^{(g)}$ (join over all $g \in Q_S$).
- A descending chain in $\catC$ is a sequence of monomorphisms $S_0 \hookleftarrow S_1 \hookleftarrow \cdots$ with pointwise-intersection limit $S_\infty$ on the rung-index and closure-data.

### 1.2 The bridge $Q_O \cong \text{Meas}(S^7, \sigma)$

From `cascade-q-o-measurement-bridge.md` Thm 3.1 (theorem-grade after G6.4-a closure via `cascade-g64a-closure.md`):

- $\mu : Q_O \to \text{Meas}(S^7, \sigma)$ is a canonical R-algebra-with-involution isomorphism.
- For any $g \in Q_O$, $\mu(g)$ is a function $S^7 \to \mathcal{B}(H)$ assigning to each observer-direction $\hat{o}$ the σ-projection operator $\Pi_{\hat{o}} \circ s_g$.
- The **support region** of $g$ is $\mathcal{O}(g) := \{\hat{o} \in S^7 : \mu(g)(\hat{o}) \ne 0\}$, a well-defined subset of $S^7$.

**Extension to $Q_S$ for $S$ containing the Observer rung.** If $O \in \underline{S}$, the support-region map extends to $Q_S$ by $\mathcal{O}(g) := $ support of the $Q_O$-projection of $g$ via the σ-projection system; in the general case where $O \notin \underline{S}$, the σ-projection of $g$ onto $Q_O$ is trivial by the Access Principle (`cascade-access-principle-theorem.md`) and $\mathcal{O}(g) = \emptyset$, so the subsequent analysis is vacuous on the Observer side — exactly matching the capstone's Def `def:F-objects` (which adjoins $O$ if it is not already in $\underline{S}$). We therefore analyse the case $O \in \underline{S_n}$ for all $n$ (since $F$ is applied to rung-structures where $O$ is either already present or adjoined before descent).

### 1.3 Support-projection $\pi_{S^7}$ on $\mathcal{G}_O$

> **Definition 1.3 (support-projection).** The support-projection
> $$
>     \pi_{S^7} : \mathcal{G}_O \longrightarrow 2^{S^7}, \quad \gamma \mapsto \text{supp}(\gamma)
> $$
> sends a morphism $\gamma \in \mathcal{G}_O$ to the set of observer-directions $\hat{o} \in S^7$ at which $\gamma$ acts non-trivially on Observer-rung data. Formally, supp$(\gamma) = \{\hat{o} \in S^7 : \gamma \cdot T_{\hat{o}} \ne T_{\hat{o}}\}$ where $T_{\hat{o}}$ is the Observer-rung-fibre tiling data at $\hat{o}$.

By construction of $\mathcal{G}_O^{(g)}$ in Def `def:min-O`, a morphism $\gamma$ lies in $\mathcal{G}_O^{(g)}$ iff supp$(\gamma) \subseteq \mathcal{O}(g)$. Hence:

> **Lemma 1.4 (support-region characterisation of $\mathcal{G}_O^{(S)}$).**
> $$
>     \mathcal{G}_O^{(S)} \;=\; \{\gamma \in \mathcal{G}_O : \text{supp}(\gamma) \subseteq \mathcal{O}(Q_S)\} \;=\; \pi_{S^7}^{-1}\big( \text{Pow}(\mathcal{O}(Q_S)) \big)
> $$
> where $\mathcal{O}(Q_S) := \bigcup_{g \in Q_S} \mathcal{O}(g)$ and $\text{Pow}$ denotes the power-set image.

*Proof.* $\mathcal{G}_O^{(S)} = \bigvee_g \mathcal{G}_O^{(g)}$ = subgroupoid generated by morphisms with supp$(\gamma) \subseteq \mathcal{O}(g)$ for some $g$. The union of such support-conditions over $g$ is supp$(\gamma) \subseteq \bigcup_g \mathcal{O}(g) = \mathcal{O}(Q_S)$. The groupoid closure-operation is compatible with support-containment because composition of morphisms has support-in-union, and inversion preserves support.

(Technical: the "generated subgroupoid" includes compositions $\gamma_1 \circ \gamma_2$ with supp$(\gamma_1 \circ \gamma_2) \subseteq $ supp$(\gamma_1) \cup $ supp$(\gamma_2)$, both contained in $\mathcal{O}(Q_S)$; hence the closure stays inside $\pi_{S^7}^{-1}(\text{Pow}(\mathcal{O}(Q_S)))$.) $\square$

## 2. Derivation of H-FIC-O, modulo G-FIC-a

### 2.1 The key computation

Using Lemma 1.4:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\bigcap_n \mathcal{G}_O^{(S_n)}
&= \bigcap_n \{\gamma \in \mathcal{G}_O : \text{supp}(\gamma) \subseteq \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_n})\} \\
&= \{\gamma \in \mathcal{G}_O : \text{supp}(\gamma) \subseteq \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_n}) \text{ for every } n\} \\
&= \{\gamma \in \mathcal{G}_O : \text{supp}(\gamma) \subseteq \bigcap_n \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_n})\}.
\end{aligned}
$$

Similarly:

$$
\mathcal{G}_O^{(S_\infty)} = \{\gamma \in \mathcal{G}_O : \text{supp}(\gamma) \subseteq \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_\infty})\}.
$$

These are equal iff $\mathcal{O}(Q_{S_\infty}) = \bigcap_n \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_n})$ — **G-FIC-a**.

### 2.2 The $\subseteq$ direction of G-FIC-a is automatic

By Prop `prop:Q-monotone` in the capstone, $S_\infty \hookrightarrow S_n$ gives $Q_{S_\infty} \subseteq Q_{S_n}$ for all $n$, hence $\mathcal{O}(Q_{S_\infty}) \subseteq \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_n})$ for all $n$, hence $\mathcal{O}(Q_{S_\infty}) \subseteq \bigcap_n \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_n})$.

### 2.3 The $\supseteq$ direction of G-FIC-a — reduction to finite-rung-index compact-support

Claim: if $\hat{o} \in \bigcap_n \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_n})$, then $\hat{o} \in \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_\infty})$.

Suppose $\hat{o} \in \mathcal{O}(Q_{S_n})$ for every $n$. Then for each $n$, there exists $g_n \in Q_{S_n}$ with $\hat{o} \in \mathcal{O}(g_n)$. The question is whether we can produce **a single** $g_\infty \in Q_{S_\infty}$ with $\hat{o} \in \mathcal{O}(g_\infty)$.

**Rung-decomposition argument.** Since $Q_{S_n} = \bigvee_{r \in \underline{S_n}} Q_r^{\Phi_n}$ (algebraically generated from rung-components, where $\Phi_n$ is the closure-data of $S_n$), and $\underline{S_\infty} = \bigcap_n \underline{S_n}$ is the rung-index limit, and $\text{Rungs}$ is a **finite** set (7 elements):

- The descending chain $\underline{S_0} \supseteq \underline{S_1} \supseteq \cdots$ on a 7-element set stabilises at some step $N$: $\underline{S_n} = \underline{S_\infty}$ for $n \geq N$.
- For $n \geq N$, $Q_{S_n}$ differs from $Q_{S_\infty}$ only in the closure-data component $\Phi_n$, which specifies $\mathcal{G}_r^{\Phi_n} \subseteq \mathcal{G}_r$ for each $r \in \underline{S_\infty}$.
- The descending chain of closure-data $\Phi_n \supseteq \Phi_{n+1} \supseteq \cdots$ with pointwise-intersection limit $\Phi_\infty$ induces a descending chain of $Q_r^{\Phi_n} \supseteq Q_r^{\Phi_{n+1}}$ at each rung $r$.

So the question reduces to: at each single rung $r$, does
$$
    \mathcal{O}(Q_r^{\Phi_\infty}) \;=\; \bigcap_n \mathcal{O}(Q_r^{\Phi_n}) ?
$$

This is a **single-rung** version of G-FIC-a. For $r = O$, it is the direct analogue under the bridge: descending subalgebras of Meas(S⁷, σ) with pointwise-intersection limit. Under the bridge:
$$
    \mathcal{O}(Q_r^{\Phi_n}) = \text{supp}(\mu(Q_r^{\Phi_n})) \subseteq S^7
$$
and since $Q_r^{\Phi_n}$ is a closed sheaf-section subalgebra (support over a closed subgroupoid $\mathcal{G}_r^{\Phi_n}$ by Gap G6.1 prerequisite), $\mu(Q_r^{\Phi_n})$ is a closed subalgebra of Meas(S⁷, σ) with closed support.

**Closed-support descending-intersection lemma.** For a descending chain of closed subsets $F_n \subseteq S^7$ on a compact Hausdorff space (which $S^7$ is), $\bigcap_n F_n$ is closed and non-empty iff each $F_n$ is non-empty (Cantor's intersection theorem). Moreover, the rung-$r$ support-region map respects this: $\mathcal{O}(Q_r^{\Phi_n})$ is the closed set of observer-directions at which rung-$r$-$\Phi_n$-equivariant sections have non-trivial σ-projection, and descending $\Phi_n$ gives descending closed support-regions on $S^7$.

**Remaining gap.** The one piece not yet fully proved is that the limit of the closed chain $\mathcal{O}(Q_r^{\Phi_n})$ is **exactly** $\mathcal{O}(Q_r^{\Phi_\infty})$, not merely $\supseteq$. The $\supseteq$ direction requires that for any $\hat{o}$ in the intersection, there exists an actual section $g_\infty \in Q_r^{\Phi_\infty}$ witnessing accessibility at $\hat{o}$. This is a **local finite-generation / Čech-reconstruction** statement: a locally-present sheaf-section datum that is compatible with every $\Phi_n$ is compatible with $\Phi_\infty$. Under Gap G6.1 + closedness + the pattern-equivariant Čech sheaf structure of `cascade-foundations.md` §3, this is standard Čech-sheaf limit-preservation.

### 2.4 Precise statement of G-FIC-a

> **Sub-gap G-FIC-a (precise form).** For each rung $r \in \text{Rungs}$ and each descending chain of closure-data $\Phi_0 \supseteq \Phi_1 \supseteq \cdots$ with pointwise-intersection $\Phi_\infty$, the support-region map satisfies
> $$
>     \mathcal{O}(Q_r^{\Phi_\infty}) \;=\; \bigcap_n \mathcal{O}(Q_r^{\Phi_n}) \quad\text{as subsets of } S^7.
> $$

G-FIC-a is strictly weaker than H-FIC-O: it replaces a subgroupoid-intersection identity in $\mathcal{G}_O$ with a support-region-intersection identity in $2^{S^7}$. The former carries non-commutative groupoid-algebra content; the latter is purely set-theoretic plus Čech-sheaf limit-preservation.

**Tractability.** G-FIC-a is closable via:
1. Closed-subgroupoid support-image lemma (Gap G6.1 prerequisite + topological closedness in the matching groupoid): straightforward from `cascade-foundations.md` §2.
2. Čech-sheaf limit-preservation under descending closures: Kellendonk–Lenz–Savinien 2006, Thm 3.4.
3. Compact-Hausdorff descending intersection (Cantor): trivial for $S^7$.

Estimated time: 1 week of focused work.

## 3. Derivation of H-FIC-E_8, modulo G-FIC-b

### 3.1 The FAN-restriction construction

From capstone Def `def:rung-structure` clause (c): for $S \in \catC$,
$$
\mathcal{G}_{E_8}^{F(S)} \;:=\; \bigg\langle \bigcup_{r' \in \underline{F(S)}, r' \ne E_8} \pi_{r' \to E_8}^{-1}(\mathcal{G}_{r'}^{F(S)}) \bigg\rangle \;\subseteq\; \mathcal{G}_{E_8}
$$
where $\pi_{r' \to E_8} : \mathcal{G}_{E_8} \to \mathcal{G}_{r'}$ is the FAN-restriction map induced by the E_8 → r' cascade restriction (via the FAN atlas of `cascade-atlas.md`), and $\langle \cdot \rangle$ denotes generated subgroupoid.

### 3.2 Preimage commutation with intersection

For any set-valued map $\pi : X \to 2^Y$, $\pi^{-1}$ commutes with intersection. So:
$$
\bigcap_n \pi_{r' \to E_8}^{-1}(\mathcal{G}_{r'}^{F(S_n)}) \;=\; \pi_{r' \to E_8}^{-1}\bigg( \bigcap_n \mathcal{G}_{r'}^{F(S_n)} \bigg).
$$

Under H-FIC-O (for $r' = O$) and the analogous rung-$r'$ statement for $r' \in \{H_4, D_4, F_{16}, L_{40}, U_0\}$, each inner intersection $\bigcap_n \mathcal{G}_{r'}^{F(S_n)}$ equals $\mathcal{G}_{r'}^{F(S_\infty)}$.

### 3.3 Outer "generated-subgroupoid" step

The remaining step is to show that
$$
\bigg\langle \bigcap_n X_n \bigg\rangle \stackrel{?}{=} \bigcap_n \langle X_n \rangle
$$
for the FAN-preimage family $X_n := \bigcup_{r'} \pi_{r' \to E_8}^{-1}(\mathcal{G}_{r'}^{F(S_n)})$.

This is **not** automatic for general subgroupoid generation: generated subgroupoids are closed under composition, and composition of elements in $\bigcap_n X_n$ may not suffice to generate elements common to every $\langle X_n \rangle$.

**However** — and this is where the rung-finiteness again bites — each $X_n$ is a **finite union of preimages** (6 non-$E_8$ rungs, so 6 preimages), and the generated subgroupoid $\langle X_n \rangle$ has a structure: morphisms in $\langle X_n \rangle$ are compositions of finite words in $X_n \cup X_n^{-1}$. A morphism lies in $\langle X_n \rangle$ for every $n$ iff it admits a finite-word factorisation using $X_n$-elements — and by descending-chain stability on a finite index-set, past some stage $N$ the factorisations use only elements of $\bigcap_n X_n$.

### 3.4 Precise statement of G-FIC-b

> **Sub-gap G-FIC-b (FAN-restriction intersection commutation).** For the FAN-preimage family $X_n := \bigcup_{r' \ne E_8} \pi_{r' \to E_8}^{-1}(\mathcal{G}_{r'}^{F(S_n)})$, the generated-subgroupoid operation commutes with descending intersection:
> $$
>     \bigg\langle \bigcap_n X_n \bigg\rangle \;=\; \bigcap_n \langle X_n \rangle \quad\text{in}\quad \mathcal{G}_{E_8}.
> $$

G-FIC-b reduces to: (i) finite-word-factorisation stability for finite-union preimage families, (ii) preimage-commutes-with-intersection for the FAN-restriction maps (automatic). It is tractable via standard generated-subgroupoid theory (MacLane, Ch. XII) plus the 6-element rung-index finiteness.

**Tractability.** Estimated time: 1 week, conditional on a clean statement of the FAN-restriction map $\pi_{r' \to E_8}$ as a groupoid morphism (which is a standing prerequisite of the capstone).

## 4. What this note closes and does not close

### 4.1 Closes (reduces)

- **H-FIC-O → G-FIC-a** (single rung-by-rung closed-support descending-intersection identity on $S^7$).
- **H-FIC-E_8 → G-FIC-b** (FAN-preimage generated-subgroupoid commutation with descending intersection).
- Eliminates the need for a graded-groupoid theory on $\mathcal{G}_O$.
- Eliminates the use of H-lift-fin in the H-FIC derivation (H-lift-fin remains needed separately for `lem:S7-coverage`).

### 4.2 Does not close

- **G-FIC-a** itself (support-region descending-intersection). Reduced to standard Čech-sheaf + compact-Hausdorff ingredients; tractable, not proved here.
- **G-FIC-b** itself (FAN-preimage generated-subgroupoid commutation). Reduced to standard generated-subgroupoid theory + rung-finiteness; tractable, not proved here.
- **Gap G6.1** (well-definedness of $\mathcal{G}_r$ as a closed subgroupoid). Prerequisite — assumed throughout.

### 4.3 New sub-gap structure

| Old hypothesis | New sub-gap(s) | Strength |
|----------------|----------------|----------|
| H-FIC-O | G-FIC-a | Čech-sheaf limit + Cantor-intersection on $S^7$ |
| H-FIC-E_8 | G-FIC-b | Generated-subgroupoid theory + rung-finiteness |
| — (eliminated) | H-lift-fin (for H-FIC) | no longer invoked |
| — (eliminated) | graded-groupoid hypothesis | no longer invoked |

Both sub-gaps are **substantially smaller** than H-FIC: they concern specific technical lemmas (closed-support descending-intersection, generated-subgroupoid commutation) rather than the full subgroupoid-identity claim, and they are tractable within 1-2 weeks total by standard references.

## 5. Why this is honest (and where the remaining weaknesses are)

**Strengths of this derivation:**
1. The support-region argument is **forced by Def `def:min-O`** — it is not a new hypothesis, it is what Def `def:min-O` already says.
2. The preimage-commutes-with-intersection step (§2.1, §3.2) is a **set-theoretic axiom**, not a mathematical claim.
3. The rung-finiteness reduction (§2.3, §3.3) uses the **finite cardinality of Rungs** (7 elements) which is combinatorially unambiguous.
4. The $\subseteq$ direction of both G-FIC-a and G-FIC-b is **monotonicity**, automatic.

**Weaknesses / honest flagging:**
1. The reduction of G-FIC-a to "Čech-sheaf limit-preservation" **cites** Kellendonk–Lenz–Savinien but does not write out the proof. For the capstone to claim H-FIC-O as a theorem, this citation needs to be unpacked into the specific lemma statement needed. As-is, G-FIC-a is "at lemma-grade, provable in standard Čech-sheaf theory."
2. G-FIC-b's reduction to "generated-subgroupoid theory" similarly cites MacLane without unpacking. The finite-word-factorisation stability claim (§3.3) is intuitively clear but not formally written out here.
3. The support-projection $\pi_{S^7}$ (Def 1.3) is **defined** in this note; it is not explicitly constructed in `cascade-q-o-measurement-bridge.md`.

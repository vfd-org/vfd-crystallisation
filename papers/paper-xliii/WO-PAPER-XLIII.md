# WO-PAPER-XLIII — Quantifying the Biology Rung

## Programme Position
This paper is the programme's first deliberate extension beyond fundamental physics into structured biological morphology. Its purpose is not to overclaim a theory of life, but to test whether the cascade's integer / geometry constraints naturally organise known discrete biological architectures.

## Scope
- Formalise the proposed biology rung as a constrained spectral / combinatorial sector rather than an analogy.
- Derive the allowed triangulation numbers $T \in \{1,3,4,5,7,12,13\}$ as the first target set for capsid-like architectures.
- Test whether DNA pitch and phyllotactic angle emerge from the same integer-selection or Hopf-related structure already used elsewhere.
- Separate hard predictions from retrospective pattern-matching.
- Define the null hypothesis clearly: where random or broad biological variability should defeat the model.

## Inputs
- Prior papers: XXII (integer selection {9,12,14,15} and $Z_3$ Hopf generational structure as precedent for discrete selection); XXXV (cascade catalogue); possibly XXVIII / XXXIII only for shared structural language, not as heavy dependencies.
- Cascade foundations: whatever F1–F8 material identifies the biology rung's position in the `16 → 8` or adjacent descent and fixes admissible integer classes.
- External mathematics: Caspar–Klug T-number geometry, helicoidal geometry of DNA, phyllotaxis / Fibonacci spiral mathematics, discrete symmetry selection on polyhedral / helical structures.

## Outputs
- Observable predictions: admissible T-number set $\{1,3,4,5,7,12,13\}$; DNA helical pitch or bp/turn target compared to canonical B-DNA ($\sim 10.5$ bp/turn) with explicit tolerance; phyllotactic divergence target compared to the golden-angle value ($\sim 137.50776^\circ$) if claimed.
- Structural theorems: admissible-biology-integer theorem; helical-selection theorem if DNA / phyllotaxis are genuinely derivable.
- Additions to VFD Explorer library: `biology_rung_tnumbers.py`, `dna_pitch_selector.py`, `phyllotaxis_angle_map.py`.

## Key derivations needed
1. Define the biological rung mathematically and show how it inherits or modifies the cascade's integer-selection rules.
2. Derive the allowed T-number set from VFD constraints rather than simply listing biologically common values.
3. Construct the helical geometry calculation linking rung integers to DNA pitch or bp/turn.
4. If phyllotaxis is claimed, derive the divergence angle from the same selection principle and identify whether the golden angle is exact or asymptotic.
5. Compare predicted allowed / disallowed structures against capsid catalogues, DNA forms, and botanical datasets.
6. Quantify how much of the apparent success exceeds simple combinatorial ubiquity.

## Open questions / risks
- Biological variability may swamp the rigidity needed for a strong derivation, leaving only suggestive numerology.
- The T-number set may be too easy to obtain from generic combinatorics, weakening uniqueness.
- DNA pitch depends strongly on chemistry and environment; a purely geometric derivation may be too crude.
- This paper is reputationally risky if it overstates physics-to-biology transfer.

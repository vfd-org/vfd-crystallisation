## Definitions

- **D1.** Cycle space (label `def:cyclespace`, §2). The 12-dimensional
  rational vector space C_Q ≅ Q^{12} with basis indexed by the 12
  T_τ-cycles in the deterministic cycle ordering of
  `vfd_v600.group.build_state()`.
  *Status:* explicit definition.

- **D2.** K-class projectors (label `def:Kproj`, §2). For each
  K ∈ {72, 0, 52, 20}, P_K ∈ End_Q(C_Q) is the diagonal operator
  P_K(e_i) = e_i if cycle i has K-class K, else 0.
  *Status:* explicit definition; ranks verified.

- **D3.** Operators Ĥ and Ĉ (label `def:ops`, §3).
  Ĥ := I_C + P_72; Ĉ := I_C − P_0.
  *Status:* explicit definition.

- **D4.** K-saturated diagonal projector algebra A_K (introduced in
  Theorem 4.2 statement, §4).
  A_K := the Q-subalgebra of End_Q(C_Q) generated by
  {I_C, P_72, P_0, P_52, P_20}; equivalently, the Q-span of the four
  mutually-orthogonal K-projectors P_72, P_0, P_52, P_20 (whose sum
  is I_C, so I_C is in A_K but is not orthogonal to the P_K — it is
  their sum). Every Q ∈ A_K has the unique decomposition
  Q = a_72 P_72 + a_0 P_0 + a_52 P_52 + a_20 P_20 with a_K ∈ Q.
  *Status:* explicit definition (load-bearing for T2 / R1).

## Lemmas

- **L1.** Phase-independence (label `lem:phaseind`, §4).
  For any τ_σ in the Z_2^5 orbit of canonical lifts of Paper 3, the
  induced cycle action on C_Q exchanges (K=52, K=20) pairs within
  each non-bulk right coset and fixes K=72, K=0 cycles. Hence P_72
  and P_0 commute with the τ_σ cycle action and the trace identities
  are phase-independent.
  *Status:* Proved in §4.1 from Paper 3's (P3) cross-K coset swap.
  Verified by `verify.py` on the canonical phase-(0,0,0,0,0) lift
  AND by exhaustive enumeration of all 2^5 = 32 phase combinations
  (each yields a cycle-level permutation that pointwise fixes both
  Ĥ and Ĉ).

## Theorems

- **T1.** Exact trace identities (label `thm:trace`, §4).
  tr(Ĥ) = 13, tr(Ĉ) = 11; ratios 13/12 and 11/12.
  *Status:* Proved in §4 (one-line trace algebra). Verified by
  `verify.py` and by `test_operator_traces.py`.

- **T2.** K-saturated admissibility (label `thm:admissibility`, §4).
  Within the K-saturated diagonal projector algebra
  A_K = ⟨I_C, P_72, P_0, P_52, P_20⟩_Q, the only rank-one projector
  corrections to I_C of the form I_C ± Q (with Q ∈ A_K rank-one) are
  ±P_72 and ±P_0.
  *Status:* Proved in §4 by writing Q = a_72 P_72 + a_0 P_0 +
  a_52 P_52 + a_20 P_20, applying Q² = Q to force a_K ∈ {0,1}, and
  selecting the rank-1 combinations from the K-multiset. Hypotheses
  ("diagonal in cycle basis" and "K-saturated") are essential — see
  R1.

## Remarks (computational, not theorems)

- **R1.** Hypotheses are essential (label `rem:hyp`, §4).
  Dropping either "diagonal in cycle basis" or "K-saturated"
  introduces a large family of rank-one projectors outside A_K. As
  a concrete example: the rank-one τ_σ-invariant projector onto
  span_Q(e_52 + e_20) for any τ_σ-paired pair of K=52, K=20 basis
  vectors commutes with the τ_σ cycle action but is not diagonal in
  the cycle basis and is not in A_K. The narrow Theorem 4.2 is the
  strongest form claimed.
  *Status:* explicit hypothesis-tracking remark.

- **R2.** Baseline rule (label `rem:baseline`, §2).
  The ΛCDM-baseline operator is DEFINED as I_C: the K-blind uniform
  cycle weighting assigning unit weight to every T_τ cycle. We do
  NOT claim I_C is the unique τ_σ-invariant rank-12 operator on C_Q;
  the baseline is a definitional choice, not a uniqueness theorem.
  *Status:* baseline-defining remark.

## Numerical results

- **N0.** K-class projector ranks and resolution of identity
  (computational fact, §2 prelude to Definition 2.2).
  rk(P_72) = rk(P_0) = 1, rk(P_52) = rk(P_20) = 5;
  P_72 + P_0 + P_52 + P_20 = I_C.
  *Status:* Verified — `verify.py` asserts ranks via trace and
  asserts the resolution of identity directly.

- **N1.** Trace ratios as exact rationals.
  *Status:* Verified — `verify.py` asserts tr_ratio(Ĥ) = 13/12 and
  tr_ratio(Ĉ) = 11/12 with `Fraction` arithmetic.

- **N2.** Phase-independence on canonical lift.
  *Status:* Verified — `verify.py` builds the cycle-level permutation
  from the canonical τ_σ in `data/tau_sigma_canonical.txt`,
  asserts bulk fixity (K=72 and K=0 cycles fixed setwise) and
  non-bulk pair exchange (K=52 ↔ K=20 within each non-bulk right
  coset), and asserts pointwise invariance H_perm == H,
  C_perm == C (stronger than trace invariance).

- **N2b.** Phase-independence on all 32 lifts (enumeration).
  *Status:* Verified — `verify.py` enumerates the 2^5 = 32 canonical
  phase combinations of Paper 3 and asserts that EVERY one yields a
  cycle-level permutation that pointwise fixes both Ĥ and Ĉ. This
  is the computational support for Lemma 4.1.

- **N3.** Rank-5 ablation traces.
  tr(I_C + P_52) = 17 (ratio 17/12 ≈ 1.417); tr(I_C − P_20) = 7
  (ratio 7/12 ≈ 0.583). The trace arithmetic is verified directly;
  the qualitative claim that these ratios fall outside the cited
  H_0 and S_8 tension bands follows by inspection (1.417 and 0.583
  are far from 1.085 and 0.92 respectively) but is NOT separately
  asserted by `verify.py`. NOTE: trace data alone do NOT distinguish
  P_72 from P_0 (both have trace 1); the assignment of each rank-1
  K-class to a specific observable is the content of Hypothesis H1.
  *Status:* Trace arithmetic verified — `verify.py`. Comparison
  to bands is observational.

- **N4.** Layer-2 numerical observation (NOT a theorem).
  H_0 row: 67.36 km/s/Mpc × 13/12 = 72.97 vs SH0ES 73.04±1.04 →
  ≈0.06σ residual.
  S_8 row: 0.832 × 11/12 = 0.763 vs KiDS-1000 multi-probe
  S_8 = 0.766(+0.020/-0.014). Predicted value is BELOW central, so
  the directional residual uses the lower KiDS error 0.014:
  (0.766 − 0.763)/0.014 ≈ 0.24σ. Combined Planck+KiDS-lower in
  quadrature (sqrt(0.013² + 0.014²) ≈ 0.019) gives ≈ 0.18σ.
  S_8 ≡ σ_8 √(Ω_m/0.3) is used because published weak-lensing
  tension intervals are reported as S_8, not bare σ_8.
  *Status:* informational only. `verify.py` reports values and the
  directional residual but does NOT assert any cosmological claim.
  Bibliographic sources pinned in references.bib (Planck2020,
  Riess2022SH0ES, Heymans2021KiDS, Asgari2021KiDSCS); the specific
  numerical values used (67.36, 73.04±1.04, 0.832±0.013, 0.766
  +0.020/-0.014) are taken from those papers' headline tables.

## Hypotheses (Layer 3, NOT claimed proved)

- **H1.** Cycle-mode coupling, S_8 form (label `hyp:coupling`, §6).
  Postulates a coupling map Γ : {scale-rate, clustering-amplitude}
  → A_K with Γ(scale-rate) = +P_72 and Γ(clustering) = −P_0, and
  the late/early-Universe ratio relations:
    H_0_late = (tr(I + Γ_scale)/tr(I)) · H_0_early,
    S_8_late = (tr(I + Γ_clust)/tr(I)) · S_8_early.
  The hypothesis is stated for S_8 (NOT bare σ_8) because published
  weak-lensing intervals are reported as S_8; translating to bare
  σ_8 would require an additional Ω_m-coupling assumption which is
  NOT made here. Falsifiable by future H_0 measurements drifting
  outside the 13/12-translated band or S_8 measurements outside the
  11/12-translated band.
  *Status:* explicit hypothesis. The paper does NOT claim H1 is a
  theorem.

# Paper III Foundation Notes

## Critical Finding

The metric-induced class-energy ratios are substantially smaller than both
phi^15 and the observed mp/me. The highest ratio achieved across 5 metric
families and 4 energy conventions is 376 (MF1/E_weighted), which is 80%
below the target 1836.

This reveals that:

1. **phi^15 was the combinatorial EXPONENT, not the energy ratio.** The
   Paper II result phi^15 = 1364 arose from assuming mass ~ phi^C, which
   is an exponential scaling. The actual metric-derived energy sums
   phi^{2n} terms, producing much smaller ratios.

2. **The gap between metric-derived energy and observed mass ratio is
   large** — a factor of 5 to 50x, not the 26% suggested by phi^15.

3. **The class-energy map is the critical missing bridge.** How the
   metric-derived shell energies combine to produce mass is the fundamental
   open question. It is not a "correction" to phi^15 — it is a
   structurally different computation.

## Analysis Answers

1. **Minimal coherent metric?** MF1 (pure radial phi). Minimal postulates,
   structurally justified from the shell nesting.

2. **Most structurally justified?** MF1 for pure geometry; MF4 for
   physics (coupling between adjacent shells is natural).

3. **Does metric differ from Paper II surrogates?** YES, dramatically.
   Paper II's phi^C is an exponential scaling; the metric gives additive
   shell-energy sums. The ratio is much smaller.

4. **Does geometry distinguish boundary/interior?** MF2 does, but the
   boundary correction makes the electron HEAVIER relative to MF1,
   reducing the ratio further (from 27 to 17 for E_sum).

5. **Does metric move ratio away from phi^15?** The metric does not
   produce phi^15 at all. phi^15 was never a metric-derived quantity.

6. **What causes the shift?** The fundamental issue: additive shell-energy
   sums (E = sum phi^{2n}) grow polynomially with shell index, while
   the combinatorial invariant C grows multiplicatively. Mass ~ phi^C
   would require an exponential relationship between energy and shell
   structure that is not present in any of the tested metrics.

7. **What is still missing?** The class-energy map that converts
   geometric shell energies into mass. This map must be nonlinear —
   specifically, it must involve an exponential or strongly nonlinear
   function of the shell structure to produce ratios of order 10^3
   from shell energies that only differ by factors of 10-100.

8. **Neutron/proton?** The metrics do not distinguish neutron from
   proton (same shell support). This confirms the Paper II finding.

9. **Strongest honest claim?** "Five candidate metric families are
   defined and evaluated. The metric-induced class-energy ratios are
   of order 10^1 to 10^2, substantially below the observed mass ratio
   of ~1836. Bridging this gap requires a class-energy-to-mass map
   that is strongly nonlinear in the metric-derived energy. This map
   is the primary open problem."

## Outcome: B

A structurally justified metric and class-energy map are defined, but
the map does not yet produce the observed mass hierarchy. The exact
missing mathematical object is identified: a nonlinear class-energy-to-mass
map, or equivalently, a justification for why mass should scale
exponentially with closure complexity rather than linearly with
metric-derived shell energy.

# Geometry-First Findings — V_600 Structural Data

**Purpose.** This document consolidates the verified mathematical structure of
the 600-cell vertex set V_600 (120 unit icosian quaternions) as a foundation
for downstream papers. The methodology is **geometry-first**: each claim is
backed by a simulation that runs, passes acceptance, and writes machine-readable
output. Nothing here depends on interpretation.

Two complementary association-scheme structures coexist on V_600:

1. A **9-class Euclidean-shell scheme** that is P-polynomial in the edge
   adjacency A_1, multiplicity-free, with the full Bose-Mesner / Krein
   axioms satisfied at the algebra level.
2. A **6-class graph-distance shell partition** with valencies (1, 12, 32,
   42, 32, 1) that is *not* distance-regular in the narrow BCN sense.

Both structures are described below.

---

## 1. V_600 — the substrate

- 120 vertices, unit icosian quaternions
- W(H_4) symmetry group, order 14400 = 120 × 120 (acts on V_600 as a doubly
  transitive permutation representation, multiplicity-free with 9 irreducible
  components verified in step 4)
- Edge adjacency A_1: edge length² = 1/φ² ≈ 0.382, valency 12-regular
- A_1 spectrum: 9 distinct eigenvalues with multiplicities
  `(1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 9, 16, 4)`, all perfect squares; sum = 120

All eigenvalues lie in Z[φ] = Z[1+√5)/2]. Eigenspaces of dim 4, 9, 16 come in
Galois-conjugate pairs under σ: φ ↦ 1-φ.

## 2. Euclidean-shell association scheme (9 classes)

### Shell structure

Pair each ordered (x, y) by squared Euclidean distance d²(x, y). Nine distinct
values arise:

| i | d² value      | k_i (valency) | f_i = mult of A_1-eigenspace |
|---|---------------|---------------|-------------------------------|
| 0 | 0             | 1             | 1                             |
| 1 | 1/φ² ≈ 0.382  | 12            | 4                             |
| 2 | 1             | 20            | 9                             |
| 3 | 1·φ⁻¹·2 ≈ 1.382 | 12          | 16                            |
| 4 | 2             | 30            | 25                            |
| 5 | (2-φ⁻¹)·1.5 ≈ 2.618 | 12      | 36                            |
| 6 | 3             | 20            | 9                             |
| 7 | 3.618...      | 12            | 16                            |
| 8 | 4 (antipodal) | 1             | 4                             |

Define A_i ∈ M_120(Z) as the 0/1-incidence operator on shell i.

### Verified axioms (`sim_association_scheme.py`, 15/15 PASS)

- **Symmetric Bose-Mesner.** {A_0, ..., A_8} is a commutative basis.
- **A_0 = I, Σ A_i = J** (all-ones matrix).
- **Closure under multiplication.** A_i A_j = Σ_k p^k_{ij} A_k with all
  p^k_{ij} non-negative integers.
- **P-matrix and Q-matrix.** P, Q are 9×9 with P·Q = Q·P = 120·I exactly.
- **Krein parameters.** q^k_{ij} are all non-negative real numbers.
- **Idempotents.** E_j = (1/120) Σ_i Q[i, j] A_i are primitive orthogonal
  idempotents.

### P-polynomial structure (`sim_p_polynomial_structure.py`)

For each i = 0, ..., 8, there exists a polynomial p_i(x) of degree i such that
`A_i = p_i(A_1)` exactly. So the 9-dim Bose-Mesner algebra is generated by
A_1 alone — V_600 is **P-polynomial in A_1**.

### But: NOT a strict distance-regular graph

The intersection array (BCN convention):
```
{1, 3, 5, 2, 5, 3, 5, 12;  12, 5, 3, 5, 2, 5, 3, 1}
```
However the **valency recursion `k_{i+1} = k_i · b_i / c_{i+1}` FAILS**, and
`a_i + b_i + c_i ≠ 12` for interior shells. This means A_1 · A_s has off-band
contributions outside {s-1, s, s+1} — the scheme is P-polynomial without being
a 3-term tridiagonal narrow DRG.

This is unusual: most P-polynomial schemes are narrow DRGs.

## 3. Graph-distance shell partition (6 classes)

BFS from any vertex on the 1-skeleton edge graph gives 6 distinct graph
distances:

| d_G | size | composed of Euclidean shells |
|-----|------|------------------------------|
| 0   | 1    | shell 0                      |
| 1   | 12   | shell 1                      |
| 2   | 32   | shells 2 + 3                 |
| 3   | 42   | shells 4 + 5                 |
| 4   | 32   | shells 6 + 7                 |
| 5   | 1    | shell 8                      |

### Why this is NOT distance-regular

Picking one representative shell-4 vertex gives intersection counts
(a_4, b_4, c_4) = (6, 0, 6). But the antipode (shell 5) has 12 neighbors in
shell 4 — those 12 specific shell-4 vertices have b_4 = 1; the other 20 have
b_4 = 0. **Intersection numbers depend on the vertex**, so the graph is not
distance-regular.

A second proof: W(H_4) has order 14400; there are 120 × 32 = 3840 ordered
pairs at graph distance 4. Stabilizer would have order 14400/3840 = 3.75,
which is not an integer. So W(H_4) does not act transitively on graph-4 pairs,
hence the graph is not distance-transitive, hence not DRG.

The L_1 tridiagonal eigenvalues `{+12, +8.57, +4.09, +0.83, 0, -2.48}` do not
lie in A_1's spectrum, confirming this empirically.

## 4. What this gives the downstream papers

### Substantive structural facts (no interpretation needed):

1. V_600 carries a **9-class symmetric association scheme** by Euclidean
   distance with **multiplicity-free** permutation representation; the
   Bose-Mesner algebra is generated by A_1.
2. **A_1's spectrum lies in Z[φ]** with multiplicities all perfect squares;
   eigenspaces of dim 4, 9, 16 come in **Galois σ-conjugate pairs**.
3. **The 1-skeleton is NOT distance-regular** — V_600's "nice combinatorics"
   sits in the Euclidean (polytope) metric, not the graph metric. This is a
   distinguishing feature: V_600 is *more* than a graph; the polytope
   geometry strictly refines the 1-skeleton.

### Where to take this next

These three facts are the foundation for two possible directions:

- **Paper A** ("V_600 as a substrate of irreducibles"): write up the
  Euclidean scheme as a multiplicity-free P-polynomial coherent algebra with
  Z[φ] spectrum and Galois pairing — purely structural, no claims about
  primes or zeta functions.
- **Paper B** (conditional, downstream): connect the 4-9-16 Galois pairing
  to Dedekind ζ_K factorisation over Q(φ). This would require new sims of
  the Z[φ]-valued P-matrix and a witnessed connection to χ_5.

## 6. What we deliberately did NOT do

To stay honest:

- No claim about "closure", "observation", "primes", or "zeta function"
  appears anywhere above. All such interpretation belongs in a downstream
  paper and must rest on the structural facts here.
- The phrase "600-cell graph" is potentially ambiguous in the literature
  (it can refer to the 1-skeleton with graph distance, the Cayley graph,
  or the Euclidean-distance scheme). We do not assume any of these
  identifications; we report empirical structure directly.
- We did not verify whether the Euclidean scheme is "imprimitive" or
  whether the 4-9-16 dimension matching across Galois-conjugate pairs has
  a number-theoretic interpretation. Both are open questions.
